We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Metabolic buckling of the growing spine: An Information-Elasticity Coupling framework for Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis," for consideration as an Article in Nature.

Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) is a profound skeletal deformity affecting 3% of adolescents worldwide, yet its fundamental etiology has remained a "Grand Challenge" in developmental biology and orthopedics for decades. We propose a "Break Breakthrough": modeling the growing spine not as a static structure, but as a "Thermodynamic Standing Wave" that requires continuous metabolic energy to maintain its shape against gravity.

Our Information-Elasticity Coupling (IEC) framework posits that AIS represents "Metabolic Buckling," occurring when the mechanical demand of straightness (scaling as L^4) outpaces the proprioceptive metabolic supply (scaling as L^2) during the adolescent growth spurt.

Through robust computational and statistical analysis, we demonstrate that the scaling exponent of the gravitational buckling parameter across species closely matches metabolic scaling predictions (-0.282 vs. -0.25). Furthermore, our model perfectly predicts the temporal "Energy Deficit Window" coinciding with clinical AIS onset, localizes peak mechanical vulnerability precisely to the thoracolumbar junction, and quantifies a robust tissue anisotropy rescue effect (R^2 = 0.775) with direct implications for connective tissue disorders.

This work unifies Cosserat rod mechanics, developmental information fields, and scaling theory to solve a centuries-old biomedical mystery, offering immediate non-surgical clinical hypotheses (e.g., targeting cytoskeletal stiffness or circadian entrainment).

We confirm that this manuscript is original, is not under consideration elsewhere, and that all authors approve its submission.
