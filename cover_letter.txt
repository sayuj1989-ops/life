We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Metabolic buckling of the growing spine," for consideration as an Article in Nature. Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) is a profound skeletal deformity affecting 3% of adolescents worldwide, yet its fundamental etiology has remained a "Grand Challenge" in developmental biology and orthopedics for decades. We propose a "Breakthrough": modeling the growing spine not as a static structure, but as a "Thermodynamic Standing Wave" that requires continuous metabolic energy to maintain its shape against gravity. We show that AIS is a predictable "Metabolic Buckling" event caused by a mismatch between mechanical demand and metabolic supply.

This work reveals a fundamental biological principle of how living organisms maintain structure against gravity, rather than a specialist orthopaedic phenomenon. We formalize this through an active control system governed by a Delay Differential Equation, identifying a critical Energy Deficit Window during the adolescent growth spurt where the strain energy cost of alignment exceeds surface-limited metabolic capacity. This thermodynamic scaling failure triggers a supercritical bifurcation into a helical deformity.
