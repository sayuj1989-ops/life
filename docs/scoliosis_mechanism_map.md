## The Control Loop: Key Components

* **KCNQ & CAV1**: Modulate the membrane potential and tension, acting as "gain controllers" for the mechanosensors (Sánchez-Carranza et al., 2024; Moreno-Vicente et al., 2018).

### 3. Energy & Metabolism (The Bottleneck)

## Pathways of Failure (Scoliotic Buckling)

When the control loop is disrupted, the spine cannot maintain counter-curvature, resulting in instability.

* **Piezo-Cilia Stress Lock**: Asymmetric compression destroys cilia, derepressing osteogenesis and driving wedging.
* **PIEZO1-GPX4 Ferroptosis Axis**: Mechanical overload overactivates PIEZO1, causing GPX4 inhibition and ferroptosis, driving growth plate dysplasia and asymmetric ossification (Chen et al., 2025).
* **PIEZO1-YAP Degeneration Axis**: Excessive strain shifts YAP signaling to trigger Matrix Metalloproteinase (MMP) release, degrading the disc's mechanical stiffness (Peng et al., 2025).
* **Cilia-TRPV4 Stress Cascade**: Mechanical unloading or compressive stress causes primary cilia shortening, which unleashes TRPV4 channel overactivation. This drives intracellular calcium overload and Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS) production, leading to a profound loss of material stiffness ($EI$) and precipitating Euler buckling (Miao et al., 2023).

> **Hypothesis**: Applying localized high-frequency vibration during adolescence could act as an artificial dither, preventing the initial scoliotic bifurcation.
* **Glymphatic Stasis & CSF Relays**: Loss of interstitial fluid flow and Reissner Fiber tension disrupts proprioceptive symmetry, altering muscle tone and unbalancing the spinal column.
