# From Molecules to Curvature: A Causal Map of Scoliosis

## Overview
The **Scoliosis Mechanism Map** links molecular actors to tissue mechanics via **Biological Counter-Curvature**. The spine actively grows into an anticipatory S-shape to neutralize gravity. Scoliosis represents the failure of this active control system.

**Counter-Curvature** unifies molecular pathways by framing them as parts of a single active control system resisting gravitational load. Instead of isolated defects, Counter-Curvature shows how genetics, forces, and metabolism form a mechanosensory loop. This loop dynamically adjusts structural stiffness ($EI$) and target curvature ($\kappa_0$) via mechanotransductive gain ($G_{mech}$) to maintain spinal stability. When components like PIEZO1, metabolism, or integrin mechanosensing fail, the loop collapses, causing Euler buckling and 3D deformity (scoliosis).

## Failure Modes

### Energy & Metabolism
* **Metabolic Bottleneck**: Energetic supply ($L^2$) lags behind structural scaling ($L^4$), compromising active postural networks (Krishnan S et al., 2025).
* **Spinal Jetlag**: Loss of `BMAL1` circadian entrainment disrupts load-synchronized anabolic repair in discs.
* **Epigenetic Asymmetry**: Unloading downregulates `METTL3` methylation, driving asymmetric muscle atrophy.

### Mechanosensing & Signaling
* **Osteocyte Dendrites**: Rapid growth outpaces supply; energy deficit starves the network, dropping gain ($G_{mech}$).
* **Integrin Gravity Sensing**: `ITGB1` $\rightarrow$ `Focal Adhesion` $\rightarrow$ `Mechanotransduction`. Integrins detect gravity via the extracellular matrix. Deletion in cartilage leads to scoliosis (DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1513252112).
> **Hypothesis**: Failure of focal adhesions removes the baseline gravity reference, reducing the active feedback gain ($G_{mech}$) needed to counteract early postural deviations.
* **Piezo-Cilia Stress Lock**: Asymmetric compression destroys primary cilia, derepressing osteogenesis.
* **PIEZO1-YAP Axis**: Mechanical overload triggers YAP/MMP axis, degrading IVD stiffness (Peng et al., 2025).
* **Osteoblast Mechanotransduction Defect**: Defective actin-VEGF-A axis impairs flow-induced adaptation, dropping active gain (Oliazadeh et al., 2022).
* **Voltage Gain**: Hyperpolarization (`KCNQ`) dampens the proprioceptive error-correction loop (Sánchez-Carranza et al., 2024).
* **Caveolin Gain Control**: Loss of `CAV1` removes the noise filter, increasing mechanotransductive hypersensitivity (Moreno-Vicente et al., 2018).
* **Stochastic Resonance Failure**: Lack of physiological "noise" (dither) from muscle micro-tremors causes high-threshold mechanosensors to lock up.

### Structural & Fluid Dynamics
* **CSF-Proprioception Relay**: Reissner Fiber (`PKD2L1`) acts as a tension plumb line for muscle symmetry.
* **Glymphatic Stasis**: Fluid stagnation drives macrophage entry and annulus degradation via MMPs.
* **PIEZO1-GPX4 Ferroptosis**: Overload triggers ferroptosis in growth plates, driving asymmetric ossification (Chen et al., 2025).
