# From Molecules to Curvature: A Causal Map of Scoliosis

## Overview
The **Scoliosis Mechanism Map** connects molecular actors to tissue mechanics via **Biological Counter-Curvature**. The spine actively grows into an anticipatory S-shape to neutralize gravity. Scoliosis is a failure of this active control system.

The organizing principle of **Biological Counter-Curvature** unifies diverse molecular pathways, framing them as components of a single active control system working to offset gravitational loading. Instead of viewing scoliosis as an isolated defect (e.g., a "bone problem"), Counter-Curvature reveals how genetics, mechanics, and metabolism feed into a unified mechanosensory loop. This continuous feedback loop dynamically adjusts structural stiffness ($EI$) and biological target curvature ($\kappa_0$) via mechanotransductive gain ($G_{mech}$) to maintain spinal stability against gravity. When pathways like PIEZO1, integrins, or cellular metabolism fail, the active control loop collapses, leading to Euler buckling and the emergent 3D deformity of scoliosis.

## Failure Modes

### 5. Integrin Mechanosensing Failure
* **Path**: `ITGB1` $\rightarrow$ `Focal Adhesion` $\rightarrow$ `Gain`
* **Role**: **Cell-ECM Uncoupling**. Integrin $\beta$1 (ITGB1) acts as the primary gravity sensor at the cell-ECM interface.
* **Evidence**: Conditional deletion of ITGB1 in cartilage leads to scoliosis (DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1513252112).
> **Hypothesis**: Loss of ITGB1-mediated focal adhesion signaling uncouples cartilage cells from external gravitational loads, severely dropping $G_{mech}$ and causing uncompensated growth.

* **Role**: Asymmetric compression destroys cilia, derepressing osteogenesis. Mechanical stress overactivates PIEZO1, inhibiting GPX4 and breaking down antioxidant defense, causing ferroptosis and growth plate dysplasia (Chen et al., 2025).

* **Role**: Without high-frequency "noise" (muscle micro-tremors, vascular pulsation) acting as a dither, high-threshold mechanosensors like PIEZO lock up. They fail to detect the initial slow geometric drift, dropping feedback gain and precipitating buckling.
