# From Molecules to Curvature: The Counter-Curvature Map

## The Organizing Principle: Biological Counter-Curvature

The **Scoliosis Mechanism Map** (`docs/figures/scoliosis_mechanism_map.mmd`) visualizes the spine not as a passive column, but as an active, information-processing structure. Its primary goal is to generate **Counter-Curvature** (e.g., lordosis) to neutralize gravitational moments. Scoliosis is defined here as a failure of this active stabilization system—a "loss of control" rather than a simple material failure.

The map traces the causal flow from genetic actors to macroscopic mechanics across five layers.

---

## 1. The Geometry Layer (The Blueprint)
**Property: Rest Curvature ($\kappa_0$)**

The spine must "know" its target shape.
*   **HOX Cluster (`HOXA10`, `HOXD11`)**: Defines segmental identity, establishing the target curvature for each region (lumbar vs. thoracic).
*   **Symmetry Breaking (`DNAH5`)**: Motile cilia generate nodal flow to establish Left-Right symmetry. Disruption here leads to "Chiral Torsion" ($\epsilon$), initiating a rotational bias.

## 2. The Stiffness Layer (The Material)
**Property: Bending Stiffness ($EI$)**

The spine must be rigid enough to resist buckling.
*   **Collagen Assembly (`COL2A1`)**: The primary structural component.
*   **Disc Maintenance (`ADGRG6`)**: Prevents failure at the intervertebral joints.
*   **Mechanotransduction (`YAP/TAZ`)**: A feedback loop where mechanical strain reinforces tissue stiffness.
*   **Degradation (`MMP1/3`)**: In microgravity or inflammation, upregulation of MMPs dissolves the matrix, leading to "Soft Buckling."

## 3. The Polarity Layer (The Vector)
**Property: Tissue Anisotropy**

The spine must grow in a specific direction. Isotropic (random) growth leads to instability.
*   **PCP Signaling (`PTK7`)**: Aligns cells along the stress vector.
*   **The Tension Tether (`POC5`)**: *New Arc*. `POC5` and `CDH23` are essential for centriolar and ciliary integrity. They act as "molecular guy-wires," maintaining the structural tension required for directional sensing. Loss of POC5 leads to **Ciliary Tethering** failure and a collapse of **Tissue Anisotropy**, allowing the spine to drift entropically.
    *   *Citation: Baschal, E. E., et al. (2014). POC5 variants in idiopathic scoliosis. J Med Genet 51.*

## 4. The Gain Layer (The Control Loop)
**Property: Feedback Gain ($G_{mech}$)**

The spine must sense errors and correct them.
*   **Piezo Duality**:
    *   **Piezo1 (Scalar)**: Senses magnitude (bone density).
    *   **Piezo2 (Vector)**: Senses direction (proprioception).
*   **Proprioceptive Maintenance (`RUNX3`)**: Ensures survival of the sensory neurons that detect posture.
*   **Urotensin Relay (`PKD2L1` $\to$ `URP`)**: CSF flow acts as a proxy for gravity, signaling paraspinal muscles to maintain symmetric tone.

## 5. The Metabolic Layer (The Support)
**Property: Muscle Tone & Nuclear Stiffness**

The control loop requires energy and structural integrity.
*   **Nuclear Stiffness (`LAMIN` $\to$ `H3K9me3`)**: Heterochromatin acts as a "dampener." Loss of tension (microgravity) softens the nucleus, decoupling the genome from mechanical signals.
*   **Metabolic Switch (`VIM` $\to$ `mtROS`)**: Cytoskeletal collapse triggers mitochondrial ROS, which drives an epigenetic shift (`METTL3`) favoring adipogenesis over myogenesis (Fatty Infiltration).

---

## Macroscopic Outcome

These layers converge on **Stability**.
*   **Success**: The system maintains a stable S-Curve that "surfs" the gravitational field.
*   **Failure**: A defect in any layer—Geometry (wrong target), Stiffness (soft material), Polarity (wrong direction), or Gain (blind sensors)—causes the system to buckle into the lowest-energy configuration: the helical deformity of **Scoliosis**.
