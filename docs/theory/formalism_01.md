# Biological Counter-Curvature: Formalism

## 1. Definitions and Units

The theory posits that biological information acts as a geometric modifier of the effective spacetime metric experienced by the organism. We model this in the limits of a Cosserat rod under gravity.

### 1.1. Core Variables

| Symbol | Definition | Dimensions | Units (SI) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| $s$ | Arc length along the biological axis | $[L]$ | $m$ |
| $t$ | Developmental time | $[T]$ | $s$ |
| $I(s,t)$ | Morphogenetic Information Field | $[1]$ | Dimensionless (normalized) |
| $\nabla I$ | Spatial gradient of information | $[L^{-1}]$ | $m^{-1}$ |
| $\boldsymbol{\kappa}_{rest}(s)$ | Intrinsic (rest) curvature vector | $[L^{-1}]$ | $m^{-1}$ |
| $EI(s)$ | Flexural rigidity (bending stiffness) | $[ML^3T^{-2}]$ | $N \cdot m^2$ |
| $\mathbf{M}_{bio}(s)$ | Active biological moment | $[ML^2T^{-2}]$ | $N \cdot m$ |

### 1.2. The Information Field $I(s,t)$

$I(s,t)$ represents the normalized concentration of morphogenetic instructables (e.g., HOX gene expression levels, morphogen gradients like SHH/BMP).
$$ I(s,t) \in [0, 1] $$

## 2. Coupling Constants

We define three coupling constants that map the dimensionless information gradient to mechanical observables.

### 2.1. The Morphomechanical Coupling Constant ($\chi_M$)

$\chi_M$ links the information gradient to the active moment generated by the organism to oppose gravity.

$$ \mathbf{M}_{bio} = \chi_M \cdot \nabla I $$

*   **Dimensions**: Since $\mathbf{M}_{bio}$ is torque $[ML^2T^{-2}]$ and $\nabla I$ is $[L^{-1}]$, $\chi_M$ must have dimensions:
    $$ [\chi_M] = [ML^3T^{-2}] $$
*   **Physical Interpretation**: This is dimensionally equivalent to **Bending Stiffness** ($EI$). It represents the "stiffness of the information field" — how much torque is generated per unit of informational difference.

### 2.2. The Geometric Coupling Constant ($\chi_\kappa$)

$\chi_\kappa$ links information to the rest curvature (the "target shape").

$$ \boldsymbol{\kappa}_{rest} = \boldsymbol{\kappa}_{gen} + \chi_\kappa \cdot \nabla I $$

*   **Dimensions**: $[1]$ (Dimensionless).
*   **Physical Interpretation**: A scaling factor determining how strongly information gradients dictate local curvature targets.

### 2.3. The Tissue Anisotropy Tensor ($\mathbf{\Lambda}$)

$\mathbf{\Lambda}$ represents the directional efficiency of the tissue in converting biochemical gradients into mechanical torque. It connects molecular anisotropy (e.g., cytoskeletal alignment) to macroscopic actuation.

$$ \mathbf{M}_{bio} = \chi_M (\mathbf{\Lambda} \cdot \nabla I) $$

*   **Dimensions**: $[1]$ (Dimensionless rank-2 tensor).
*   **Physical Interpretation**:
    *   In a perfectly aligned tissue (isotropic transduction), $\mathbf{\Lambda}$ is the identity matrix $\mathbf{I}$.
    *   If Planar Cell Polarity (PCP) signaling is skewed (e.g., *PTK7* mutation), $\mathbf{\Lambda}$ acquires off-diagonal components, converting a planar gradient ($\nabla I_y$) into a torsional moment ($M_z$), driving scoliotic rotation.
    *   The trace $\text{Tr}(\mathbf{\Lambda})$ corresponds to the "Anisotropy Score" measured in protein structural analysis.

## 3. Measurable Proxy: The Counter-Curvature Energy

To operationalize the hypothesis, we define the **Counter-Curvature Energy** ($U_{CC}$) as the work done by the organism against the gravitational field to maintain its shape, normalized by the elastic energy.

$$ U_{CC} = \frac{\int_0^L \mathbf{M}_{bio}(s) \cdot (\boldsymbol{\kappa}(s) - \boldsymbol{\kappa}_{rest}(s)) ds}{\int_0^L \rho A g z(s) ds} $$

A higher $U_{CC}$ indicates a stronger "anti-gravity" response driven by information.

## 4. Falsifiable Tests

The theory makes specific predictions about growth in altered gravity environments and genetic knockouts.

### Test A: The "Geodesic Growth" Prediction (Microgravity)
*   **Hypothesis**: If $\chi_\kappa$ is intrinsic (genetic), the spinal S-shape is an informational standing wave that persists even when $g \to 0$.
*   **Data Needed**: 3D spinal scans of developing vertebrates (e.g., zebrafish, mice) raised in microgravity (ISS or clinostat).
*   **Refutation**: If the organism grows in a straight line (Euclidean geodesic) or a random walk in the absence of gravity, the hypothesis that "curvature is intrinsic information" is falsified. The theory predicts the S-shape ($I(s)$) remains.

### Test B: The "Mechanotransduction Blindness" Prediction (Piezo Knockout)
*   **Hypothesis**: The maintenance of $\chi_M$ requires active feedback. Knocking out mechanosensors (e.g., Piezo1/2) breaks the link between $\nabla I$ and momentary load, effectively setting $\chi_M \to 0$.
*   **Data Needed**: Spinal geometry of *Piezo1/2* conditional knockouts in paraspinal muscles/osteoblasts.
*   **Refutation**: If *Piezo* KO mice develop normal S-curves despite the loss of sensation, the active coupling mechanism ($\mathbf{M}_{bio} \propto \nabla I$) is falsified. The theory predicts progressive scoliosis or collapse (buckling).

### Test C: The "Tensor Misalignment" Prediction (PCP Perturbation)
*   **Hypothesis**: The directionality of $\mathbf{M}_{bio}$ depends on PCP signaling. Disrupting PCP genes (e.g., *PTK7*, *VANGL2*) alters $\mathbf{\Lambda}$, causing off-axis torque generation.
*   **Data Needed**: 3D kinematics of spinal growth in *Ptk7* mutant zebrafish/mice compared to wild-type, specifically measuring the ratio of torsion to lateral bending ($\tau / \kappa$).
*   **Refutation**: If *Ptk7* mutants exhibit only reduced magnitude of curvature (lower $\chi_M$) but no torsional instability (maintained planarity), the hypothesis that "PCP defines the $\mathbf{\Lambda}$ tensor direction" is falsified.

### Test D: The "Anisotropy Threshold" Prediction
*   **Hypothesis**: Effective mechanotransduction requires a minimum degree of protein anisotropy to maintain $\text{Tr}(\mathbf{\Lambda}) > \Lambda_{crit}$.
*   **Data Needed**: Correlation between AFCC "Anisotropy Scores" of variant proteins (e.g., truncated forms) and the resulting spinal cobb angle in corresponding organism models.
*   **Refutation**: If variants with low structural anisotropy (spherical proteins) can still rescue the spinal phenotype in complementation assays, the link between "molecular anisotropy" and "macroscopic torque generation" is falsified.
