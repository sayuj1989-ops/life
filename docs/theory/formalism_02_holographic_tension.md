## 1. The Holographic Tension Hypothesis

Building upon the Information-Cosserat framework, we introduce the **Holographic Tension Hypothesis**. This postulates that developmental information is not just a local gradient, but acts as a global, lower-dimensional "holographic" projection onto the boundaries of the developing organism, creating a self-tensioning tensegrity network.

### 1.1 The Holographic Boundary Tensor ($\mathbf{H}$)

Instead of a bulk information field $I(s,t)$, we define a surface information tensor $\mathbf{H}(\partial V, t)$ that resides only on the geometric boundary ($\partial V$) of the structure.

$$ \mathbf{H}(\partial V, t) = \oint_{\partial V} \mathbf{n} \otimes \nabla_s I_{surf} \, dA $$

*   **Symbols**:
    *   $\mathbf{n}$: Outward normal vector to the boundary.
    *   $\nabla_s I_{surf}$: Surface gradient of the boundary morphogenetic field.
*   **Physical Interpretation**: The boundary tensor encodes the "target" stress state required to counteract gravity, projected entirely from the skin/fascial envelope inward, akin to a tensegrity structure.

### 1.2 The Bulk-Boundary Duality Constant ($\mathcal{D}_{Holo}$)

The internal bulk properties (like vertebral rest curvature $\boldsymbol{\kappa}_{rest}$) are driven by the boundary tensor via the duality constant $\mathcal{D}_{Holo}$.

$$ \boldsymbol{\kappa}_{rest}(s) = \mathcal{D}_{Holo} \int_{\partial V} K_{kernel}(s, r') \mathbf{H}(r') dr' $$

*   **Symbols**:
    *   $K_{kernel}$: A non-local propagator kernel function.
    *   $\mathcal{D}_{Holo}$: Holographic coupling constant $[L^{-1}]$.
*   **Implication**: Scoliosis is not initiated by an internal vertebral defect, but by a failure in the tensioning of the fascial boundary (the "holographic screen"). The spine buckles because the boundary is slack.

## 2. Falsifiable Tests

### Test BK: The Fascial Slit Experiment
*   **Hypothesis**: If bulk geometry is holographically encoded by boundary tension, creating a longitudinal slit in the deep fascia (disrupting $\mathbf{H}$) without damaging the spine or paraspinal muscles should instantly induce a predictable lateral curvature.
*   **Data Needed**: Immediate spinal curvature changes following unilateral fascial incisions in a growing animal model.
*   **Refutation**: If releasing fascial tension does not alter the resting geometry of the spine, the holographic tension hypothesis is falsified.
