The spatial morphology of Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) falls into 6 distinct Lenke curve types. The Biological Countercurvature framework predicts these shapes as emergent buckling eigenmodes of a multi-segment Cosserat rod. The spine's regions have varying stiffness (EI), neural delay (tau), and damping (b) due to factors like rib cage buttressing and paraspinal muscle mass. When the global stability threshold is breached, the deficit localization determines the primary buckling zone.
