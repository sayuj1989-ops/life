Concept: Derivative Gain Trap in Biological Countercurvature
Focus: The transition from stable damping to "Metabolic Buckling" during adolescent growth spurt.

Visual Elements:
1. Two connected panels comparing "Childhood Spine" vs "Adolescent Spine" (Growth Spurt).
2. Left Panel (Childhood):
   - Normal S-curve spine.
   - Low neural delay (\tau).
   - Derivative gain (D-gain) provides "Stable Damping".
   - Shows a strong, balanced inverted pendulum.
3. Right Panel (Adolescent Growth Spurt):
   - Deformed scoliotic spine (buckling).
   - High neural delay (\tau).
   - The same Derivative gain now causes "Metabolic Buckling" (Destabilizing).
   - Visual cue of "Delay-induced instability" creating an unstable inverted pendulum.
