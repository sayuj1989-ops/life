Derivative Gain Trap Mechanism in Temporal Cognition
During rapid adolescent growth (Peak Height Velocity), the spine's physical length L(t) and mass m(t) increase rapidly.
As a consequence, the physical neural delay \tau(t) naturally increases due to longer nerve conduction pathways.
To maintain stability of the human inverted pendulum posture, the cognitive predictive horizon T_pred(t) must actively expand to match or exceed \tau(t).
However, when the growth rate exceeds the learning rate of the central nervous system, a temporal mismatch occurs: T_pred(t) < \tau(t).
This 'Derivative Gain Gap' leads to delayed motor corrections, causing macroscopic instability (Metabolic Buckling/Scoliosis).
