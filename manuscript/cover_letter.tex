\begin{letter}{The Editors \\ \textit{Nature}}

\opening{Dear Editors,}

Following helpful reviewer feedback on our initial submission, we have substantially revised the manuscript, titled \textbf{''Biological Counter-Curvature: An Information–Cosserat Framework for Vertebral Geometry and Adolescent Scoliosis,''} for consideration as a Research Article in \textit{Nature}.

In this revision, we have heavily edited the manuscript to reflect the strict Nature standard. Specifically, we trimmed the abstract to 150 words, removed all General Relativity framing, replacing it with the rigorously defined Information-Elasticity Coupling (IEC) framework, and expanded the protein structural analysis from 16 to 23 proteins to thoroughly quantify the Demand-Supply Anisotropy Gap. We added robust, multi-scale discussions detailing the protein-level dynamics underlying the thermodynamic energy deficit, explicitly compared our IEC framework head-to-head with estrogen, melatonin, and proprioceptive theories, and strengthened the limitations section to explicitly acknowledge the theoretical nature of this work without wet-lab experimentation.

The IEC framework is the first quantitative theory to unify spinal geometry, adolescent scoliosis, and microgravity adaptation under a single physical principle, with direct clinical relevance to a condition affecting 3\% of adolescents globally. We present 12 specific, falsifiable predictions generated by the model.
