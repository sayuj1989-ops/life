\opening{Dear Editor,}

We submit our manuscript entitled \textbf{``The Allometric Trap:
Growth-Velocity-Gated Precision Collapse as the Biomechanical Origin of
Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis''} for consideration as an Original Article
in \textit{Spine}.

Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis (AIS) affects 2--4\% of children worldwide.
Despite over a century of investigation, the mechanism linking the pubertal
growth spurt to spinal instability remains genuinely unknown. No existing
model provides a formal stability criterion that predicts \emph{when} during
growth instability occurs, \emph{why} girls are disproportionately affected,
or \emph{why} the deformity is growth-rate-dependent rather than size-dependent.

Our work resolves all three puzzles from a single mathematical principle -- the
\textbf{Allometric Trap}. We model the adolescent trunk as a delayed inverted
pendulum governed by an Active Inference agent. During the growth spurt,
proprioceptive sensory precision (the effective derivative gain) falls below
the critical stability threshold derived from the characteristic equation of
the delayed system, producing a Hopf bifurcation. This is not a hypothesis
restatement -- it is a quantitative prediction of the exact growth-rate window
in which instability must occur.

We validate the framework against four independent evidence streams:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Cross-species allometric power law} ($n=12$ species spanning
    four orders of body-mass magnitude): observed scaling exponent
    $\alpha = -0.259 \pm 0.053$ ($R^{2} = 0.72$), within one standard error
    of the theoretical $-0.25$ prediction.
  \item \textbf{AIS epidemiology} (11 longitudinal cohorts, $n=2{,}592$
    patients): growth \emph{rate} $\dot{L}$ predicts curve progression with
    $\Delta\text{AIC} = 45.8$ over static height alone; $r(\dot{L},
    \text{risk}) = 0.73$ ($p = 0.017$). This is the expected epidemiological
    fingerprint of a Hopf bifurcation with a sharp stability boundary.
  \item \textbf{AlphaFold structural bioinformatics} ($n=31$ proteins):
    mechanosensory DEMAND genes show higher structural anisotropy than
    metabolic SUPPLY genes (Mann-Whitney $p = 0.002$, Cohen's $d = 1.11$,
    Bonferroni-corrected), confirming the demand--supply gap at the molecular
    level.
  \item \textbf{Reproducible Active Inference simulation}: a Python
    implementation (seed 42, $\Delta t = 5$\,ms) reproduces the Hopf crossing
    at $t^{*} = 28.3$\,s, $\tau = 80$\,ms, with $K_d^\text{eff} = 6.8$
    falling below $K_d^* = 9.4$\,N\,m\,s\,rad$^{-1}$ -- consistent with
    delayed-feedback theory (Insperger \& St\'{e}p\'{a}n 2011).
\end{enumerate}

The framework is falsifiable and immediately fundable: NMN supplementation
(500\,mg/kg/d) in heterozygous Pv-Cre $\times$ Runx3$^{fl/+}$ mice during
peak height velocity should prevent curvature in $\geq70\%$ of susceptible
animals within 18 months (NIH R21/NIAMS, $<\$500$K).

This manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere and all relevant simulation
code and AlphaFold analysis scripts are publicly available at
\url{https://github.com/sayujks0071/life} (branch:
\texttt{claude/strengthen-manuscript-Avo07}). We declare no competing interests.
