We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Metabolic Buckling: An Energy Deficit Window Explains Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis," for consideration as an Article in Nature.

Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) affects 3% of the global population and remains a "medical mystery" despite decades of genomic research. We present a paradigm shift: AIS is not a bone disease, but a thermodynamic failure of the "Biological Countercurvature" mechanism that maintains our upright posture against gravity.

Using a novel Information-Cosserat framework, we identify an "Energy Deficit Window" during rapid growth where the metabolic cost of maintaining straightness ($L^4$) outstrips supply ($L^2$). We show that high-anisotropy mechanosensors like Vimentin collapse under this deficit, leading to "sensor blindness" and buckling. This explains the 10:1 female prevalence and the specific vulnerability of the adolescent growth spurt.

This work bridges theoretical physics, structural biology, and clinical medicine. It is not a specialist orthopedic study; it reveals a fundamental scaling law of vertebrate morphogenesis.
