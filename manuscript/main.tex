\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent\textbf{Impact Statement:} We identify a universal scaling law governing the metabolic cost of maintaining body shape against gravity, explaining why human spines are uniquely vulnerable to scoliosis during adolescent growth and reclassifying a common disease as a predictable thermodynamic scaling failure.
\vspace{0.5em}

\begin{mathbox}[Model Summary: The Information--Elasticity Coupling (IEC) Framework]
\textbf{The Question:} What physical law governs how organisms maintain an S-shaped spine against gravity, and why does this maintenance fail during adolescent growth causing scoliosis?\\[4pt]
\textbf{The Scaling Law:} The Bio-Gravitational Number $\mathcal{B}_g = EI/(MgL^2)$ predicts that humans ($\mathcal{B}_g \approx 0.01$) cannot passively resist gravity and must actively maintain spinal geometry at metabolic cost. The S-curve is not a passive structure, but an actively maintained thermodynamic standing wave.\\[4pt]
\textbf{The Mechanism:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Information--Elasticity Coupling}: A developmental information field $I(s)$ (representing HOX-patterned segmental identity) locally modifies Cosserat rod mechanics to specify the target S-shape.
    \item \textbf{Scoliosis as Scaling Failure}: During the adolescent growth spurt, the metabolic cost of this counter-curvature ($P_{\mathrm{counter}} \propto L^4$) outpaces proprioceptive supply ($S_{\mathrm{proprio}} \propto L^2$). This Energy Deficit Window triggers a supercritical Hopf bifurcation.
    \item \textbf{Loss of Geometry}: Small patterning asymmetries are amplified, forcing the spine to collapse from the metabolically expensive S-curve into a lower-energy, scoliotic mode.
\end{itemize}
\end{mathbox}
