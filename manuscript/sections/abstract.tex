\begin{abstract}
The human spine maintains a complex S-shaped sagittal profile against gravity, yet its physical principle and adolescent vulnerability remain unexplained. We introduce the Information--Elasticity Coupling (IEC) framework, integrating a developmental information field representing HOX patterning with Cosserat rod mechanics. We define the Bio-Gravitational Number $\mathcal{B}_g = EI/(MgL^2)$, demonstrating humans ($\mathcal{B}_g \approx 0.01$) require active metabolic maintenance of shape. The IEC framework selects the S-curve as the energetic ground state, reproducing normal lordosis and kyphosis. Phase diagram analysis reveals gravity-dominated, cooperative, and information-dominated regimes. During simulated adolescent growth ($L > 0.35$\,m), an Energy Deficit Window emerges where small information-field asymmetries undergo supercritical bifurcation into scoliotic deformities. AlphaFold analysis of 23 mechanotransduction proteins reveals a 72\% demand-supply anisotropy gap, quantifying this structural vulnerability. Spinal geometry acts as a thermodynamic standing wave; our framework unifies developmental patterning and biomechanics, providing testable predictions for adolescent scoliosis onset.
\end{abstract}
