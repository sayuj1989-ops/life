\begin{abstract}
The human spine maintains a complex S-shaped sagittal profile against gravity, yet its physical principle and adolescent vulnerability remain unexplained. We introduce the Information--Elasticity Coupling (IEC) framework, integrating a developmental information field representing HOX patterning with Cosserat rod mechanics, implemented via PyElastica simulations. The IEC framework selects the S-shaped spinal profile as the energetic ground state, reproducing normal lordosis and kyphosis. Phase diagram analysis reveals gravity-dominated, cooperative, and information-dominated morphological regimes. During simulated adolescent growth, small information-field asymmetries are amplified into scoliotic deformities through a supercritical bifurcation at a critical length, explaining adolescent scoliosis onset. Furthermore, AlphaFold structural analysis of mechanotransduction proteins reveals a demand-supply anisotropy gap quantifying this structural vulnerability. We conclude that spinal geometry emerges as a thermodynamic standing wave maintained by metabolic expenditure. The IEC framework unifies developmental patterning and biomechanics, generating specific testable predictions for scoliosis prevention.
\end{abstract}
