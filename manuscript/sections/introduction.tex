\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Background: Gravity as a Morphogenetic Cue}
Life on Earth has evolved within the unyielding context of a constant gravitational field ($g \approx 9.81$ m/s$^2$). This force acts not merely as a mechanical load, but as a ubiquitous morphogenetic cue~\cite{thompson1917growth,morey2002physiological}. For sessile or passive structures, gravity dictates a simple, low-energy equilibrium: a beam clamped at one end and subject to its own weight will inevitably sag into a monotonic C-shape~\cite{goriely2017mathematics,moulton2013morphoelastic}. This passive tendency is opposed by biological growth and remodeling, which operate under the constraints of ``morphomechanics''---the interplay between mechanical stress and developmental patterning~\cite{taber1995biomechanics,beloussov1998morphomechanics}.

In vertebrates, particularly bipedal humans, the challenge is acute. The human spine must support the head and trunk against gravity while enabling locomotion. Instead of sagging into a passive C-shape, the healthy human spine maintains a complex, robust S-shaped profile. This geometry is encoded in the HOX gene clusters~\cite{wellik2007hox,burke1995hox}, but the ``Translation Problem'' remains: how is a discrete genetic code translated into continuous analog curvature? Evidence from spaceflight shows that the S-shape persists even in microgravity~\cite{green2018spinal,clement2025adaptation}, suggesting it is an actively maintained ``counter-curvature''—a geometric standing wave driven by biological information against the passive gravitational metric.

\subsection{Bridge: Biological Counter-Curvature as Active Inference}
To explain the robustness of spinal geometry and its vulnerability to idiopathic deformity, we propose a unifying hypothesis rooted in the Free Energy Principle~\cite{friston2010free}: the spine acts as an \textbf{Active Inference Machine}. In this framework, spinal alignment is not a static property but a dynamic process of minimizing prediction error.

\subsubsection{The Generative Model vs. Sensory Evidence}
The system possesses a \textbf{Generative Model}---the Genetic Reference Metric ($\bar{g}$)---which encodes the evolutionarily optimized S-shape. This model generates top-down predictions of proprioceptive and vestibular states. Simultaneously, gravity provides the primary \textbf{Sensory Evidence} (the Actual Metric, $g$), which constantly tests these predictions. The discrepancy between the genetically expected shape and the gravitationally deformed reality constitutes the \textbf{Free Energy} or prediction error ($U_{CC}$), which the system minimizes via two pathways:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Action}: Generating active moments (muscle tone, differential growth) to physically align the spine with the reference model~\cite{adams2013predictions}.
    \item \textbf{Perception}: Adjusting the gain of sensory channels to contextualize the error (e.g., down-weighting proprioception during rapid movement)~\cite{peterka2002sensor, cullen2012vestibular}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{The Vector-Scalar Mismatch Failure Mode}
We identify a critical vulnerability in this system: the \textbf{Vector-Scalar Mismatch}. Spinal mechanotransduction relies on two distinct sensor classes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Vector Sensors} (e.g., Piezo2, Lamin A/C): Anisotropic structures detecting alignment and tension (the ``Direction'' of gravity)~\cite{ranade2014piezo2, swift2013nuclear}. These sensors provide the directional error signal required for alignment.
    \item \textbf{Scalar Sensors} (e.g., Piezo1, SLYM): Isotropic sensors detecting hydrostatic pressure or tissue density (the ``Magnitude'' of stress)~\cite{sun2019piezo1, ramli2024piezo1}. These sensors regulate non-directional tissue homeostasis.
\end{itemize}

In microgravity or under specific genetic defects (e.g., AIS), Vector Sensors go silent due to the loss of directional load or sensitivity. However, Scalar Sensors remain active (e.g., responding to osmotic swelling or fluid stasis). This sensory dissociation blinds the Active Inference controller to orientation while retaining a signal of stress. The system interprets this residual scalar noise as a large, unresolvable prediction error, driving a "panic response" of high-gain remodeling that manifests as scoliosis or "Spinal Jetlag"~\cite{dudek2017clock, touchstone2019recovery}.

\subsection{Testable Predictions}
This framework yields specific, falsifiable predictions regarding the etiology of spinal deformity and adaptation to microgravity:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{The Vestibular Precision Ratio ($\Omega_{VP}$)}: We predict that Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) is a ``Geometric Hallucination'' caused by a low Vestibular Precision Ratio ($\Omega_{VP} = \gamma_P / \gamma_V$). Patients rely excessively on noisy proprioception due to a failure in vestibular gain ($\gamma_V$)~\cite{pialasse2015vestibular}. We predict that adding white noise via Stochastic Vestibular Stimulation (SVS) will paradoxically \textit{improve} EMG symmetry in scoliosis patients by lowering the signal-to-noise detection threshold (stochastic resonance) and forcing the CNS to re-weight vestibular priors~\cite{peterka2002sensor, agrawal2009vestibular}.

    \item \textbf{The Glymphatic-MMP Axis}: We posit that spinal glymphatic clearance is gravity-assisted. We predict that unloading leads to immediate fluid stasis in the paravascular pathway~\cite{mader2026glymphatic}, causing local accumulation of metabolic waste. This chemical stress triggers the upregulation of Matrix Metalloproteinases (MMP1/MMP3) before any structural degeneration occurs. This can be validated by measuring tracer clearance rates in microgravity analogs; we predict a localized retention of tracers in the thoracolumbar junction that correlates with subsequent MMP expression~\cite{chen2025mmp, iliff2012glymphatic}.

    \item \textbf{The Adipogenic Switch}: We propose that ``fatty infiltration'' in unloaded paraspinal muscle is not passive atrophy but an active metabolic switch gated by mitochondrial ROS. The loss of nuclear mechanotransduction (Lamin A/C softening) decouples the nucleus from the cytoskeleton, leading to mitochondrial dysfunction and ROS spikes~\cite{shao2025ros, handschin2024paraspinal}. We predict that treating unloaded animals with mitochondrial ROS scavengers (e.g., MitoQ) will prevent the adipogenic switch and preserve muscle quality, even in the absence of mechanical loading.
\end{enumerate}

In the following sections, we formalize this theory using Cosserat rod mechanics, demonstrating how minimizing $U_{CC}$ naturally emerges the S-shaped spine and how parameter mismatches lead to scoliotic deformities.
