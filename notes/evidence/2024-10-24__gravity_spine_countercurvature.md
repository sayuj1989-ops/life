# Evidence Note: Astrogliosis and Neuroinflammation Underlie Scoliosis Upon Cilia Dysfunction

**Date:** 2024-10-24
**Reference:** Djebar et al. (2024) *Astrogliosis and neuroinflammation underlie scoliosis upon cilia dysfunction*. eLife, 13. [DOI: 10.7554/elife.96831](https://doi.org/10.7554/elife.96831)

### Claim
Cilia dysfunction, specifically in the transition zone protein `rpgrip1l`, induces juvenile scoliosis via perturbed ventricular homeostasis and Reissner fiber disruption, which subsequently triggers localized astrogliosis and neuroinflammation rather than operating strictly via global structural weakness.

### Mechanism
The onset of spinal curvature is preceded by ventricle dilations, the perturbation of Reissner fiber polymerization, and the loss of multiciliated tufts around the subcommissural organ. This leads to neuroinflammation (associated with increased Annexin levels) and astrogliosis around the diencephalic and rhombencephalic ventricles. Anti-inflammatory treatment reduces both the astrogliosis and the severity/penetrance of scoliosis.

### Why it matters for S-shaped spinal growth / scoliosis
This provides a non-musculoskeletal neuro-inflammatory pathway for scoliosis development. Instead of scoliosis being solely a mechanical failure of bone or muscle, it highlights that central nervous system (CNS) hydrodynamics, specifically cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) flow and Reissner fiber tension, are critical for maintaining spinal straightness during growth.

### How it constrains or supports the counter-curvature hypothesis
**Supports:** The biological counter-curvature hypothesis frames scoliosis as a failure of the active postural control system to resolve a gravitational parameter. The Reissner fiber and associated multiciliated cells act as a flow-sensitive "straightness" sensor or central proprioceptive relay. If cilia dysfunction disrupts this sensor, the controller (CNS) loses its high-fidelity feedback regarding spinal alignment, plunging the system into an instability (buckling) as the metabolic cost of counter-curvature cannot be properly calibrated. The localized astrogliosis indicates a site of intense neuro-inflammatory stress where this sensory failure is localized.

### Open question + proposed test
**Open Question:** Does gravitational unloading (simulated microgravity) exacerbate or mask the neuro-inflammatory astrogliosis in cilia-dysfunctional models?
**Proposed Test:** Expose `rpgrip1l` mutant zebrafish to simulated microgravity (e.g., using a clinostat) versus 1G controls, and quantify the severity of astrogliosis (via GFAP/Annexin markers) and scoliosis penetrance to determine if the inflammatory response is dependent on the magnitude of the gravitational vector.
