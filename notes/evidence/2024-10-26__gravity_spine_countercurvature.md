# Evidence Note: EPHA4, Locomotion, and Spinal Counter-Curvature

## Claim
Dynamic gravity resistance (locomotion) via coordinated central pattern generators (CPGs) is a requisite mechanical input for maintaining spinal symmetry and preventing idiopathic scoliosis.

## Mechanism (genes/proteins/pathways)
EPHA4 (Ephrin type-A receptor 4) signaling is required for proper axon pathfinding in the spinal cord. Dysregulation (e.g., loss-of-function mutations or exon skipping variants like c.1318+10344A>G) disrupts neural patterning and CPG function. This impairment leads to uncoordinated left-right locomotion, causing asymmetric muscular loading on the developing spine.

## Why it matters for S-shaped spinal growth / scoliosis
Scoliosis is traditionally viewed either as a structural defect (e.g., ECM or bone) or a passive mechanosensory failure (e.g., Piezo). This finding demonstrates that the *active, rhythmic generation of symmetric force* during gait is equally critical. The human S-shaped spine evolved for bipedal locomotion; if the locomotor input is asymmetric due to CPG defects, the spine is subjected to uneven tension, initiating buckling and wedging during periods of rapid growth.

## How it constrains or supports the counter-curvature hypothesis
This supports the "biological counter-curvature" framing by emphasizing that gravity adaptation is not merely static weight-bearing. The counter-curvature is an active, dynamic thermodynamic structure maintained by symmetric tension (proprioceptive supply matching metabolic demand). EPHA4 dysfunction breaks the symmetry of the information field ($I(s)$), causing localized energy deficits and phase shifts that manifest as macroscopic curvature. It confirms that the system requires high-frequency, rhythmic tension signals (locomotion) to maintain its null state against gravity.

## Open question + proposed test
**Open Question:** Does the loss of EPHA4-mediated locomotion induce scoliosis by creating a static load imbalance, or by depriving the spine of high-frequency tension signals needed to "refresh" mechanosensory setpoints?
**Proposed Test:** Compare spine alignment in EPHA4 mutant models under normal cage activity (uncoordinated gait) vs. a suspended locomotion treadmill (forced symmetric rhythmic movement) vs. forced swimming (rhythmic movement with reduced gravity loading).
