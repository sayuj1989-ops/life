# Evidence Note: Proprioceptive Feedback as the Physical Substrate of the "Information Field"

**Date:** 2025-02-17
**Topic:** Gravity, Spinal Alignment, and Mechanotransduction
**Reference:** Xie, J. et al. (2023). "PIEZO2 regulates dorsal root ganglion neuron-mediated proprioception to guide spinal alignment". *Nature*, 615, 459–465.

## 1. Key Claim
Xie et al. (2023) demonstrate that the mechanosensitive ion channel **PIEZO2**, expressed specifically in Dorsal Root Ganglion (DRG) proprioceptors, is essential for maintaining normal spinal alignment. Conditional knockout of *Piezo2* in proprioceptive neurons leads to severe scoliosis in mice, without underlying bone or muscle defects. This establishes that spinal geometry is not merely a passive structural property but an **active state maintained by continuous neural feedback**.

## 2. Mechanistic Bridge
-   **Sensor:** PIEZO2 channels in DRG neurons detect mechanical strain (stretch/compression) in paraspinal tissues.
-   **Pathway:** Proprioceptive signals are transmitted to the spinal cord and brainstem, closing a feedback loop that adjusts paraspinal muscle tone.
-   **Effect:** This feedback creates a "righting reflex" at the level of individual functional spinal units, resisting the passive gravitational tendency to collapse or buckle.
