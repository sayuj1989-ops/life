# Evidence Note: Spinal Alignment as Active Inference

**Date:** 2025-02-20
**Topic:** Active Inference / Proprioceptive Gain / Geometric Hallucinations
**References:** Adams, Shipp & Friston (2013); Proske & Gandevia (2012)

## 1. The Claim
The vertebrate spine maintains its anti-gravity geometry not merely through mechanical stiffness, but through **Active Inference**, where descending motor commands function as "proprioceptive predictions" ($\mu$) and local muscle tone represents the minimization of sensory prediction error ($\varepsilon$). In this framework, scoliosis is a form of **"Geometric Hallucination"** (False Inference), where the system accepts a curved spinal configuration as the "correct" prior to explain away persistent, unresolvable prediction errors (e.g., from vestibular-proprioceptive mismatch).

## 2. Mechanism
*   **Precision Weighting ($\Pi$):** The gain of the gamma-motor loop ($\gamma$) encodes the *precision* (reliability) of proprioceptive afferents. High gain implies high confidence in the local geometry; low gain implies reliance on top-down priors (Gravity).
*   **Sensory Attenuation:** During voluntary movement, proprioceptive prediction errors are attenuated (ignored). In static posture, they must be minimized by reflex arcs (action).
*   **The "Double-Bind":** If Vestibular (Gravity) and Proprioceptive (Spine) signals conflict—e.g., due to microgravity unloading or asymmetric growth—the system must reduce the precision of one. If it reduces Vestibular precision, it "hallucinates" a new vertical, driving the spine to curve to match a false prediction.

## 3. Relevance to Counter-Curvature
This supports the **"Biological Counter-Curvature"** hypothesis by providing the *control theory* mechanism for how the "Information" term ($I$) in the energy functional interacts with the "Elastic" term ($E$).
*   The "Counter-Curvature" is the *Top-Down Prediction* (The Prior).
*   Scoliosis occurs when the *Bottom-Up Error* (The Data) overwhelms the Prior, or when the Prior is corrupted by noisy Vestibular gain.

## 4. Open Question & Proposed Test
**Question:** Can we induce scoliotic curvature in a healthy system solely by manipulating the *precision* of the vestibular reference, creating a "phantom curve"?

**Proposed Test:**
*   **Protocol:** Apply Stochastic Vestibular Stimulation (SVS) to healthy subjects (or mice) to degrade vestibular precision (add noise).
*   **Measure:** Monitor spinal sway and paraspinal EMG gain (H-reflex).
*   **Prediction:** As vestibular precision drops, the system will over-increase proprioceptive gain ($\gamma$) to compensate, leading to "rigidity" and susceptibility to "buckling" (Bastien instability) from minor proprioceptive noise.
