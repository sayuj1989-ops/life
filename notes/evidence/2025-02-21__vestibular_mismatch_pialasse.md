## Claim
Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) is characterized by a specific deficit in processing **low-frequency vestibular signals** (gravity), leading the central nervous system to **down-weight** the vestibular reference ($\gamma_V \to 0$) and **up-weight** short-range proprioceptive cues ($\gamma_P \uparrow$). This "Sensory Re-weighting" creates a reliance on internal geometric coherence over external gravitational alignment.

## Mechanism
*   **Vestibular Gain Loss:** Pialasse et al. (2015) demonstrated that AIS patients exhibit a reduced Center of Pressure (CoP) sway response to Galvanic Vestibular Stimulation (GVS) compared to controls. Since GVS directly stimulates the vestibular afferents (mimicking a gravity shift), a reduced output implies a reduced **Feedback Gain** ($\gamma_V$).
*   **Sensory Substitution:** According to Peterka's (2002) model, when one sensory channel acts "noisy" or unreliable, the CNS re-weights its reliance to others. In AIS, the noise in the otolith/semicircular canal signal forces a shift toward proprioception ("dead reckoning").
*   **Phantom Straightness:** Without a strong Gravity Vector to prune the integration drift, proprioceptive errors accumulate. The spine perceives itself as "straight" (proprioceptively coherent) even while buckling gravitationally.

## Relevance to Spinal Growth & Scoliosis
*   **The "Vestibular Precision Ratio" ($\Omega_{VP}$):** This evidence directly supports our theoretical construct $\Omega_{VP} = \pi_V / \pi_P$. In AIS, $\pi_V$ (vestibular precision) drops, causing the organism to ignore the Generative Model of verticality.
*   **Active Inference:** The curvature is not a mechanical failure but an *optimal* minimization of Free Energy given the biased sensory weights. The brain minimizes the "surprise" of proprioceptive mismatch by allowing the spine to curve, effectively "hallucinating" a new posture.

## Support for Counter-Curvature Hypothesis
This validates the idea that **Counter-Curvature ($U_{CC}$)** is an active, information-driven process. The failure is not in the muscle's ability to pull, but in the controller's definition of "Up."
