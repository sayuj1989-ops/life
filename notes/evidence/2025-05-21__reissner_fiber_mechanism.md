# Evidence Note: Reissner Fiber Tension as an Active Geometric Information Channel

## Claim
The Reissner Fiber (RF) functions as a continuous, tension-bearing "string" within the central canal that actively maintains the straightness of the spinal axis (or a specific curvature) by transducing mechanical signals (CSF flow/tension) to Contacting Neurons (CSF-cNs), which in turn regulate paraspinal muscle tone via Urotensin (URP1/2) signaling. Loss of this mechanical "guide wire" leads to buckling (scoliosis).

## Mechanism
1.  **Reissner Fiber Assembly:** Cilia beating in the central canal is required to aggregate SCO-spondin into a continuous fiber (RF) (Cantaut-Belarif et al., 2018).
2.  **Mechanotransduction:** The RF is under tension and physically contacts the cilia of CSF-contacting neurons (CSF-cNs) (Orts-Del'Immagine et al., 2020).
3.  **Signaling:** Mechanical tension/contact triggers CSF-cNs to release Urotensin II-related peptides (Urp1/Urp2).
4.  **Effector:** Urp1/2 signal to somites/muscles to maintain symmetric tone.
5.  **Failure Mode:** Disassembly of the RF (e.g., *scospondin* mutants) removes this tension signal, downregulating Urp1/2, leading to asymmetric muscle forces and 3D spinal buckling (Troutwine et al., 2020; Rose et al., 2020).

## Relevance to Counter-Curvature
This provides a biological embodiment of the "Active Information Field" $I(s)$ described in the counter-curvature formalism.
*   The "straight" state is not a passive energy minimum but an *active stationary state* maintained by continuous energy consumption (ciliary beating) and information transmission (RF tension).
*   The RF acts as a physical instantiation of a "non-local" interaction—it connects the head (SCO) to the tail, integrating geometry across the entire length.
*   Gravity adaptation: In microgravity, CSF hydrodynamics change. If RF tension is flow-dependent or gravity-dependent (buoyancy), its signal would be corrupted, potentially explaining "Spaceflight Associated Neuro-Ocular Syndrome" (SANS) spinal links or general spinal elongation/flattening.

## Open Question & Proposed Test
**Question:** Does the RF tension/signaling system calibrate the "target curvature" (S-shape vs Straight)?
**Hypothesis:** The RF system acts as the "Zero-Point" reference. In humans, the target is non-zero (S-shape).
**Test:** Correlate CSF flow rates and URP levels with spinal curvature in microgravity vs. 1G analogs. Specifically, test if *hyper-activation* of Urp1 (mimicking high tension) can rescue curvature in unloading models.
