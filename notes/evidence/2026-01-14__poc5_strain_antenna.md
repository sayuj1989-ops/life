# Evidence Note: POC5 as a Gravitational Strain Antenna

## 1. The Claim
The centriolar protein **POC5**, identified in our AlphaFold Counter-Curvature (AFCC) analysis as a "High-Anisotropy Strain Integrator" (Cluster 2/0), acts as a molecular lever arm within the cilium. Its truncation, associated with Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS), disrupts this "antenna" function, decoupling the cellular perception of gravity from the geometric response required to maintain spinal straightness.

## 2. Mechanism
POC5 is essential for the elongation of the distal portion of centrioles.
- **AFCC Data**: POC5 exhibits extreme structural anisotropy (Score ~24.7), suggesting it forms a rigid, rod-like scaffold.
- **Pathology**: Mutations found in AIS patients (e.g., p.A429V) often lead to truncated or misfolded proteins that cannot assemble into this long, rigid structure.
- **Link**: The cilium functions as a flow and gravity sensor (statocyst analog). Loss of the POC5 "spine" within the cilium renders it flaccid or insensitive to subtle gravitational shear, breaking the proprioceptive loop.

## 3. Relevance to Spinal Growth & Scoliosis
The "Biological Counter-Curvature" theory posits that the spine must actively grow *against* gravity. This requires a high-fidelity sensor. If paraspinal muscle or osteoblast cilia act as these sensors, their stiffness is critical. POC5 provides this stiffness. Its failure leads to a "noisy" gravity signal, causing the control system to drift into the error state we call scoliosis (a buckling mode).

## 4. Support for Counter-Curvature
This finding strongly supports the "Information-First" view of spinal geometry. The defect is not initially in the bone material properties, but in the *sensing* apparatus. The body grows crooked because it *perceives* gravity incorrectly (or not at all) due to the broken antenna.

## 5. Open Question & Proposed Test
*Question*: Does the POC5-containing centriole align with the gravity vector in non-ciliated cells (like myoblasts)?
*Test*: Cultivate human paraspinal myoblasts in 3D hydrogels under normal and clinorotation (simulated microgravity) conditions. Stain for POC5.
- **Prediction**: In 1g, POC5 rods will show a preferred orientation relative to gravity. In microgravity, this alignment will be random.
