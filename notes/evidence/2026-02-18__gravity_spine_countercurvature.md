# Evidence Note: The Piezo1-Primary Cilia 'Stress-Lock' Axis

**Date:** 2026-02-18
**Topic:** Mechanotransduction, Primary Cilia, Growth Plate Ossification
**Citation:** Chen et al. (2025) *PIEZO1-Primary Cilia Axis Mediates Compressive Stress-Induced Growth Plate Degeneration and Ossification in Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis*. JOR Spine.

## Claim
The conversion of compressive stress (e.g., gravitational loading) into premature vertebral ossification is explicitly mediated by a **Piezo1-Primary Cilia Axis**. Dysfunction of this axis—specifically its over-activation under stress—locks the growth plate into a degenerative, osteogenic fate, preventing the adaptive remodeling required for counter-curvature maintenance.

## Mechanism
Chen et al. (2025) demonstrate a direct molecular pathway:
1.  **Colocalization:** Piezo1 mechanochannels physically colocalize with Primary Cilia (IFT88) in growth plate chondrocytes.
2.  **Stress Activation:** Compressive stress (100 kPa) upregulates Piezo1 expression.
3.  **Ciliary Signaling:** Piezo1 activation drives Ca2+ influx which, dependent on IFT88 integrity, triggers the downregulation of chondrogenic markers (**COL2A1**, **ACAN**) and upregulation of osteogenic markers (**RUNX2**).
4.  **Rescue:** Disrupting the cilium (via IFT88 knockdown or Chloral Hydrate) or blocking Piezo1 prevents this stress-induced ossification, maintaining the chondrocyte phenotype even under load.

## Relevance to Counter-Curvature
This finding provides the "Hardware Spec" for the **Sensory Mismatch** hypothesis in the Counter-Curvature framework:
*   **Maladaptive Gain:** The spine's failure in scoliosis is not just "weakness" but an *active*, erroneous response to gravity. The Piezo1-Cilia axis acts as a "Stress-Lock" mechanism. Under asymmetric load, it triggers premature hardening (ossification) of the concave side, structurally locking in the curve.
*   **Sensory Mismatch:** The fact that *removing* the sensor (Cilia/IFT88) rescues the phenotype suggests that the pathology is driven by *too much* (or incorrect) information integration, not too little. In the "Biological Counter-Curvature" model, this represents a failure of the **Gain Control** ($\gamma$) parameter—the system fails to downweight the error signal from the compressed side, instead amplifying it into a permanent structural defect.

## Open Question
Does the **circadian clock** regulate the sensitivity of this Piezo1-Cilia axis? Given that primary cilia length is clock-controlled (Miyoshi et al., 2024), is there a "vulnerable phase" of the day where gravitational compression is more likely to trigger this ossification cascade?

## Proposed Test
**Hypothesis:** If the Piezo1-Cilia axis functions as a maladaptive "Stress-Lock," then pharmacological blockade of Piezo1 (e.g., GsMTx4) or ciliary disassembly during the "active phase" (loaded) of the circadian cycle will prevent vertebral wedging in scoliotic models.
