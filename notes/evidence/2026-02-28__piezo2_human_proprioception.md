# Evidence Note: Piezo2 Proprioceptive Failure Drives Human Scoliosis

## 1. Insight Summary
**The Insight:** Loss-of-function mutations in *PIEZO2* in humans result in profound proprioceptive deficits (sensory ataxia) and severe, progressive scoliosis, confirming the critical role of this mechanosensor in maintaining spinal geometry (Chesler et al., 2016; Assaraf et al., 2020).

**The Claim:** Piezo2 acts as the primary "Vector Sensor" for the spinal proprioceptive control loop. Its failure removes the error signal ($S_{proprio}$) required to correct deviations from the reference curvature, leading to a "blind" system that drifts into instability (scoliosis) under the influence of gravity.

## 2. Mechanism & Connection
*   **Pathway:** Mechanical Strain $\rightarrow$ Piezo2 (DRG Neurons) $\rightarrow$ Proprioceptive Signal $\rightarrow$ Spinal Cord $\rightarrow$ Paraspinal Muscle Tone $\rightarrow$ Alignment.
*   **Thermodynamic Link:** Piezo2 is a massive, homotrimeric channel with high anisotropy (~19.7) and extensive transmembrane domains. According to the "Thermodynamic Cost of Information" framework, such a sensor is metabolically expensive to maintain and fold.
*   **Relevance to Counter-Curvature:** This provides the definitive "Supply-Side" constraint. The "Biological Counter-Curvature" theory posits that spinal alignment is an active, energy-consuming process ($P_{counter}$). Piezo2 is the specific molecular component that "supplies" the geometric information. Its loss (genetic or metabolic) breaks the feedback loop, allowing the "Demand" (gravity/growth) to overwhelm the system.

## 3. Support/Constraints
*   **Supports:**
    *   **Proprioceptive Supply Hypothesis:** Directly links sensory loss to geometric collapse.
    *   **Vector-Scalar Mismatch:** Piezo2 is uniquely suited for vector sensing (directional strain) due to its extended structure, unlike scalar sensors (Piezo1/Caveolae).
    *   **Anisotropic Cost:** The high structural complexity of Piezo2 makes it a likely candidate for failure during "Metabolic Crunch" periods (e.g., rapid growth), explaining why AIS is idiopathic and adolescence-onset.
*   **Constraints:**
    *   The Chesler study involves *complete* loss of function. It remains to be seen if *partial* downregulation (e.g., 20-30% reduction due to energy deficits) is sufficient to trigger the instability in otherwise genetically normal individuals.

## 4. Open Questions & Proposed Test
**Open Question:** Does the "Energy Deficit Window" during adolescent growth cause a transient, functional downregulation of Piezo2 activity (due to ATP gating or folding stress) that mimics the genetic loss-of-function phenotype?

**Proposed Test:**
*   **Hypothesis:** "The Piezo2 Vulnerability Hypothesis" (see Register).
*   **Experiment:** Correlate quantitative proprioceptive acuity (e.g., joint position matching error) with:
    1.  Spinal growth velocity (cm/year).
    2.  Resting Metabolic Rate (RMR).
    3.  Serum markers of metabolic stress (e.g., Lactate/Pyruvate ratio).
    in a cohort of AIS patients vs. healthy controls.
*   **Prediction:** AIS patients will show a transient dip in proprioceptive acuity ("Sensory Brownout") specifically during the peak growth spurt, correlating with the onset of curvature.
