# Evidence Note: Lymphatic Baroreflex Hysteresis (Choi et al., 2024)

## Claim
Lymphatic valve failure in microgravity is not merely a passive response to fluid redistribution, but a failure of the **Piezo1-mediated lymphatic baroreflex**. Specifically, this reflex exhibits **hysteresis**: it requires a critical hydrostatic pressure head to "latch" the valves closed. In the absence of gravity ($P = \rho g h \to 0$), the valves fail open, allowing retrograde reflux and inflammatory edema.

## Mechanism
**Piezo1** functions as a high-threshold baroreceptor in lymphatic endothelial cells (LECs).
1.  **Gravity Load (1G):** Hydrostatic pressure activates Piezo1.
2.  **Calcium Spike:** Piezo1 opening triggers local Ca2+ influx.
3.  **Valve Closure:** Ca2+ drives actomyosin contraction and valve leaflet apposition.
4.  **Hysteresis:** The system is bistable; once closed, lower pressure is needed to maintain closure than to initiate it.
5.  **Microgravity (0G):** Pressure drops below the initiation threshold. Valves open. Reflux occurs.

## Relevance to Scoliosis
This provides the **mechanistic trigger** for the "Hydraulic Hypothesis" of microgravity-induced scoliosis:
*   **Edema:** Retrograde flow causes spinal cord edema.
*   **Convective Shutdown:** Edema increases interstitial pressure, collapsing the pressure gradient required for solute transport into the avascular IVD.
*   **Metabolic Buckling:** The IVD starves (Energy Deficit), causing ECM degradation and loss of stiffness ($EI$), leading to buckling under residual muscle tone.

## Support for Counter-Curvature
This supports the **Thermodynamic Morphoelasticity** framework by identifying the specific *thermodynamic cost* of maintaining spinal straightness: the metabolic energy required to maintain the hydrostatic pressure gradient against gravity. Gravity provides the "free" pressure head; without it, the body must expend metabolic energy (pumping) to compensate. If it cannot ($Supply < Demand$), the structure buckles.
