# Daily Evidence Note: Metabolic Proteins AFCC Expansion

**Date:** 2026-03-02
**Topic:** Addition of metabolic and regulatory supply proteins to the AlphaFold Counter-Curvature (AFCC) pipeline.

## Overview

We expanded the candidate pool analyzed by the AFCC pipeline to include 6 additional proteins tightly linked to thermodynamic supply and cytoskeletal tone:
- **PPARGC1A** (Q9UBK2): Mitochondrial biogenesis master regulator; determines energy SUPPLY capacity.
- **IGF1R** (P08069): Insulin-like growth factor 1 receptor; mediates growth plate signaling under load.
- **GHR** (P10912): Growth hormone receptor; master regulator of adolescent growth spurt rate.
- **ARNTL** (O00327): BMAL1; circadian clock TF in intervertebral disc regulating metabolism.
- **DMD** (P11532): Connects cytoskeleton to ECM (costamere), protecting muscle from contraction-induced mechanical damage and gravity load.
- **MYLK** (Q15746): Regulates smooth/non-muscle motility and contractility via myosin phosphorylation.

## Metrics Summary

The AFCC pipeline yielded the following key metrics for these candidates:
- **GHR** showed the highest anisotropy (5.13), indicating a highly fibrous/extended morphology.
- **ARNTL** also displayed a fibrous/extended morphology with an anisotropy index of 3.32.
- **PPARGC1A** presented an intermediate morphology (anisotropy 2.19) with significant disorder (low structural confidence, mean pLDDT 52.7), which is common for regulatory proteins with extensive intrinsically disordered regions (IDRs).
- **IGF1R**, **DMD**, and **MYLK** yielded more globular morphologies with lower anisotropy indices (1.31-1.46). IGF1R and DMD showed higher overall structural confidence (pLDDT ~76-78).

## Implications for the Biological Counter-Curvature Framework

The addition of these proteins maps directly to the thermodynamic cost parameters of our Biological Counter-Curvature framework:
- By characterizing the physical properties of primary metabolic regulators (PPARGC1A, ARNTL, GHR, IGF1R), we strengthen our understanding of the 'energy supply bottleneck' during the adolescent growth spurt, a period closely linked to AIS curve progression.
- The extended morphology of **GHR** and **ARNTL** could suggest physical coupling or sensitivity to mechanical strain, potentially integrating mechanosensation directly into growth and circadian regulation.
- **DMD** and **MYLK** anchor the mechanical response side, highlighting the necessity of robust muscle tone maintenance and contractility against gravity, failure of which leads to spinal collapse.
