# Claim
Metabolic and regulatory supply proteins (PPARGC1A, IGF1R, GHR, ARNTL, DMD, MYLK) integral to the Biological Counter-Curvature framework exhibit distinct structural morphologies that map directly to their roles as flexible mechanosensors or stable functional effectors.

# Mechanism (genes/proteins/pathways)
The AlphaFold Counter-Curvature pipeline identifies GHR and ARNTL as highly extended, fibrous structures (Anisotropy 5.13 and 3.32, respectively), suggesting physical strain coupling. PPARGC1A exhibits significant intrinsic disorder (pLDDT 52.7) and intermediate anisotropy (2.19), functioning as a flexible regulatory hub for mitochondrial biogenesis. IGF1R, DMD, and MYLK present stable, globular architectures (Anisotropy 1.31-1.46) with higher structural confidence, consistent with robust roles as metabolic effectors and cytomechanical anchors.

# Why it matters for S-shaped spinal growth / scoliosis
The adolescent growth spurt introduces an energy deficit bottleneck where mechanosensory demand outpaces metabolic supply. The physical properties of primary metabolic regulators (GHR, PPARGC1A) directly influence the rate of spinal elongation and the available energy supply, while structural anchors like DMD maintain vital postural muscle tone against gravity; failure across these nodes drives structural collapse and curvature progression.

# How it constrains or supports the counter-curvature hypothesis
The presence of highly extended, low-confidence geometries in primary metabolic regulators (GHR, ARNTL) supports the hypothesis that these molecules can act directly as strain-coupled mechanical antennas, directly integrating mechanical loading cues into growth rate and circadian metabolic timing, thus linking gravity resistance to energetic supply pathways.
