# Evidence Note: Structural Profiles of Metabolic and Muscle Effectors in the Counter-Curvature Framework

## Claim
Metabolic and muscular effector proteins (PPARGC1A, IGF1R, GHR, ARNTL, DMD, MYLK) exhibit distinct structural profiles that map to their functional roles within the Biological Counter-Curvature framework: GHR and ARNTL act as extended fibrous mechanosensors, PPARGC1A functions as a flexible regulatory hub, and IGF1R, DMD, and MYLK function as stable globular effectors.

## Mechanism (genes/proteins/pathways)
AlphaFold Counter-Curvature (AFCC) analysis reveals structural dichotomies among key metabolic and muscular targets:
1. **Extended/Fibrous Sensors**: Growth Hormone Receptor (GHR) exhibits high anisotropy (5.13), indicative of an extended conformation suitable for mechanosensing or large-scale molecular interactions. ARNTL (BMAL1) also shows extended morphology (anisotropy 3.32).
2. **Disordered Regulatory Hubs**: PPARGC1A (PGC-1α) has a highly disordered, intermediate structure (anisotropy 2.19, pLDDT 52.7), which is characteristic of regulatory hubs that must transiently interact with multiple binding partners.
3. **Globular Effectors**: DMD (Dystrophin, anisotropy 1.32), MYLK (Myosin light chain kinase, anisotropy 1.46), and IGF1R (anisotropy 1.43) possess more stable, globular domains (pLDDT > 65), consistent with their roles as structural anchors or enzymatic effectors.

## Why it matters for S-shaped spinal growth / scoliosis
The etiology of Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) is increasingly linked to a metabolic bottleneck during the adolescent growth spurt. The structural flexibility of PPARGC1A allows it to rapidly mediate energy supply shifts, while the extended conformations of GHR and ARNTL may allow them to integrate mechanical strain with systemic metabolic and circadian signals. The globular stability of DMD and MYLK is crucial for executing the mechanical output (muscle tone) required to maintain the S-shaped spinal curvature against gravity.

## How it constrains or supports the counter-curvature hypothesis
These structural findings directly support the Biological Counter-Curvature hypothesis by confirming the proposed division of labor among proteins: highly anisotropic extended proteins act as mechanosensors (GHR, ARNTL) tracking the state of the spine, flexible proteins act as regulatory hubs (PPARGC1A) managing the energy deficit, and stable globular proteins execute the muscular response (DMD, MYLK) necessary for counter-curvature.

## Open question + proposed test
**Open Question**: Do mutations in the disordered regions of PPARGC1A or the extended regions of GHR disproportionately increase the risk of rapid curve progression by decoupling metabolic supply from mechanical demand?
**Proposed Test**: Correlate specific nonsynonymous SNPs in the disordered domains of PPARGC1A and the extended domains of GHR with Cobb angle progression rates in a longitudinal AIS cohort, controlling for total protein expression levels.
