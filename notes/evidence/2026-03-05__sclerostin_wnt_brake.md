# Evidence Note: Sclerostin as the Gravity-Brake in Spinal Buckling

## The Claim
Sclerostin (SOST), an osteocyte-secreted Wnt inhibitor, functions as a "Gravity-Brake" on spinal bone formation. Its physiologic role is to prevent excessive bone growth in the absence of mechanical load. However, in states of **Mechanoblindness** (e.g., AIS or Piezo1 dysfunction) or **Unloading** (Microgravity), Sclerostin is pathologically upregulated, actively suppressing osteogenesis and leading to the "Material-First" collapse of spinal stiffness ($EI$) that precedes geometric buckling.

## The Mechanism
1.  **Gravity Load**: Normally sensed by osteocytes via fluid shear stress (Piezo1) and integrin signaling.
2.  **Transduction**: Mechanical load actively **downregulates** *SOST* expression.
3.  **Effector**: Low SOST allows Wnt/$\beta$-catenin signaling to proceed, driving osteoblast differentiation and bone formation.
4.  **Failure Mode**: In the absence of load (or the *perception* of load), *SOST* levels rise unchecked.
    - High SOST $\rightarrow$ Blocked Wnt $\rightarrow$ Osteopenia/Osteoporosis.
    - In AIS, this creates a **"False Microgravity"** state where the spine remodels as if it were unloaded, despite being under normal gravity.

## Relevance to Counter-Curvature
This mechanism provides the molecular link between the "Information Phase" (sensing) and the "Thermodynamic Phase" (buckling). The spine requires constant "Energy Injection" (Load) to suppress the "Entropy Generator" (Sclerostin). If the sensor fails (Information loss), the system defaults to the high-entropy state (Osteopenia/Buckling). This resolves the paradox of why AIS patients have lower bone density despite normal loading: their sensors are not telling the nucleus to turn off the brake.

## Evidence
- **Vasiliadis et al. (2021)**: Identified Sclerostin upregulation as a key driver of osteopenia in AIS.
- **Chen et al. (2025)**: Demonstrated MMP-mediated degradation in microgravity, which parallels the osteopenic remodeling seen here.
- **Li et al. (2023)**: Showed Piezo1 loss leads to osteopenia; this likely proceeds via the Sclerostin-Wnt axis.

## Open Question & Proposed Test
**Does pharmacological inhibition of Sclerostin rescue spinal alignment in microgravity?**
If Sclerostin is the primary effector of unloading-induced atrophy, then blocking it (e.g., with Romosozumab) should preserve spinal stiffness and prevent buckling even in the absence of gravity.

**Hypothesis:**
> If microgravity or AIS-associated mechanoblindness leads to pathologic upregulation of Sclerostin (SOST), then pharmacological inhibition of Sclerostin (e.g., Romosozumab) will rescue bone density and prevent spinal curvature progression in unloading models.
