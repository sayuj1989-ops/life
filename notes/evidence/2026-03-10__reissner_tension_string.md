# Evidence Note: Reissner Fiber as a Tensioned Resonator

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Source:** Bellegarda et al. (2023), *eLife*
**Tags:** #mechanotransduction #reissner-fiber #proprioception #counter-curvature

## Claim
The Reissner Fiber (RF) is not a passive aggregate but a dynamic, tensioned "string" suspended in the cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) that oscillates dorsoventrally and interacts physically with mechanosensory cilia (CSF-cNs and ependymal radial glia).

## Mechanism
- **Tension Gradient:** RF exhibits heterogeneous tension, highest in the rostral spine and decreasing caudally. This tension is maintained likely by rostral CSF flow and ciliary beating.
- **Oscillation:** The fiber behaves like a soft elastic polymer (Young's modulus ~1 MPa) and oscillates spontaneously in vivo (frequency > 20 Hz, amplitude ~100 nm).
- **Sensory Coupling:** RF ablation reduces the spontaneous calcium activity of ventral CSF-cNs, confirming it acts as a "gain enhancer" or reference signal for these proprioceptors.
- **Ciliary Interaction:** Motile cilia "brush" against the RF, transferring kinetic energy into the fiber (driving oscillation) and potentially sensing its tension state.

## Relevance to Counter-Curvature
This provides the "missing link" for the **Biological Counter-Curvature** hypothesis:
1.  **The Reference Frame:** The spine has a literal "plumb line" (RF) under tension. Gravity/posture deviations would detune this string (changing tension/frequency).
2.  **The Gradient:** The observed rostral-to-caudal tension gradient matches the "Counter-Curvature" prediction where the anti-gravity drive must be calibrated against the gravity vector (which is uniform, but the moment arm varies).
3.  **The Noise:** Loss of RF tension (slack) removes the reference, increasing sensory noise (as seen in RF ablation). This aligns with the "Exploding Gradient" failure mode where noise overwhelms the controller.
