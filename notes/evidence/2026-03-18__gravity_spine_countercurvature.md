# Evidence Note: PIEZO2 and Scoliotic Buckling

**Claim**: Biallelic loss of function of the PIEZO2 mechanosensitive ion channel causes a complex clinical syndrome encompassing profound proprioceptive deficits, muscular atrophy, and progressive scoliosis.

**Mechanism (genes/proteins/pathways)**: PIEZO2 is the principal mechanotransduction channel for proprioception, expressed heavily in dorsal root ganglia and muscle spindles. Loss of PIEZO2 eliminates the rapid mechanosensory feedback necessary for closed-loop motor control and tone maintenance.

**Why it matters for S-shaped spinal growth / scoliosis**: The absence of proprioception means the neuromuscular system cannot detect postural deviations from the vertical gravity vector. Without this real-time geometric error signal, the paraspinal muscles cannot exert corrective counter-bending forces, leading to uncontrolled scoliotic buckling.

**How it constrains or supports the counter-curvature hypothesis**: This provides direct human genetic evidence supporting the Bioelectric Counter-Curvature hypothesis. It confirms that the 'Information Bus' relies critically on PIEZO2 to continuously compute and apply the necessary counter-curvature torque ($P_{counter}$) against gravity.

**Open question + proposed test**: Is the scoliotic curve in PIEZO2-deficient patients solely driven by passive biomechanical buckling, or does the lack of proprioception trigger an aberrant compensatory reflex that actively worsens the curve? Proposed test: Perform detailed paraspinal electromyography (EMG) mapping in PIEZO2-null mice during the onset of scoliotic curvature to determine if muscle activation is silent (passive buckling) or asymmetrically hyperactive (aberrant reflex).
