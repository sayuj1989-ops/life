# Evidence Note: The ROS-METTL3-ESR1 Axis in Spinal Asymmetry

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Topic:** Mitochondrial Metabolism / Epigenetics / Muscle Asymmetry
**Source:** Shao et al. (2025), *Research Square*; Sun et al. (2023), *J. Transl. Med.*

## Key Finding
Shao et al. (2025) demonstrate that **Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS)** accumulation in concave paraspinal muscle progenitor cells (PMPCs) triggers an epigenetic cascade that drives asymmetric muscle atrophy in Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS). Specifically, high ROS levels upregulate the m6A methyltransferase **METTL3**, which methylates the mRNA of **Estrogen Receptor 1 (ESR1)**. This methylation suppresses ESR1 expression, impairing muscle differentiation and growth on the concave side.

Complementary work by Sun et al. (2023) confirms that **functional unloading (denervation)** fundamentally alters the m6A methylation landscape in skeletal muscle, driving atrophy via the ubiquitin-proteasome pathway.

## Mechanistic Bridge
This finding provides a direct molecular link between **metabolic stress** (ROS) and **geometric asymmetry** (Muscle Imbalance).
1.  **Gravity/Load Loss**: Microgravity or localized unloading causes mitochondrial dysfunction and ROS accumulation (well-established).
2.  **Epigenetic Switch**: ROS $\uparrow$ $\to$ METTL3 $\uparrow$.
3.  **Target Suppression**: METTL3 methylates *ESR1* mRNA, promoting its degradation.
4.  **Structural Failure**: Reduced ESR1 impairs myogenic differentiation, leading to the fatty infiltration and atrophy characteristic of the concave side (and spaceflight).

## Relevance to Counter-Curvature
The "Biological Counter-Curvature" theory posits that active muscle torque ($\chi_M$) must be maintained symmetrically to resist gravitational buckling. This evidence suggests that $\chi_M$ is not just "turned off" by unloading, but actively suppressed via a metabolic-epigenetic loop. If microgravity induces systemic ROS (a known phenomenon), it may trigger this METTL3 pathway globally, reducing $\chi_M$ below the critical threshold ($\chi_{crit}$) required for stability, thus explaining the rapid spinal lengthening and "flattening" (loss of lordosis) seen in astronauts.

## Testable Prediction
If this mechanism is universal to unloading:
1.  **Prediction**: METTL3 inhibitors (e.g., STM2457) should prevent paraspinal muscle atrophy in hindlimb-suspended mice, preserving the "active information" ($\chi_M$) and preventing spinal straightening.
2.  **Prediction**: Space-flown muscle tissue should show elevated METTL3 and reduced ESR1 levels compared to ground controls.
