# Evidence Note: PIEZO2 Mechanotransduction and Scoliosis

**Claim:** Loss of proprioception-related PIEZO2 mechanotransduction causes profound spinal curvature abnormalities, specifically scoliosis.

**Mechanism:** PIEZO2 is the principal high-threshold mechanosensitive ion channel responsible for translating physical forces (like stretch or tension) into electrical signals in proprioceptive neurons. Biallelic loss-of-function mutations eliminate this signaling, resulting in an inability to sense body position and mechanical load.

**Why it matters for S-shaped spinal growth / scoliosis:** The spine requires continuous error-correction to maintain an upright, S-shaped posture against gravity. PIEZO2 provides the critical feedback loop that controls active muscle tone in the paraspinal musculature. Without it, the spinal column lacks the dynamic support needed to prevent structural buckling and lateral deviation.

**How it constrains or supports the counter-curvature hypothesis:** This strongly supports the "biological counter-curvature of spacetime" framing. It proves that the spine's shape is not a static architectural feature but a dynamic equilibrium actively maintained by mechanosensory feedback. Gravity ($g$) is the input signal; PIEZO2 is the sensor. When the sensor fails, the active counter-curvature cannot be computed, and the spine succumbs to passive mechanical buckling (scoliosis).

**Open question + proposed test:** Does PIEZO2 downregulation precede curvature onset in microgravity environments?
*Proposed Test:* Time-course transcriptomics and protein quantification of PIEZO2 in the DRG and paraspinal muscles of mice subjected to 30-day hindlimb suspension, correlated with longitudinal micro-CT imaging of spinal curvature.
