## Claim
The PIEZO1-primary cilia axis mediates compressive stress-induced growth plate degeneration and ossification, directly linking asymmetric mechanical loading to the structural pathogenesis of adolescent idiopathic scoliosis (AIS).

## Mechanism (genes/proteins/pathways)
In growth plate chondrocytes, PIEZO1 co-localizes with primary cilia (marked by acetylated α-tubulin). Under compressive stress (simulating concave-side loading in scoliosis), PIEZO1 is upregulated, which subsequently downregulates chondrocyte markers (e.g., COL2A1, ACAN) and upregulates osteogenic markers (e.g., RUNX2). Silencing PIEZO1 or disrupting primary cilia (e.g., via IFT88 knockdown/knockout or chloral hydrate) mitigates stress-induced cartilage matrix loss and premature ossification. Thus, primary cilia act as the antenna, while PIEZO1 serves as the mechanosensitive transducer driving the chondrocyte-to-osteoblast phenotypic shift under chronic loading.

## Why it matters for S-shaped spinal growth / scoliosis
The Hueter-Volkmann law states that increased compression slows growth, while tension accelerates it—a core principle of spinal deformity progression. This study provides the exact molecular basis for this law in the spine. Excessive concave loading activates the PIEZO1-cilia axis, causing premature ossification and halting local growth, which structurally locks in the deformity and perpetuates the S-shape curvature.

## How it constrains or supports the counter-curvature hypothesis
This strongly supports the biological counter-curvature hypothesis. It demonstrates that the macroscopic spine curve is a direct geometric manifestation of micro-scale cellular decisions. If local gravity-induced stresses exceed a threshold, mechanosensory pathways (PIEZO1/cilia) act as "brakes" on growth (premature ossification). The counter-curvature is essentially an emergent map of these local mechanotransduction gradients, where the tissue attempts to minimize stress but instead enters a runaway positive feedback loop when physiological thresholds are breached.

## Open question + proposed test
Open Question: Can targeted modulation of the PIEZO1-cilia axis (e.g., via Yoda1 or focal antagonists) reverse early-stage wedging before structural ossification completes?
Proposed Test: Apply asymmetric compression to murine tail models to induce curvature, then inject a PIEZO1 antagonist (GsMTx4) locally to the concave side to assess whether it prevents premature ossification and mitigates curve progression.
