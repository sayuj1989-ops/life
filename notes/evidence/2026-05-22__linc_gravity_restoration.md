# Evidence Note: LINC Complex Degradation in Microgravity and Rescue via LIV

**Date:** 2026-05-22
**Topic:** Gravity, Mechanotransduction, LINC Complex, Lamin A/C
**Reference:** Touchstone et al. (2019), *npj Microgravity*

## Claim
Simulated microgravity (sMG) actively degrades the nuclear-cytoskeletal coupling machinery, specifically downregulating **Lamin A/C** and **Sun-2** proteins. This degradation decouples the nucleus from mechanical cues, leading to reduced YAP nuclear entry and halted proliferation. Crucially, low-intensity vibration (LIV) (0.7g, 90Hz) can restore these structures and rescue proliferation, but only if the LINC complex remains functional.

## Mechanism
1.  **Microgravity Input:** Removal of the gravity vector (or randomization via clinostat) reduces basal cytoskeletal tension.
2.  **Structural Degradation:** In response to reduced tension, cells downregulate **Sun-2** (linker) and **Lamin A/C** (nuclear stiffness gauge).
3.  **Signaling Failure:** Loss of LINC/Lamin integrity prevents **YAP** from sensing mechanical signals (even if they exist), preventing its nuclear translocation.
4.  **Phenotype:** Reduced proliferation and "blindness" to mechanical cues (Vector Deficiency).
5.  **Rescue:** LIV provides a high-frequency surrogate mechanical signal that stimulates re-expression of Sun-2/Lamin A/C, restoring the information channel.

## Relevance to Counter-Curvature
This finding strongly supports the **Vector Deficiency** hypothesis: scoliosis or spinal flattening in microgravity isn't just "muscle atrophy" but a failure of the **mechanosensory apparatus itself** (the "Vector Sensors"). The cell loses its "antenna" (LINC/Lamin) for detecting the counter-curvature vector.
It also validates our focus on **Lamin A/C** (Cluster 0, High Anisotropy) as a key "gravity sensor" that is lost in unloading.
Most importantly, it suggests **LIV** as a specific "information therapy" to maintain the structural integrity of the sensor itself, not just to exercise the muscle.
