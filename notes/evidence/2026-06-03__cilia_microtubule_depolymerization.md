## 1. The Claim
Primary cilia do not merely "fail to sense" gravity in microgravity; they actively trigger a downstream cascade that depolymerizes the microtubule (MT) network. Crucially, removing the sensor (via IFT88 knockdown or chemical ablation) *prevents* this cytoskeletal collapse, effectively "blinding" the cell to the microgravity environment and preserving its structural integrity.

## 2. The Mechanism
Shi et al. (2020) demonstrated in rat calvarial osteoblasts (ROBs) that:
1.  **Simulated Microgravity (SMG)** causes gradual shortening and loss of primary cilia.
2.  However, *before* they disappear, the cilia signal a massive depolymerization of the cytoplasmic microtubule network.
3.  **Rescue by Ablation:** Knockdown of `IFT88` (preventing ciliogenesis) or chemical removal of cilia *blocked* the microgravity-induced MT depolymerization. The cells without cilia maintained their MT network in SMG.
4.  **Rescue by Stabilization:** Pharmacological stabilization of microtubules (e.g., Taxol) restored osteogenic differentiation even in SMG.

This implies the pathway is: `Microgravity -> Cilia -> MT Depolymerization -> Loss of Differentiation`.
