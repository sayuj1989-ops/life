# Evidence Note: Reissner Fiber as a Gravitational Tension Rod

**Date:** 2026-06-20
**Topic:** Reissner Fiber (RF) mechanics in microgravity
**Reference:** Sternberg et al. (2018), *Pkd2l1 is required for mechanoception...*

## Claim
The Reissner Fiber (RF) functions as a suspended "heavy string" (denser than CSF) that maintains tension via gravity and fluid flow. Pkd2l1-positive CSF-contacting neurons (CSF-cNs) sense this tension to regulate spinal straightness. In microgravity, the loss of the gravitational vector causes the RF to slacken or "float," mimicking the Pkd2l1 loss-of-function phenotype (scoliosis).

## Mechanism
*   **Reissner Fiber (RF):** A sub-commissural organ (SCO)-spondin polymer forming a continuous thread through the central canal.
*   **Sensor:** Pkd2l1 channels on CSF-cNs (ciliated neurons lining the canal).
*   **Signal:** The RF physically contacts the cilia of CSF-cNs. Tension in the RF (maintained by posterior flow + gravity) bends the cilia, keeping Pkd2l1 open (or closed, depending on polarity).
*   **Effect:** Pkd2l1 activity suppresses local motor neuron output, keeping the spine straight.

## Relevance to Counter-Curvature
This perfectly maps to our "Slack-Rod Mechanism". The RF is the ultimate "Tension Rod" (Cluster 0 equivalent on a macro scale).
*   **Gravity Dependence:** If the RF relies on density difference ($\rho_{RF} > \rho_{CSF}$) to maintain a ventral position or specific catenary curve, microgravity removes this bias.
*   **Scoliosis Driver:** Sternberg et al. (2018) showed that ablating Pkd2l1 causes 3D spinal curvature (scoliosis). If microgravity functionally ablates the *stimulus* (tension/contact) rather than the *sensor*, the outcome is the same.
