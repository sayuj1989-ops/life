# Evidence Note: Hypoxia Removal Rescues Spinal Alignment

**Date:** 2026-09-18
**Source:** Ugur et al., *Children (Basel)*, 2024. [DOI: 10.3390/children11091134]

## Claim
The removal of hypoxic factors (via adenoidectomy for sleep-disordered breathing) leads to a significant regression of scoliotic curvature (Cobb angle) in pediatric patients. Specifically, 83% of patients with initial Cobb angles ≥10° showed improvement post-surgery.

## Mechanism
Chronic hypoxia (due to airway obstruction) likely disrupts the metabolic homeostasis required for spinal maintenance. While the paper focuses on the clinical outcome, our framework suggests this operates via the **Frequency-Hypoxia Coupling** (Test AE):
1.  Hypoxia stabilizes **HIF-1alpha**.
2.  HIF-1alpha disrupts the circadian clock (BMAL1/CLOCK) via metabolic feedback (Adamovich et al., 2017).
3.  Clock disruption uncouples the "Metabolic Cost" sensing, preventing the spine from detecting or correcting its geometric error ("Spinal Jetlag").
4.  Restoring oxygenation (adenoidectomy) degrades HIF-1alpha, restores clock amplitude, and re-engages the counter-curvature maintenance loop.

## Relevance to Counter-Curvature
This supports the "Biological Counter-Curvature" hypothesis by framing scoliosis not just as a mechanical failure, but as a **metabolic failure** to maintain an energetically expensive anti-gravity posture.
-   **Energy Deficit:** Hypoxia mimics the "Energy Deficit Window" by reducing ATP availability or shunting metabolism to glycolysis, which may be insufficient for the high cost of counter-curvature ($P_{counter}$).
-   **Reversibility:** The fact that curves *regress* upon re-oxygenation implies the deformity is actively maintained (or passively drifting due to loss of maintenance) and can be corrected if the metabolic/informational constraint is lifted.
