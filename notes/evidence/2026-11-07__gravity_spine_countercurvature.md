# Evidence Note: Metabolic Control of the Reissner Fiber via DPY19L1L

**Date:** 2026-11-07
**Author:** Curvature Librarian
**Topic:** Reissner Fiber / Metabolic Glycosylation / Spinal Straightening

## 1. The Insight
Tian et al. (2025, *Science Advances*) demonstrate that the C-mannosyltransferase **DPY19L1L** is essential for the assembly of the **Reissner Fiber (RF)** in zebrafish. Loss of DPY19L1L leads to severe idiopathic scoliosis-like curvature despite normal cilia motility. The mechanism involves the specific C-mannosylation of thrombospondin type 1 repeats (TSRs) on **SCO-spondin**, the main structural component of the RF.

## 2. Mechanism & Connection
*   **Gene:** *DPY19L1L* (C-mannosyltransferase)
*   **Target:** SCO-spondin (TSR domains)
*   **Effect:** Proper folding and polymerization of SCO-spondin into the Reissner Fiber.
*   **Outcome:** The RF acts as a tensioned "string" in the central canal; its loss or slackening removes the reference signal for Pkd2l1+ CSF-contacting neurons (CSF-cNs), leading to asymmetric muscle contraction and curvature. This separates "cilia motility" (flow generation) from "fiber assembly" (flow sensing structure).

## 3. Support for "Metabolic Countercurvature"
This finding is a "smoking gun" for the **Metabolic Countercurvature** hypothesis because glycosylation (specifically mannosylation) is a metabolically demanding process dependent on the hexosamine biosynthetic pathway and ER/Golgi health.
*   **Metabolic Gating:** If the metabolic supply of mannose (e.g., via Mannose-1-Phosphate) or the energetic state of the ER is compromised (e.g., by "Energy Deficit"), DPY19L1L activity may drop, leading to a "ragged" or slack Reissner Fiber even if gene expression is normal.
*   **Structural Vulnerability:** The RF is not just a genetic product but a *metabolic artifact*—its mechanical integrity is constantly renewed via energy-dependent secretion and modification.

## 4. Open Question & Test
*   **Question:** Is DPY19L1L activity rate-limited by cytosolic mannose-1-phosphate levels or ATP during periods of rapid growth?
*   **Proposed Test:** Supplement *DPY19L1L* hypomorphic zebrafish with dietary Mannose or activate the hexosamine pathway to see if it rescues RF structure and spinal alignment (The "Sweet Rescue").
