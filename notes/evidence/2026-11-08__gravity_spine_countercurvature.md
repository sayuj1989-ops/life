# Evidence Note: PIEZO1-Primary Cilia Axis in Scoliosis

**Date:** 2026-11-08
**Reference:** Chen, F. et al. (2025). PIEZO1-Primary Cilia Axis Mediates Compressive Stress-Induced Growth Plate Degeneration and Ossification in Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis. *JOR SPINE*, 8(4). doi: 10.1002/jsp2.70133.

## Claim
The mechanosensitive ion channel PIEZO1 co-localizes with primary cilia in growth plate chondrocytes. Under excessive compressive stress, this PIEZO1-primary cilia axis is activated, leading to chondrocyte apoptosis, inhibition of cartilage matrix synthesis (e.g., downregulation of COL2A1 and ACAN), and promotion of osteogenic molecules (upregulation of RUNX2), which collectively cause growth plate degeneration and abnormal ossification in Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS).

## Mechanism
PIEZO1 operates as a mechanically activated cation channel (specifically regulating Ca2+ influx) while primary cilia function as the cellular mechanosensory "antenna". The study demonstrates that PIEZO1 directly relies on functional primary cilia to execute its pro-ossification and pro-apoptotic effects in chondrocytes. Stress loading upregulates PIEZO1, but its destructive downstream cascade is alleviated if primary cilia are genetically (e.g., via *IFT88* knockout) or pharmacologically disrupted.

## Why it matters for S-shaped spinal growth / scoliosis
In normal development, the growth plates must dynamically adapt to gravity to maintain symmetric, linear elongation. The progressive deformity in AIS is known to be exacerbated by asymmetric biomechanical loading on the concave side of the curve (the "Vicious Cycle" of Hueter-Volkmann). This paper identifies the specific cellular and molecular machinery—the PIEZO1-primary cilia crosstalk—that senses this compressive overload and incorrectly drives the ossification and degradation of the concave endplate.

## How it constrains or supports the counter-curvature hypothesis
This strongly supports the *Sensor-Anisotropy Energy Number* aspect of the counter-curvature framework. Primary cilia (highly anisotropic structures acting as strain amplifiers) and PIEZO channels are the exact sensors proposed to interpret the spatial metric ($g_{eff}$). While PIEZO2 regulates the *active* proprioceptive feedback loop for paraspinal tone, this paper confirms that PIEZO1 (with cilia) regulates the *passive* structural stiffness ($EI$) by modulating cartilage matrix homeostasis directly in response to compression. It supports the hypothesis that scalar compression (sensed by PIEZO1) lowers the buckling threshold via structural softening.
