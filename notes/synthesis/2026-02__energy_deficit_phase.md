# Weekly Synthesis Note: Energy Deficit Phase Diagram

## Finding
The 2D phase diagram of the Energy Deficit Window in $(\chi_\kappa, L)$ parameter space forms a wedge-shaped instability region.

## Mechanism
The $P_{counter}$ scaling ($L^{2-3}$) combined with the information-coupling strength $\chi_\kappa$ creates a multiplicative effect. High $\chi_\kappa$ means higher curvature deviation from passive, thus higher $P_{counter}$ at any $L$. When this metabolic cost exceeds the proprioceptive supply capacity $S_{proprio}$, the system enters an "Energy Deficit Window" where shape maintenance fails, and the spine buckles under gravitational load.

## Clinical Relevance
This wedge-shaped boundary explains risk stratification in AIS:
*   **High $\chi_\kappa$ patients:** Enter the instability window at shorter spinal lengths $L$ (earlier in adolescence) and exit later, experiencing a wider vulnerability window and earlier onset of scoliosis.
*   **Low $\chi_\kappa$ patients:** May never enter the window, remaining stable throughout the growth spurt.

This establishes $\chi_\kappa$ (the strength of the information-to-curvature coupling) as a critical predictive biomarker for AIS progression risk.
