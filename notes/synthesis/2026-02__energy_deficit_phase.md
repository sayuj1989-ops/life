## Key Findings
- **2D Phase Diagram Result:** Our weekly simulation in `scripts/weekly_sim_energy_deficit_bifurcation.py` generated a 2D parameter sweep over IEC coupling strength ($\chi_\kappa$) and spinal length ($L$). By calculating $R_{deficit} = P_{counter} / S_{proprio}$, we identified a clear parameter space where countercurvature metabolic cost exceeds proprioceptive supply.
- **Wedge-Shaped Instability Region:** The phase diagram demonstrates that the Energy Deficit Window forms a wedge-shaped instability region in ($\chi_\kappa$, $L$) space.
- **Clinical Risk Stratification Connection:** The shape of this region implies that patients with high $\chi_\kappa$ values will cross the $R_{deficit} = 1$ boundary at a shorter spinal length $L$. This means they enter the vulnerability window earlier in adolescence and exit later, enduring a wider total window of risk. Conversely, patients with low $\chi_\kappa$ values may remain below the deficit boundary indefinitely. This strongly aligns with clinical risk stratification profiles for Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS).
