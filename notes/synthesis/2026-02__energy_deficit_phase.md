# Synthesis Note: Energy Deficit Phase Diagram
**Date**: 2026-02

## 1. The 2D Phase Diagram Result
Our recent 2D parameter sweep simulation generated a phase diagram mapping the Energy Deficit Window across both information-coupling strength ($\chi_\kappa$) and spinal length ($L$). By comparing the metabolic power required for countercurvature ($P_{counter}$) against proprioceptive supply ($S_{proprio}$), we delineated a clear boundary where the deficit ratio $R_{deficit} = \frac{P_{counter}}{S_{proprio}}$ exceeds 1.

## 2. Wedge-shaped Instability Region
The energy deficit boundary maps out a wedge-shaped instability region in the ($\chi_\kappa$, $L$) space. Due to the multiplicative effect of $\chi_\kappa$ on active curvature generation, higher values of $\chi_\kappa$ demand significantly greater $P_{counter}$ at any given length $L$. Consequently, the vulnerability window (where the system lacks sufficient energy to resist buckling) expands dramatically as $\chi_\kappa$ increases.

## 3. Clinical Risk Stratification Connections
This finding has direct implications for clinical risk stratification in Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS). Patients with a high intrinsic coupling strength $\chi_\kappa$ will enter the Energy Deficit Window at shorter spinal lengths—meaning an earlier onset in adolescence—and will exit the window much later, enduring a wider and more perilous period of vulnerability. Conversely, patients with low $\chi_\kappa$ values may never cross the $R_{deficit} = 1$ boundary and thus never enter the instability window at all.

This framework suggests a concrete strategy: by correlating pre-treatment $\chi_\kappa$ estimates (potentially derived from initial radiographs) with age-at-onset in clinical cohorts, we could probabilistically forecast both the severity and duration of scoliotic risk for individual patients.
