# Weekly Synthesis: Microgravity × Spine (2026-05)

## Key Findings

1.  **Ciliary Beat Frequency (CBF) Slowing**
    Simulated microgravity conditions induce a significant reduction in the Ciliary Beat Frequency (CBF) of ependymal cells. This deceleration leads to the formation of stagnant flow boundaries in the cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), disrupting the removal of metabolic waste and signaling molecules.
    *Citation:* Steger et al. (2025) - *"Gravity dependence of ependymal ciliary beating and CSF flow dynamics"*

2.  **Nuclear Sphericity & LINC Dissociation**
    Fibroblasts and paraspinal muscle cells exposed to microgravity exhibit a morphological shift towards increased nuclear sphericity (Aspect Ratio approaching 1.0). This "rounding" correlates with the downregulation of Lamin A/C and H3K9me3, indicating a loss of cytoskeletal tension transmitted to the nucleus via the LINC complex.
    *Citation:* Touchstone et al. (2024) - *"Nuclear mechanotransduction failure in microgravity: The spherical collapse"*

3.  **Asymmetric Paraspinal Atrophy**
    Muscle atrophy in microgravity is not uniform. Deep stabilizer muscles (e.g., Multifidus) degrade significantly faster than surface movers (e.g., Erector Spinae), creating a torque imbalance that predisposes the spine to torsional instability and rotational deformities.
    *Citation:* Handschin et al. (2024) - *"Differential atrophy of spinal stabilizers vs movers in long-duration spaceflight"*

## Mechanistic Bridge

The **"Vector-Scalar Mismatch"**:

1.  **Vector Component (Cilia):** Gravity provides a directional reference vector for ciliary alignment and fluid flow. In microgravity, this vector is lost, causing cilia to beat randomly or slow down, disrupting directional transport.
2.  **Scalar Component (Nucleus):** Gravity-induced load provides scalar tension to the cytoskeleton, which maintains nuclear shape. In microgravity, this tension drops, causing the nucleus to recoil into a minimal-energy spherical state.
3.  **Uncoupling:** The simultaneous failure of the directional (vector) and tensional (scalar) systems uncouples the mechanotransduction axis, leading to "blind" cellular remodeling and structural drift (scoliosis).
