# Microgravity × Spine: Weekly Synthesis (2026-W13)

## Observed Findings

1. **Primary Cilium Disruption in Bone Mechanotransduction:**
   Microgravity alters mechanical stimuli, leading to the disruption of primary cilium-dependent mechanotransduction pathways in bone tissue. This disrupts musculoskeletal cell physiology by affecting mechanosensing and subsequent downstream signaling, which contributes to bone density loss in astronauts.
   *Citation:* Effects of microgravity mechanotransduction in bone tissue and cells: systematic review on primary cilium-dependent mechanisms (DOI: 10.1038/s41526-025-00556-y)

2. **Biomechanical Alterations in Lumbar IVDs:**
   Microgravity exposure induces significant biochemical and biomechanical changes within the lumbar intervertebral discs (IVDs). The removal of standard gravitational loading alters the interstitial fluid phase and solute transport (e.g., glucose, oxygen, lactate) across the charged solid phase, impacting overall disc homeostasis and mechanical properties.
   *Citation:* Numerical study on the effect of microgravity on biomechanics in human lumbar intervertebral discs (DOI: 10.1038/s41526-025-00483-y)

3. **Paraspinal Muscle Degeneration and Inflammatory Gene Expression:**
   Spaceflight microgravity (aboard the Bion M1 biosatellite) induces significant differential expression of genes related to inflammation (Il6ra, Tnfaip2, Myo5a), muscle atrophy (Fbox32, Cdkn1a, Mafb), and reactive oxygen species (ROS) in mouse paraspinal muscles. These transcriptomic shifts drive the characteristic pathological alterations of paraspinal muscle degeneration (PSMD), including fatty infiltration and tissue fibrosis.
   *Citation:* Identification of differentially expressed genes in mouse paraspinal muscle in response to microgravity (DOI: 10.3389/fendo.2022.1020743)

## Mechanistic Bridge & Predicted Directionality

**Bridge to Mechanotransduction + ECM Remodeling:**
The loss of gravity functionally removes the baseline compressive and tensile pre-stress on the spine. Mechanically, this unloads both the solid phase (e.g., bone via primary cilia) and the fluid phase (e.g., IVD solute transport). Biologically, this sensory deprivation triggers a pathological remodeling cascade: the absence of adequate strain signals (via impaired cilia) shifts paraspinal mesenchymal and muscle cells toward an inflammatory, adipogenic, and atrophic state, accelerating ECM degradation and fibrosis.

**Directionality of Response:**
- **Under Unloading (Microgravity):**
  - Downregulation of primary cilia-mediated osteogenic/myogenic signals.
  - Decreased convective fluid/solute transport within the IVD (stagnation/hypoxia).
  - Upregulation of inflammatory (IL6ra) and atrophic/ROS-related pathways in paraspinal muscles, driving fatty infiltration and ECM fibrosis.
- **Under Loading (1G or Centrifugation):**
  - Restoration of primary cilia mechanosensing, suppressing adipogenesis.
  - Increased dynamic convective pumping of nutrients/waste across the IVD endplate.
  - Suppression of ROS and inflammatory transcripts, maintaining paraspinal muscle tone and healthy ECM turnover.
