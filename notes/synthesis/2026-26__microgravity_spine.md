# Weekly Synthesis: The Stagnant Spine: Convective Failure

**Date:** 2026-07-29 (Week 26)
**Author:** Microgravity Synthesizer

## 1. Key Findings

### Observed: The "Congestive" Disc
Evidence confirms that nutrient transport in the Intervertebral Disc (IVD) relies on a dual-mode mechanism: **Passive Diffusion** (for small molecules like Oxygen) and **Convective Transport** (fluid pumping driven by axial loading for larger metabolites like Lactate/Glucose). Microgravity eliminates the "pumping" component, creating a **"Congestive" state** where the disc swells with water (static pressure) but starves of nutrients (low dynamic flow).
* **Source:** Sato et al. (2025), *Journal of Biomechanics*. (See `notes/evidence/2026-06-23__IVD_Congestion.md`)

### Observed: Glymphatic Stasis
The removal of gravity and the "headward fluid shift" severely impairs the **Glymphatic System** (CSF-ISF exchange). In 1G, the cardiac and respiratory pulses, assisted by gravity during upright posture, drive CSF flow. In microgravity (and bed rest), this clearance drops by ~40%, leading to the accumulation of neurotoxic waste (Amyloid, Lactate) in the spinal parenchyma.
* **Source:** Mader et al. (2026), *Nature Neuroscience*. (See `notes/evidence/2026-06-23__slym_glymphatic_barrier.md`)

### Hypothesized: YAP/TAZ as the "Flow Sensor"
While often studied as a "stiffness" sensor, **YAP/TAZ** is also a potent "shear/flow" sensor. In the liver and bone, fluid flow across the cell surface drives YAP nuclear translocation. We hypothesize that the loss of interstitial fluid flow in the spine (due to lack of "squishing") is the primary signal that inactivates YAP, triggering the adipogenic switch, even before the tissue actually "softens."
* **Source:** Wang et al. (2023), *Cell Biology*. (Based on liver dysfunction models in microgravity).

## 2. Mechanistic Bridge: Flow *Is* The Signal

We are moving from a "Static Stiffness" model to a "Dynamic Flow" model.
1.  **The Pump:** Gravity provides the background "bias" that allows movement to generate pressure gradients (pumping).
2.  **The Flush:** This pumping clears metabolic waste (Glymphatic) and delivers nutrients (Convection).
3.  **The Signal:** The *shear stress* of this fluid movement is detected by the cell (likely via Cilia or Caveolae) and transduced into **YAP/TAZ** activation.

**Conclusion:** The spine degenerates in microgravity not just because it is "unloaded" (force = 0), but because it is "stagnant" (flow = 0). The tissue suffocates in its own waste, and the cells interpret this stasis as a "detach" signal (anoikis).
