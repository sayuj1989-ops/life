## 1. Key Findings

### Finding 1: Gravitational Loading as the Zeitgeber
The intervertebral disc (IVD) possesses an intrinsic circadian clock (BMAL1/CLOCK) that regulates the anabolic/catabolic balance of the ECM. This clock relies on rhythmic mechanical loading—specifically the daily compression/recovery cycle driven by gravity—for phase entrainment. In the absence of this "Gravity Pulse" (e.g., microgravity), the clock drifts ("Spinal Jetlag"), locking the tissue in a catabolic state.
*   **Source:** Dudek et al. (2017) confirmed the existence of the IVD clock and its link to degeneration; *Evidence Note 2026-07-30*.

### Finding 2: Vector-Scalar Mechanosensory Duality
Spinal alignment is governed by a dual-channel system:
1.  **Scalar Channel (Piezo1):** Senses membrane tension to regulate stiffness (Osteogenesis/ECM synthesis). Failure leads to **Buckling**.
2.  **Vector Channel (Piezo2):** Senses directional strain for proprioceptive alignment. Failure leads to **Drift**.
Microgravity uncouples these, creating a "Soft & Blind" spine.
*   **Source:** Ramli et al. (2024), Assaraf et al. (2020); *Evidence Note 2026-06-24*.

### Finding 3: The Constitutive Day Model
Unloading mimics a "Constitutive Day" state where melatonin-mediated suppression of muscle tone (normally active at night/unloaded) fails. This leads to persistent, noisy muscle tone that prevents the "reset" required for geometric error correction.
*   **Source:** Moreau et al. (2004); *Evidence Note 2025-02-22* (Contextual).
