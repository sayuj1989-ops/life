## Key Findings

1.  **Scalar Gain Increase (Swelling)**
    *   **Observation**: Unloading triggers rapid IVD height increase and osmotic swelling due to the loss of compressive regulation (*Treffel et al., 2016*). This represents an unchecked increase in "Scalar Gain" (isotropic pressure).
    *   **Context**: *Moulia (2009)* predicted that gravitropic systems require a balance between growth (scalar) and proprioception (vector); swelling disrupts this balance.

2.  **Vector Gain Loss (Atrophy)**
    *   **Observation**: Anti-gravity muscles (Multifidus, Erector Spinae) undergo rapid, selective atrophy and fatty infiltration in microgravity, removing the active directional stabilization required for alignment (*Handschin et al., 2024*).
    *   **Context**: This is a loss of "Vector Gain" ($\gamma_{proprio}$), destabilizing the Bastien oscillator.

3.  **Nuclear Softening & Epigenetic Drift**
    *   **Observation**: The loss of gravitational strain on the nucleus leads to a reduction in Lamin A/C levels and a subsequent loss of H3K9me3 heterochromatin, causing "Epigenetic Drift" and cellular senescence (*Touchstone et al., 2024*; *Nava et al., 2020*).
    *   **Context**: Gravity acts as a "compressive molding" force for chromatin; without it, the nucleus softens and genes drift.

4.  **Glymphatic Stasis**
    *   **Observation**: Spinal cord waste clearance drops significantly in unloading conditions due to the lack of hydrostatic pressure gradients, leading to the accumulation of metabolic waste (*Mader et al., 2026*).
    *   **Context**: This chemical stagnation acts as a proinflammatory driver independent of mechanics.

## Mechanistic Bridge: Vector-Scalar Mismatch

The pathology of the "Microgravity Spine" is a product of **Vector-Scalar Mismatch**.
*   **Scalar Sensors (Piezo1)**: Respond to isotropic stress (swelling). In microgravity, the *signal* (compression) is lost, but the *response* (swelling) is unrestrained, leading to "Scalar Senescence" (ROS/Inflammation).
*   **Vector Sensors (Piezo2)**: Respond to directional strain (alignment). In microgravity, the *reference vector* ($\vec{g}$) is lost, causing these sensors to silence or drift (Proprioceptive Blindness).

**Conclusion**: The system swells (High Scalar) while losing direction (Low Vector), pushing the spine towards a buckling instability.
