# Weekly Simulation: Torsion Anisotropy Tradeoff

## Hypothesis
We hypothesize that torsion acts as a key symmetry breaker that transforms stable planar S-curves into complex 3D scoliosis. We test if high anisotropy can rescue this.

## Parameters
- **Random Seed**: 2026
- **Active Curvature**: 12.0
- **Torsions**: [0.0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0]
- **Anisotropies**: [1.0, 2.0, 3.0, 5.0]

## Results
Torsion significantly amplifies the Cobb angle across all anisotropies. Higher anisotropy (R > 1.0) provides partial stabilization but cannot completely eliminate the torsion-driven buckling at high torsion values.

## Emergent Shapes
At high torsion and low anisotropy, profound 3D helical buckling occurs, mirroring severe adolescent idiopathic scoliosis.
