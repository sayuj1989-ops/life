# Simulation Report: Anisotropy S-Shape Emergence under Growth+Gravity

**Date:** 2026-04-12

## What Changed
Swept stiffness anisotropy from 1.0 to 15.0 under high active curvature (15.0), gravity (9.81), and a small initial lateral defect (0.05).

## Informing Scoliosis vs Normal S-curve
Low anisotropy under high growth leads to substantial lateral buckling (scoliosis). High anisotropy stabilizes the spine, allowing a normal sagittal S-curve while minimizing lateral deviation.
