# Day 1: Ageing and Postural Control

## Proprioceptive Decline
Age-related proprioceptive decline significantly impacts postural control. Response to proprioception in standing posture is governed by interactions between the ankle and hip strategies. Reduced proprioceptive function contributes to changes in these strategies, increasing fall risk.
*Citation: Proprioception and Geriatric Low Back Pain (PMC9605761).*

## Vestibular Degeneration
The vestibular system is critical for spatial orientation and postural control. Healthy ageing involves changes in vestibular-guided navigation and balance. Loss of vestibular function is associated with increased postural sway in older adults.
*Citations: Spatial orientation, postural control and the vestibular system in healthy elderly and Alzheimer's dementia (PMC10162042).*

## Cerebellar Atrophy
The cerebellum is essential for sensory error prediction signaling in postural control. While marked cerebellar atrophy occurs with ageing, predictive motor timing scores can remain stable, suggesting compensatory mechanisms (e.g., increased frontal activity). However, age-related cerebellar changes still impact motor learning and postural correction capabilities.
*Citation: CONSENSUS PAPER: CEREBELLUM AND AGEING (PMC10776824).*
