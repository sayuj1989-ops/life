# Day 1: Postural Control and Ageing Literature Summary

## Overview
This document summarizes key findings from the literature regarding the degradation of postural control mechanisms in ageing, specifically focusing on proprioceptive decline, vestibular degeneration, and cerebellar atrophy. These findings provide empirical foundations for the "terminal derivative gain gap" model of geriatric postural instability.

## 1. Postural Control & Balance in Ageing
* **Lumbosacral orthosis can improve postural control in older adults with chronic low back pain.**
  * *Authors*: d'Assomption RM, Larivière C, Mecheri H, Ngomo S, da Silva RA.
  * *DOI*: [10.1186/s12891-026-09602-6](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12891-026-09602-6)
  * *Relevance*: Highlights the role of mechanical support and altered sensory feedback in maintaining balance in the elderly, pointing to degraded baseline control.

* **Measures of Human Position Sense Do Not Always Include Contributions From Peripheral Sensory Receptors.**
  * *Authors*: Proske U, Weber BM.
  * *DOI*: [10.1111/ejn.70444](https://doi.org/10.1111/ejn.70444)
  * *Relevance*: Discusses the complex integration of muscle spindles and other sensors in position sense. Degradation in these peripheral receptors strongly influences the proprioceptive delay ($\tau$) in the PID model.

## 2. Vestibular Degeneration
* **Influence of BPPV and Meniere's Disease on Cognitive Abilities: A Questionnaire-Based Study.**
  * *Authors*: Bhattacharyya R, Barman A, Antony F.
  * *DOI*: [10.1016/j.joto.2023.11.001](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.joto.2023.11.001)
  * *Relevance*: Emphasizes that vestibular system degradation not only impairs direct postural feedback but also impacts spatial perception and cognition, further degrading the brain's predictive postural models.

* **Age-related hearing loss and balance disorders: analysis of interactions and clinical implications in older persons.**
  * *Authors*: Frosolini A, Franz L, Calvanese L, Caragli V, Parrino D, Fabbris C, Badin G, Negrisolo M, Cenedese R, Doretto L, Brescia G, de Filippis C, Genovese E, Zanoletti E, Marioni G.
  * *DOI*: [10.3389/fragi.2025.1752488](https://doi.org/10.3389/fragi.2025.1752488)
  * *Relevance*: Confirms the high comorbidity of vestibular/gait dysfunction in older populations, contributing directly to an increased derivative gain gap by compromising multi-sensory integration.

## 3. Cerebellar Atrophy & Central Processing
* **The neural underpinnings of cognitive and postural profile of a young adult with congenital cerebellar atrophy: a longitudinal case report.**
  * *Authors*: Devita M, Ravelli A, Ceolin C, De Rui M, Pini L, Bendini M, Sarlo M, Sergi G, Castiello U, Mapelli D, Begliomini C.
  * *DOI*: [10.3389/fnins.2026.1724744](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnins.2026.1724744)
  * *Relevance*: Demonstrates the cerebellum's critical role in gross motor and postural functioning. Atrophy in this region degrades the predictive controller, rendering feedback loops significantly slower and less accurate.

## 4. Peripheral Neuropathy & Sarcopenia
* **Comparison of Up-on-the-Toes Stand Test Performance in Young Adults and Older Adults With Diabetic Peripheral Neuropathy.**
  * *Authors*: Buckley JG, Sabouni A, Rodacki ALF, Brown S, Reeves ND.
  * *DOI*: [10.1002/jfa2.70112](https://doi.org/10.1002/jfa2.70112)
  * *Relevance*: Shows that peripheral neuropathy significantly degrades ankle functioning and balance, modeling an explicit mechanism for increased delay ($\tau$) and decreased actuator gain.

* **Balance, Gait, Functionality and Fall Occurrence in Adults and Older Adults with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus and Associated Peripheral Neuropathy.**
  * *Authors*: Tavares NMB, Silva JM, Silva MDMD, Silva LDT, Souza JN, Ithamar L, Raposo MCF, Melo RS.
  * *DOI*: [10.3390/clinpract14050161](https://doi.org/10.3390/clinpract14050161)
  * *Relevance*: Directly links altered foot sensory information due to neuropathy to balance disorders and fall occurrence.

* **Assessing the impact of sarcopenia on balance in the elderly: an analysis using a force platform.**
  * *Authors*: Michel E, Chorin F, Guerin O, Sacco G, Zory R.
  * *DOI*: [10.1186/s12877-025-06755-2](https://doi.org/10.1186/s12877-025-06755-2)
  * *Relevance*: Sarcopenia reduces the functional strength (actuator gain) available to counteract postural sway, a critical component of the failing PID plant.

## Synthesis for Paper 3
These papers provide a solid empirical basis for the **terminal derivative gain gap**. Ageing causes a simultaneous degradation of:
1. **The Controller (Sensorimotor Delay, $\tau$)**: Increased via peripheral neuropathy and vestibular degeneration.
2. **The Predictive Integration**: Compromised by cerebellar atrophy.
3. **The Plant (Actuator Gain)**: Weakened by sarcopenia.

Unlike the transient gap in adolescence, these concurrent, progressive deteriorations lead to permanent postural collapse, presenting clinically as geriatric falls.
