# Day 1: Postural Control and Ageing

## Proprioceptive Decline with Age

*   **Age-related Decline in Proprioception**
    *   *DOI:* 10.1097/00003086-198404000-00035
    *   *Summary:* General age-related decline in proprioception.
*   **Age-related changes in leg proprioception: implications for postural control**
    *   *DOI:* 10.1152/jn.00067.2019
    *   *Summary:* Highlights specific changes in leg proprioception which are critical for the inverted pendulum model of postural control.
*   **A Physically Active Lifestyle Can Protect against Age-Related Decline in Ankle Proprioception**
    *   *DOI:* 10.1080/00222895.2023.2293003
    *   *Summary:* Ankle proprioception is crucial for the continuous feedback loop in postural stability; decline is a major factor in ageing, though mitigable by activity.

## Vestibular Degeneration

*   **Age-Related Changes to Vestibular Heave and Pitch Perception and Associations with Postural Control**
    *   *DOI:* 10.1038/s41598-022-09807-4
    *   *Summary:* Direct association between vestibular perception changes and postural control in ageing.
*   **Vestibular Evoked Myogenic Potentials and Postural Control in Adults with Age-Related Hearing Loss**
    *   *DOI:* 10.21007/etd.cghs.2020.0504
    *   *Summary:* Link between age-related vestibular decline and postural stability.
*   **Impaired Vestibular System Linked with Higher Fall-Risk in Alzheimer’s Patients**
    *   *DOI:* 10.1044/2022-0607-impaired-vestibular-alzheimers
    *   *Summary:* Highlights the importance of the vestibular system in fall risk.

## Cerebellar Atrophy and Postural Instability

*   **Cerebellar Purkinje cell stripe patterns reveal a differential vulnerability and resistance to cell loss during normal aging in mice**
    *   *DOI:* 10.7554/elife.106273.3.sa3 (and related preprints/reviews)
    *   *Summary:* Discusses the loss of Purkinje cells during normal aging. Purkinje cells are central to the cerebellum's role in postural prediction.
*   **Postural Ataxia in Cerebellar Downbeat Nystagmus: Its Relation to Visual, Proprioceptive and Vestibular Signals and Cerebellar Atrophy**
    *   *DOI:* 10.1371/journal.pone.0168808
    *   *Summary:* Connects cerebellar atrophy to postural ataxia, involving the integration of visual, proprioceptive, and vestibular signals.
*   **DELAYED AND ENHANCED LONG LATENCY REFLEXES AS THE POSSIBLE CAUSE OF POSTURAL TREMOR IN LATE CEREBELLAR ATROPHY**
    *   *DOI:* 10.1093/brain/104.1.97
    *   *Summary:* explicitly mentions delayed reflexes as a cause of postural tremor in cerebellar atrophy, providing direct evidence for the "Terminal Derivative Gain Gap" model's premise of increased $\tau$ and degraded controller function.
