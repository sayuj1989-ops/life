# Day 2: Neuropathy and Conduction Delay Literature Review

- **Age-related decline in Nerve Conduction Velocity (NCV)**: Multiple studies report significant slowing of both motor and sensory NCV with age. Tong et al. (2004) and Werner et al. found median sensory velocities decrease at a rate of 0.14 m/s to 0.41 m/s per year. Falco et al. noted a 10% reduction in conduction rate by age 60. (DOI: 10.4103/jnrp.jnrp_273_17, Impact of Aging on Nerve Conduction Velocities and Late Responses in Healthy Individuals).
- **Proprioceptive Delay (\tau)**: While some studies emphasize central processing speed as the main factor in tracking delays, the decrease in peripheral conduction velocity directly contributes to an overall increase in the neural time delay (\tau) for proprioceptive feedback loops in postural control. As described by Peterka (2002), the delayed PID feedback model relies on this \tau value, which inevitably increases as the peripheral nervous system undergoes demyelination, axonal loss, and general peripheral neuropathy associated with aging.
