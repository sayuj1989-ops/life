# Phase 1, Day 2: Literature Review — AIS Genetics

This document comprehensively reviews key Genome-Wide Association Study (GWAS) hits associated with Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) to lay the genetic groundwork for understanding modifications in proprioceptive delay ($\tau$).

## 1. GPR126 (ADGRG6)
- **Locus:** Chromosome 6q24.1
- **Key Risk Variant:** rs6570507
- **Effect Size:** Odds Ratio = 1.22 (Combined P = 2.95 $\times$ $10^{-20}$ in a multi-ethnic meta-analysis of 6,873 cases and 38,916 controls) (Kou et al., 2018, Sci Rep. doi: 10.1038/s41598-018-29011-7).
- **Population Frequency:** Discovered in Japanese cohort, confirmed worldwide (East Asia, Northern Europe, USA).
- **Proposed Biological Function:** GPR126 expression is high in cartilage. Knockout models (e.g., in zebrafish) exhibit delayed bone formation in vertebrae during development. Furthermore, GPR126 is essential for peripheral nerve development and myelination in mammals. Mice lacking Gpr126 exhibit growth failure and abnormalities in limb posture. Hypothesis: Impaired myelination leads to reduced conduction velocity and increased $\tau_{afferent}$.

## 2. LBX1 (Ladybird Homeobox 1)
- **Locus:** Chromosome 10:101219450 (near LBX1 / LBX1-AS1)
- **Key Risk Variant:** rs11190870
- **Effect Size:** Effect size (Odds Ratio) approx. 1.52 to 1.61 across studies in GWAS catalog. T allele associated with curve progression (larger Cobb angle in patients with TT genotype, p=0.01) in an Acehnese population (Aulia et al., 2025).
- **Population Frequency:** T allele frequency ~0.557 to 0.56 in GWAS cohorts; T allele prevalence 73.3% in a small Acehnese preliminary study.
- **Proposed Biological Function:** Associated with disease severity / curve progression. Hypothesis: Given its role in dorsal spinal cord interneuron specification, variants alter the development of proprioceptive relay circuits in the spinal cord, leading to increased $\tau_{spinal}$.

## 3. PAX1 (Paired Box 1)
- **Locus:** Enhancer locus near PAX1
- **Key Risk Variants:** A PAX1 enhancer locus (Sharma et al., 2015). Variants at rs6137473 (downstream of PAX1) associated with increased risk of developing AIS.
- **Effect Size:** Associated with susceptibility to idiopathic scoliosis in females.
- **Proposed Biological Function:** PAX1 is required for adequate formation of vertebral bodies and can regulate the transformation of the notochord in the intervertebral disc. Deletion of PAX1 scoliosis-associated regulatory elements (PEC7) leads to a female-biased tail abnormality in mice. This affects vertebral geometry (the "plant" in the PID model) rather than the proprioceptive delay controller.

## Synthesis
These variants collectively illustrate that AIS susceptibility is polygenic, affecting different components of the proprioceptive control system. While PAX1 acts on structural geometry, GPR126 and LBX1 directly implicate neural transmission mechanisms—afferent conduction and spinal relay, respectively—which are expected to directly increase the total proprioceptive delay ($\tau$).
