# Phase 1, Day 2: Literature Review — AIS Genetics

This document provides a comprehensive review of major genome-wide association study (GWAS) hits for adolescent idiopathic scoliosis (AIS), focusing on key genetic variants and their functional mapping to the proprioceptive delay ($\tau$) model.

## 1. GPR126 (ADGRG6)
- **Gene Function**: Encodes an adhesion G-protein-coupled receptor critical for Schwann cell myelination of peripheral nerves and bone development.
- **Key Variant**: rs225694, rs7774095, rs2294773 (identified in various populations, including Northern Han Chinese) (Zhao et al., 2018, DOI: 10.3978/j.issn.2305-5839.2015.03.54).
- **Risk Allele & Effect Size**: Allele A of rs225694 is a risk allele, OR ~ 1.2-1.3 in Asian populations (Zhao et al., 2018). Minor allele frequencies range around 30-40% depending on the exact cohort.
- **Proposed Biological Function in AIS**: Delayed ossification of the developing spine, or impaired myelination of proprioceptive afferents leading to slower conduction velocity and increased $\tau_{aff}$.
- **Connection to $\tau$ Budget**: Affects Afferent Conduction Delay ($\tau_{aff}$).

## 2. LBX1
- **Gene Function**: Ladybird homeobox 1 is a transcription factor heavily involved in the specification of dorsal spinal cord interneurons, particularly those involved in somatosensory and proprioceptive circuits.
- **Key Variant**: rs11190870, rs11190878.
- **Risk Allele & Effect Size**: T allele of rs11190870 is consistently replicated as a risk allele across Asian and Caucasian populations, OR ~ 1.2-1.5, reaching up to OR 1.56 in some Japanese cohorts. The frequency of the risk allele is exceptionally high in East Asian populations (~50-60%) (Takahashi et al., 2011, DOI: 10.1038/ng.961; Jiang et al., 2019).
- **Proposed Biological Function in AIS**: Abnormal somatosensory function, specifically altering the development of proprioceptive relay circuits in the dorsal horn or Clarke's column.
- **Connection to $\tau$ Budget**: Affects Spinal Relay Delay ($\tau_{spin}$).

## 3. PAX1
- **Gene Function**: Transcription factor essential for vertebral column development and somite patterning.
- **Key Variant**: Enhancer locus variants (e.g., in the 20p11.22 inter-genic region between PAX1 and FOXA2).
- **Risk Allele & Effect Size**: e.g., allele A in the 20p11.22 region, OR ~ 1.1-1.2. Minor allele frequencies vary widely but typically >15% across diverse female cohorts (Sharma et al., 2015, DOI: 10.1038/ncomms7452).
- **Proposed Biological Function in AIS**: Influences skeletal development and structural integrity of the spine.
- **Connection to Model**: PAX1 likely acts on the "plant" (the structural geometry and mechanics of the spine itself) rather than the "controller" (proprioceptive loop). It does not directly increase $\tau$, but rather alters the biomechanical environment that the delayed controller must stabilize.

## 4. Other notable genes (e.g., SCN11A/SCN9A/Myelin Proteins)
- **Gene Function**: SCN11A (Nav1.9) and SCN9A (Nav1.7) are voltage-gated sodium channels in DRG neurons governing action potential propagation. Structural components of peripheral myelin like MBP, PMP22, MPZ directly influence conduction velocity.
- **Connection to $\tau$ Budget**: Variants in these pathways could subtly alter channel kinetics or myelination efficiency, directly modulating afferent conduction velocity ($\tau_{aff}$).

## Conclusion
The key GWAS hits map cleanly onto the components of the $\tau$ model. GPR126 and myelination proteins govern $\tau_{aff}$, LBX1 governs $\tau_{spin}$, and PAX1 primarily alters the mechanical plant. This separation of "controller defects" ($\tau$ increasers) and "plant defects" (geometry changers) provides a powerful dual-mechanism framework for AIS pathogenesis.
