# Phase 1, Day 2: Literature Review — AIS Genetics

## Overview
This document reviews the major genome-wide association study (GWAS) hits for Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS), focusing on their role in the components of the proprioceptive delay $\tau$.

## LBX1 (Ladybird Homeobox 1)
* **Key SNPs:** rs11190878, rs11190870
* **Risk Allele:** T (for rs11190878 in Caucasian population, based on haplotype TTA)
* **Effect Size:** OR = 1.56 for the recessive risk haplotype (OR = 0.63 for the overall rs11190878 association signal, implying the protective allele is common or opposite) [1]
* **Population Frequency:** Control frequency for the TTA haplotype is 0.52 [1]
* **Proposed Biological Function:** Abnormal somatosensory function and dorsal spinal cord interneuron specification. This suggests a direct mapping to the spinal relay delay ($\tau_{spin}$).
* **Sources:**
  1. "Haplotypes at LBX1 Have Distinct Inheritance Patterns with Opposite Effects in Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis"
  2. Takahashi et al. 2011 Nat Genet PMID:22019779

## GPR126 (ADGRG6)
* **Key SNPs:** rs6570507
* **Risk Allele:** *unverified — needs literature confirmation*
* **Effect Size:** P = 2.25 × 10(-10); odds ratio (OR) = 1.28 (Japanese population) [3]
* **Population Frequency:** *unverified — needs literature confirmation*
* **Proposed Biological Function:** Delayed ossification of the developing spine. GPR126 is an adhesion G-protein-coupled receptor critical for Schwann cell myelination of peripheral nerves. This maps to the afferent conduction delay ($\tau_{aff}$).
* **Sources:**
  3. "Genetic variants in GPR126 are associated with adolescent idiopathic scoliosis" Kou I et al. 2013 Nat Genet PMID:23666238

## PAX1 (Paired Box 1)
* **Key SNPs:** rs6137473 (G > A) [4], rs12946942 (unverified, seen in other contexts)
* **Risk Allele:** A [4]
* **Effect Size:** *unverified — needs literature confirmation*
* **Population Frequency:** *unverified — needs literature confirmation*
* **Proposed Biological Function:** Required for adequate formation of vertebral bodies and can regulate the transformation of the notochord in the intervertebral disc. Also associated with female-specific susceptibility [5]. This may alter the mechanical plant geometry rather than the controller $\tau$.
* **Sources:**
  4. "Polymorphisms in paired box 1 gene were associated with susceptibility of adolescent idiopathic scoliosis: A case–control study"
  5. "A PAX1 enhancer locus is associated with susceptibility to idiopathic scoliosis in females." Sharma S et al. 2015 Nat Commun PMID:25784220

## Summary
The major AIS GWAS loci map onto different components of the control model:
* **LBX1:** Spinal relay delay ($\tau_{spin}$)
* **GPR126:** Afferent conduction delay ($\tau_{aff}$)
* **PAX1:** Plant mechanics (body geometry)
