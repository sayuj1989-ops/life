# Phase 1, Day 2: AIS Genetics and GWAS

Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) is a highly heritable complex genetic disorder. Genome-wide association studies (GWAS) have identified several susceptibility loci. This document breaks down the major hits and their proposed role in proprioceptive delay ($\tau$) and spinal development.

## 1. LBX1 (Ladybird Homeobox 1)
*   **Risk Alleles / Locus:** rs11190870 (chromosome 10q24.31).
*   **Effect Size:** Odds Ratio (OR) = 1.56 (Takahashi et al., 2011, DOI: 10.1038/ng.974).
*   **Population Frequency:** Widely replicated across Japanese and Chinese Han populations.
*   **Proposed Biological Function:** Transcription factor essential for the specification of dorsal spinal cord interneurons, including somatosensory relay neurons.
*   **Relevance to $\tau$ model:** Highly relevant to **$\tau_{spinal}$**. Alterations in LBX1 may affect the developmental timing or efficiency of proprioceptive circuits in the spinal cord.

## 2. GPR126 (ADGRG6)
*   **Risk Alleles / Locus:** rs6570507 (chromosome 6q24.1).
*   **Effect Size:** Odds Ratio (OR) = 1.28 (Kou et al., 2013, PubMed 23666238).
*   **Population Frequency:** Validated in multi-ethnic cohorts.
*   **Proposed Biological Function:** Adhesion G-protein-coupled receptor critical for Schwann cell myelination of peripheral nerves. Highly expressed in cartilage, and knockdown in zebrafish causes delayed ossification.
*   **Relevance to $\tau$ model:** Highly relevant to **$\tau_{afferent}$**. Impaired myelination of proprioceptive afferents could decrease conduction velocity, increasing $\tau_{afferent}$.

## 3. PAX1 (Paired Box 1)
*   **Risk Alleles / Locus:** Enhancer locus associated with susceptibility in females.
*   **Effect Size:** (Sharma et al., 2015, DOI: 10.1038/ncomms7452).
*   **Population Frequency:** Female-specific susceptibility heavily implicated in GWAS.
*   **Proposed Biological Function:** Transcription factor involved in vertebral column development.
*   **Relevance to $\tau$ model:** Primarily affects the **"plant"** in the PID model (vertebral geometry) rather than the neural controller ($\tau$). It provides the mechanical substrate upon which the delayed controller fails.

## 4. BNC2 (Basonuclin-2)
*   **Risk Alleles / Locus:** rs10738445 (chromosome 9p22.2).
*   **Effect Size:** Odds Ratio (OR) = 1.21 (Ogura et al., 2015, PubMed 26211971).
*   **Proposed Biological Function:** Zinc finger transcription factor. Overexpression produces body curvature in zebrafish in a gene-dosage-dependent manner.
