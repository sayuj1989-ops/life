**Key Findings/Decisions:**
- Decomposed the total proprioceptive delay ($\tau$) into 7 physiological sub-components: transduction, afferent conduction, spinal relay, cerebellar processing, efferent conduction, NMJ, and electromechanical delay.
- Estimated the latency for each component based on established physiology, noting electromechanical delay ($\tau_{EM}$) ranges from 10-30 ms (Komi & Cavanagh, 1977; DOI: 10.1249/00005768-197721000-00021) and afferent/efferent conduction ranges from 15-25 ms each.
- The total theoretical budget (~75-145 ms) aligns closely with Peterka's (2002) model delays (150-200 ms), with the remainder likely representing more complex cerebellar state-estimation latency or developmental variation.
