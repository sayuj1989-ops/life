# Day 6: Consciousness Theories and Prediction Error

## Core Connections
1. **Predictive Processing (PP) and Consciousness**: PP posits that conscious experience is the brain's "best guess" (posterior distribution) about the causes of sensory input. Attention is the mechanism by which the brain optimises the precision (inverse variance) of these prediction errors.
2. **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)**: IIT argues that consciousness is determined by the amount of integrated information ($\Phi$) generated by a system.
3. **Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW)**: GNW posits that consciousness occurs when information is globally broadcast across the brain via a network of long-range connections.

## The Bridge to Paper 1 and 2
Paper 1 introduced the concept of "Biological Counter-Curvature": organisms push back against gravity, potentially modulating emergent spacetime geometry via an "Information Term" ($I_{\mu\nu}$) in Einstein's field equations.
Paper 2 identified the "derivative gain gap" ($K_d$ degradation) as a transient postural instability in adolescence.

Paper 5 synthesises these by interpreting the gain gap as a *transient, pathological reduction in precision weighting*.
When the precision of proprioceptive prediction errors collapses (due to rapid growth outstripping model updates), the generative model and the sensory feedback become temporarily decoupled.
In PP terms, the posterior distribution over the body state becomes wide and uncertain. The "best guess" is degraded.
In IIT terms, this decoupling reduces the system's ability to integrate information ($\Phi$ drops) within the postural control network.
In the context of Paper 1, this represents a local reduction in the informational coupling between the organism and the gravitational field. The "counter-curvature" weakens precisely because the predictive model fails to generate precise enough expectations about the body's interaction with gravity.
