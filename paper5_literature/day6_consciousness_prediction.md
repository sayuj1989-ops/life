# Phase 1, Day 6: Consciousness theories and prediction error

## Core Concepts
Connecting the computational/Bayesian mechanisms of motor control back to the phenomenological aspects of consciousness (building on Paper 1).

### Predictive Processing (PP) and Consciousness
In PP (e.g., Clark, Hohwy, Seth), conscious experience is often equated with the brain's "best guess" or the *posterior* distribution over hidden causes. The contents of consciousness are the high-level predictions that successfully suppress sensory prediction errors.
- **Precision and Attention:** Precision-weighting is the PP equivalent of attention. High precision assigned to a prediction error boosts its influence, making it more likely to update the generative model and enter consciousness.
- **Precision Collapse:** If the precision of a sensory channel (like proprioception) collapses, those signals are functionally attenuated. The system relies more on its priors, and the resulting conscious experience might become detached from actual sensory reality, or the sense of agency/ownership over that body part might be altered.

### Integrated Information Theory (IIT)
IIT (Tononi) posits that consciousness is integrated information ($\Phi$).
- In the active inference framework, integration requires a tightly coupled cycle between the generative model (predictions) and the sensory apparatus (prediction errors).
- During the derivative gain gap, the forward model's predictions (specifically about velocity) are systematically incorrect, and the precision on those errors is down-weighted. This decouples the top-down model from bottom-up reality, effectively reducing the effective connectivity and, theoretically, lowering the integrated information ($\Phi$) within the sensorimotor networks maintaining posture.

### Attentional Schema Theory (AST)
AST (Graziano) suggests consciousness is the brain's simplified model of its own attentional processes.
- The precision-weighting mechanism *is* the attentional process in PP. A breakdown in appropriate precision allocation (like the $\Pi_{y,1}$ drop) could lead to a degraded attentional schema regarding postural control.

## Relevance to Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS)
1. **The "Counter-Curvature" Motif (Paper 1):** Paper 1 proposed a mind-geometry coupling where biological organisms use conscious information processing to push back against gravity. The derivative gain gap is a localized *failure* of this coupling.
2. **Altered Body Awareness:** We predict that during the derivative gain gap, adolescents may experience subtle alterations in body awareness, proprioceptive uncertainty, or a degraded sense of agency over their posture due to the wide posterior (low precision) over body states.
3. **The Information Term:** The transient collapse of precision ($\Pi_{y,1}$) represents a local reduction in the "Information Term" ($I_{\mu\nu}$) proposed in Paper 1. The predictive model degrades, information integration ($\Phi$) drops locally, and the body succumbs to mechanical forces (gravity) rather than actively maintaining counter-curvature.
