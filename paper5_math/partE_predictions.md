# Day 16: Testable Predictions Unique to FEP Framing

This section explicitly derives five testable predictions that distinguish the active inference / free-energy principle interpretation of the "derivative gain gap" from a purely biomechanical or engineering PID control model.

## Prediction 1: Elevated Sensory Attenuation (The Tickle Paradigm)
*   **Rationale:** The FEP predicts that the system down-weights precision on velocity prediction errors ($\Pi_{y'}$) because the generative model is misspecified during rapid growth. This precision collapse is not confined to posture; it represents a systemic down-weighting of proprioceptive reliability.
*   **Protocol:** Use force-matching tasks or the classic "self-tickling" paradigm.
*   **Expected Effect:** AIS patients during the peak height velocity (PHV) window (the gain gap) will exhibit *greater* sensory attenuation (reduced ticklishness, stronger force-matching illusions) compared to age-matched controls, even when seated or lying down (i.e., when postural control is not active).
*   **Distinguishing Feature:** A pure biomechanical PID model only predicts instability when upright and loaded. It does not predict changes in sensory perception when unloaded.

## Prediction 2: Temporal Prediction Deficits
*   **Rationale:** The gain gap is driven by a failure of the generative model to accurately predict the temporal evolution of sensory states (delayed velocity estimation).
*   **Protocol:** Temporal order judgement tasks or sensorimotor synchronisation tasks (e.g., rhythmic finger tapping to a perturbed metronome).
*   **Expected Effect:** AIS patients will show wider temporal binding windows and increased phase variance in synchronisation tasks, correlating with the onset of their curvature.
*   **Distinguishing Feature:** Standard control models do not link spinal reflex parameters to supraspinal temporal processing.

## Prediction 3: Attentional Compensation
*   **Rationale:** In FEP, attention is the mechanism that optimises precision. Top-down attention can temporarily increase the precision of sensory channels.
*   **Protocol:** Measure postural sway on a force plate. Compare a baseline condition with a condition where the subject is explicitly instructed to focus intense attention on their trunk position.
*   **Expected Effect:** The application of top-down attention will temporarily restore the "effective $K_d$" and significantly reduce postural sway in AIS patients, compensating for the gain gap.
*   **Distinguishing Feature:** In a mechanical PID model with a physically degraded sensor (e.g., neuropathy), attention cannot improve the signal-to-noise ratio. In FEP, precision is a dynamically tunable hyperparameter.

## Prediction 4: Protective Effect of Interoceptive Practices
*   **Rationale:** If the gain gap is a collapse in precision, practices that systematically train the maintenance of high interoceptive and proprioceptive precision (e.g., body-scanning meditation, specific forms of yoga/Pilates that emphasise internal state awareness rather than just strength) should counteract the collapse.
*   **Protocol:** Longitudinal study of adolescents engaged in interoceptive training vs. standard physical education.
*   **Expected Effect:** A lower incidence or slower progression of AIS in the interoceptive training group, independent of muscle strength gains.
*   **Distinguishing Feature:** Explains why "postural awareness" might be therapeutic even without structural bracing or brute-force muscle strengthening.

## Prediction 5: Attenuated Mismatch Negativity (MMN)
*   **Rationale:** EEG signatures like Mismatch Negativity (MMN) are electrophysiological markers of prediction error precision.
*   **Protocol:** Record EEG during an oddball paradigm involving passive proprioceptive perturbations (e.g., robotic manipulation of a joint).
*   **Expected Effect:** AIS patients will show an attenuated MMN amplitude in response to proprioceptive deviants compared to controls, directly reflecting the down-weighted precision ($\Pi$) on these prediction errors.
*   **Distinguishing Feature:** Provides a direct neural correlate for the mathematical variable $\Pi_{y'}$, linking the spinal deformity to a specific cortical predictive processing signature.
