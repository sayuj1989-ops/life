# LBX1 Mechanosensor Falsifiability Plan

## Overview
This document outlines explicit criteria and experiments required to falsify the hypothesis that LBX1 acts as a mechanosensor or structural tension transducer within the Biological Countercurvature framework. Based on recent structural assessments, LBX1 is a low-confidence, intermediate-anisotropy candidate with high domain blockiness, challenging its narrative as a simple rigid tension rod.

## Experiment 1: Single-Molecule Force Spectroscopy (smFS) of LBX1 Domains
**Hypothesis:** If LBX1's "blocky" PAE regions correspond to functional mechanical springs (tension-gated scaffolds), they will exhibit discrete, force-dependent unfolding steps under tension.
*   **Assay Design:** Use optical tweezers or AFM to apply controlled pulling forces to recombinant LBX1 constructs, focusing on the predicted flexible linkers separating globular domains.
*   **Quantitative Readout:** Unfolding force (pN) and step size (nm) at physiological loading rates.
*   **Expected Direction:** Distinct unfolding events occurring at forces physiologically relevant to the nuclear/cytoskeletal environment (e.g., 5-20 pN).
*   **Falsification Threshold:** If LBX1 exhibits continuous, featureless force-extension behavior indistinguishable from a random coil (unstructured IDR) without discrete load-bearing steps, the "tension spring" hypothesis is falsified.

## Experiment 2: LINC Complex Uncoupling (Nuclear Tension Deprivation)
**Hypothesis:** If LBX1 requires nuclear tension (transmitted via LMNA and the LINC complex) for proper localization and transcriptional activity, then decoupling this tension will impair LBX1 function.
*   **Assay Design:** In C2C12 or neural progenitor cells, disrupt the LINC complex via dominant-negative KASH domain overexpression or LMNA knockdown (siRNA).
*   **Quantitative Readout:** Ratio of nuclear to cytoplasmic LBX1 localization (via immunofluorescence/fractionation) and transcriptional activity of known LBX1 target genes (via qPCR or reporter assay).
*   **Expected Direction:** Significant reduction in nuclear localization and target gene expression under tension-deprived conditions compared to controls.
*   **Falsification Threshold:** If LBX1 maintains normal nuclear localization and full transcriptional activity despite complete LINC complex disruption/LMNA knockdown, its functional dependence on nuclear tension is falsified.

## Experiment 3: Domain Truncation and Rescue in Microgravity
**Hypothesis:** If the high PAE "blocky" regions (flexible linkers) are essential for LBX1's specific function under altered gravity, removing them will abolish its gravity-sensitive response.
*   **Assay Design:** Expose cells expressing wild-type LBX1 versus a truncated LBX1 mutant (lacking the putative flexible linkers, creating a rigid/monolithic variant) to simulated microgravity (Random Positioning Machine) and 1G control.
*   **Quantitative Readout:** Fold-change in LBX1-driven transcriptional activity (Microgravity vs 1G) for both WT and mutant.
*   **Expected Direction:** WT LBX1 shows a robust gravity-dependent shift in activity, whereas the rigid mutant loses this sensitivity.
*   **Falsification Threshold:** If the truncated, rigid LBX1 mutant exhibits the exact same gravity-dependent fold-change in activity as the WT, the hypothesis that the specific "blocky" architecture is the mechanosensory element is falsified.
