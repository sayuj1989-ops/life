# Next Step Evidence Summary: Countercurvature Hypothesis

## 1. Strengthened Evidence Base (Since Baseline)
Our rigorous audit and confidence-weighted analysis have solidified several pillars of the Biological Countercurvature hypothesis:

- **Confirmed "Tension Rods":** We have high-confidence structural validation (Anisotropy > 3.0, pLDDT > 70) for **PIEZO2** (4.44), **LMNA** (4.75), and **ADGRG6** (3.06). These proteins are structurally capable of acting as long-range mechanical sensors or stiffeners.
- **Metabolic Anisotropy Signal:** **GHR** (Growth Hormone Receptor) exhibits high anisotropy (5.13), supporting the idea that metabolic supply lines may be geometrically constrained, though confidence is lower (Tier 2).
- **Baseline Integrity:** The dataset from **2026-02-16** is confirmed as a fresh, non-redundant computation event, providing a reliable foundation for these conclusions.

## 2. Weaknesses & Falsifications (To Address)
The analysis also highlighted critical weaknesses in the current narrative:

- **LBX1 is NOT a Rod:** With Anisotropy ~2.27 and Blockiness ~7.35 (Tier 4), LBX1 resembles a standard globular/multi-domain protein. The hypothesis that LBX1 physically buckles or stiffens the spine is **unsupported by structural data**. It likely acts downstream as a transcriptional regulator.
- **Artifactual Candidates:** High-anisotropy scores for **POC5** (24.7) and **COL1A1** (2.8) are driven by low-confidence regions (pLDDT < 65) or intrinsic disorder/repeats. These should be treated as potential artifacts, not confirmed structures.
- **Data Redundancy:** The "daily refresh" pipeline frequently re-processes identical inputs (e.g., Feb 11-13, Jan 20-21), creating a false sense of activity.

## 3. Top 3 High-Leverage Next Experiments
To resolve the LBX1 paradox and validate the "Metabolic Buckling" mechanism, we propose the following prioritized experiments:

### Experiment 1: The "Strain-Silence" Test (Transcriptional)
**Hypothesis:** LBX1 is a metabolic sensor, not a mechanosensor.
**Action:** Subject LBX1-expressing progenitors to 10% cyclic strain.
**Falsification:** If LBX1 levels/localization change >1.5x (like YAP/TAZ), the "Pure Metabolic" hypothesis is weakened. If unchanged, the "Structural Rod" hypothesis is falsified.

### Experiment 2: The "Ghost Rod" Test (Structural Stiffness)
**Hypothesis:** LBX1 does not contribute to cellular stiffness.
**Action:** Measure Young's Modulus of LBX1-overexpressing cells via AFM.
**Falsification:** If stiffness increases >0.5 kPa, the "Globular Regulatory" model is challenged. Null result confirms LBX1 is non-structural.

### Experiment 3: The "Metabolic Rescue" (Functional)
**Hypothesis:** Energy deficit drives scoliosis, not just mechanics.
**Action:** Treat "unloaded" organoids with NAD+ precursors (NR/NMN).
**Falsification:** If NAD+ rescues curvature/LBX1 levels despite continued unloading, the "Metabolic Origin" is confirmed, creating a strong therapeutic lead.

## 4. Conclusion
The "Tension Rod" model holds for PIEZO2 and LMNA but fails for LBX1. The manuscript should pivot to frame **LBX1 as a metabolic victim** of energy deficit, rather than a mechanical culprit. Future work must focus on the *functional* link between metabolic supply (GHR) and transcriptional output (LBX1), rather than structural mechanics of LBX1 itself.
