# Next Step Evidence Summary

## Executive Summary
This report summarizes the current evidentiary state of the Biological Countercurvature hypothesis, separating robust structural signals from speculative narratives. Recent audits have clarified the confidence landscape, particularly regarding the core candidate LBX1.

## What is Stronger Now Than Baseline
- **Clear Separation of Confidence:** We have systematically decoupled high-anisotropy candidates into high-confidence (e.g., PIEZO2, FBLN5, STOML3) and low-confidence (e.g., POC5, GHR) tiers (`outputs/afcc/confidence_weighted_ranking.csv`, 2026-04-05).
- **Static Artifact Identification:** The freshness audit (`reports/evidence_freshness_audit.md`, 2026-04-05) confirmed that repeated narrative updates regarding LBX1 and others were based on static, unchanging structural inputs, halting the inflation of computational claims.
- **Robust Anchors:** Candidates like PIEZO2 (Anisotropy 4.44, pLDDT 79.4) provide much stronger anchors for geometry-driven mechanosensing models than LBX1.

## What Remains Weak (Evidence Against/Weakening the Hypothesis)
- **LBX1 Structural Certainty:** LBX1 remains an intermediate-anisotropy (2.27), low-confidence (pLDDT 66.9) structure. Claims that its predicted blockiness definitively acts as a functional mechanical module are not strongly supported by current AFCC data alone.
- **High-Anisotropy Outliers:** Candidates with extreme anisotropy (e.g., POC5: 24.69, GHR: 5.13) have very low confidence scores (pLDDT < 65). Their extended geometries may represent artifacts of modeling unstructured regions rather than true physiological tension rods.
- **Narrative Over-Interpretation:** The lack of metric evolution across runs indicates that the computational pipeline has exhausted its discovery potential for these specific structures without new experimental inputs.

## Top 3 Highest-Leverage Next Experiments
1. **Nuclear Tension Modulator Assay for LBX1:** Directly test whether LBX1 nuclear localization and clustering dynamically respond to applied mechanical strain or LINC complex disruption in vitro.
2. **Biophysical Validation of "Tension Rods":** Perform SAXS or smFRET on purified high-anisotropy outliers (like POC5) and LBX1 to determine if the predicted extended/blocky states exist in solution or if they represent random coils.
3. **Orthogonal Mechanosensor Screening:** Shift focus toward high-confidence, high-anisotropy candidates (like FBLN5, STOML3) via proteomics localization and perturbation phenotypes to build a more solid foundation for the Countercurvature model.
