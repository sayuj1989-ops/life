# Executive Summary: Evidence Evolution & Next Steps

## 1. What is stronger now than baseline
- **Confidence-Weighted Signal Separation**: By formally separating structural candidates by pLDDT confidence (Adequate vs. Low), we have distilled a highly credible Tier 1 list of putative structural transducers (CNNM2, FBLN5, PIEZO2, STOML3) that are defensible for publication.
- **Data Provenance Transparency**: The Evidence Freshness Audit confirms that core AlphaFold metrics for key genes (LBX1, PIEZO2, LMNA) were completely static between Jan 09 and Feb 16, 2026. This allows us to cleanly separate empirical structural data from narrative hypothesis-generation, preventing the illusion of accumulating evidence from repeated static measurements.
- **Falsifiability Framework**: We have established a concrete falsifiability plan for LBX1, shifting its role from an assumed structural transducer to a formally testable hypothesis with defined quantitative failure modes.

## 2. What remains weak
- **The "High-Anisotropy" Artifact Risk**: Candidates like POC5 and GHR show massive anisotropy but low pLDDT. Without orthogonal validation, relying on AlphaFold for these extended, likely disordered geometries remains a major vulnerability in the current hypothesis structure.
- **LBX1 Structural Role**: The data strongly indicates LBX1 is not a rigid structural rod (Anisotropy ~2.27, pLDDT ~66.9). The narrative heavily relies on its "PAE blockiness" to infer a modular load-bearing role, but this is a speculative interpretation of low-confidence predictions.
- **Causal Linking**: While we have identified potential structural players, the causal link between these specific geometries and the onset of the *Energy Deficit Window* or *Information-Elasticity Coupling* failure remains entirely theoretical.

## 3. Top 3 Highest-Leverage Next Experiments
1. **Single-Molecule Force Spectroscopy of LBX1**: Directly measure the force-extension curve and persistence length of LBX1 to test if its high PAE blockiness/low pLDDT actually corresponds to a tension-responsive structural domain, or merely a random coil.
2. **Nuclear Localization / Tension Assay**: Subject cells to cyclical mechanical strain and measure the nuclear-to-cytoplasmic ratio and chromatin binding affinity of LBX1 and Tier 1 candidates (e.g., CNNM2) to validate dynamic mechanosensory function.
3. **In Vivo Rescue with Tier 1 Anchors**: Use an animal model of scoliotic progression (e.g., driven by LBX1 or generalized ECM deficit) and attempt to rescue the phenotype by overexpressing a verified Tier 1 structural anchor (e.g., PIEZO2), testing the modularity of the mechanosensory failure hypothesis.
