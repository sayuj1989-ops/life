# Cluster 2: High-Anisotropy Mechanotransduction Scaffold

## Cluster Members
- **PIEZO2**: Mechanotransduction, Proprioception
- **LMNA**: Mechanotransduction, Nucleus, Cytoskeleton
- **PLOD1**: ECM, Enzyme

## Shared Properties
This cluster is characterized by high structural anisotropy (mean = 4.20) and exceptionally low structural disorder (mean = 0.14). The high anisotropy and low disorder suggest rigid, extended, rod-like configurations capable of robustly transmitting mechanical force across cellular compartments without dissipating energy through disordered hinges.

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
This cluster likely forms a contiguous, tension-bearing structural scaffold that spans from the extracellular matrix (PLOD1) across the cell membrane (PIEZO2) and deep into the nucleus (LMNA). This 'mechanosensory wire' allows instantaneous transmission of macroscopic tissue strain (such as spinal loading) directly into nuclear mechanotransduction pathways, effectively bypassing slower, diffusion-based biochemical signaling.

## Concrete Test
**Cell/Tissue/Phenotype:** Cultured patient-derived osteoblasts or chondrocytes.
**Perturbation:** Apply uniaxial cyclic stretch using a Flexcell system.
**Measurement:** Quantify the instantaneous force transmission to the nucleus (via FRET tension sensors on LMNA) and the resulting downstream mechanotransduction output (e.g., YAP/TAZ nuclear localization or calcium transients via PIEZO2). Compare responses before and after targeted disruption of the ECM-membrane linkage (e.g., using collagenase to perturb the PLOD1-mediated ECM network).
