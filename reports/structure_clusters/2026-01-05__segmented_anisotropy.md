## Shared Properties
The members of this cluster share a distinct "Segmented Cable" topology. Unlike Vimentin (VIM), which appears to be a continuous rigid strut (Coiled-coil), CDH2 and ITGB1 are highly elongated but punctuated by numerous flexible regions (hinges).
*   **CDH2**: The hinges likely correspond to the inter-domain linkers between its 5 Extracellular Cadherin (EC) domains.
*   **Mechanics**: This structure resembles a "chain" rather than a "rod".

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
**"Low-Pass Mechanical Filtering"**

We hypothesize that the "Segmented Anisotropy" of CDH2 allows it to act as a mechanical filter.
*   **Tension Transmission:** Under static load (gravity/growth tension), the chain pulls taut and transmits alignment information effectively along its principal axis (High Anisotropy).
*   **Noise Damping:** Under high-frequency dynamic perturbations (fluid flow, rapid motility), the flexible hinges dissipate energy, preventing noise from disrupting the long-range alignment signal.
*   **Relevance to Spine:** Proper spinal alignment requires sensing the static vector of gravity amidst the noise of developmental growth. CDH2 (critical for neural tube closure and somite alignment) provides the necessary mechanical "quietness" for this signal.

## Concrete Test
**Perturbation:** Modify the inter-domain linkers of CDH2 in a zebrafish model.
*   **Stiffening:** Replace flexible linkers with rigid alpha-helical spacers to create a "monolithic" CDH2.
*   **Prediction:** The "monolithic" CDH2 will transmit high-frequency mechanical noise, preventing the subtle gravity-alignment signal from being resolved. The spine will fail to straighten or will exhibit "jittery" defects (kinks) rather than a smooth curve.
*   **Phenotype:** High-frequency spinal curvature (short wavelength), distinct from the long-wavelength curves of scoliosis.
