# Cluster Note: The Tension-Dependent Supply Gating Hypothesis
**Date:** 2026-03-07
**Cluster:** High-Anisotropy Integrators
**Members:** PIEZO2, LMNA, PLOD1, GHR
**Anisotropy Range:** ~4.43 (High)

## Shared Properties
- **Structural:** Highly anisotropic (> 4.0), extended geometries with low intrinsic disorder (< 0.25).
- **Functional:** Enriched for terms related to physical structure and strain-sensing: Mechanotransduction, Proprioception, Nucleus, Cytoskeleton, ECM.
- **Context:** These proteins span the entire spatial scale of mechanotransduction, from extracellular cross-linking (PLOD1), to cell surface/membrane mechanosensing (PIEZO2, GHR), down to the nuclear envelope (LMNA).

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
**The Tension-Dependent Supply Gating Hypothesis:**
This cluster represents an integrated "tension-bearing structural network" where growth signaling (GHR) and structural maintenance (PLOD1, LMNA) are physically gated by the same vector-aligned cytoskeletal tension that PIEZO2 detects.

Because GHR shares the high-anisotropy geometry of classical mechanosensors (PIEZO2) and load-bearing elements (LMNA, PLOD1), we hypothesize that **GHR is intrinsically mechanosensitive**. Specifically, the extended conformation of GHR requires uniaxial tension (vector-aligned loading) to optimally bind growth hormone and initiate the pubertal growth spurt. In microgravity or scoliotic unloading, the loss of this tension causes GHR to entropically coil, creating a "Tension-Gated Supply Bottleneck." This prevents spinal elongation from outpacing the structural load-bearing capacity, acting as a built-in safety mechanism against the Energy Deficit.

**Test: The GHR Strain-Gating Assay**
1. **System:** Primary human chondrocytes or osteoblasts in a 3D mechanically tunable hydrogel (e.g., GelMA).
2. **Perturbation:** Apply static uniaxial stretch (10% strain) vs. unloaded control.
