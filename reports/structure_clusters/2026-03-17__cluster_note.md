# Cluster Structure Analysis: The Trans-Sarcolemmal Tension Scaffold

**Date:** 2026-03-17

## 1. Cluster Identification
We identified a distinct structural cluster (Cluster 2) from the AlphaFold Counter-Curvature metrics.

**Cluster Members:**
- **MYLK** (Myosin light chain kinase)
- **DMD** (Dystrophin)
- **DAG1** (Dystroglycan 1)
- **PPARGC1A** (Peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor gamma coactivator 1-alpha)
- **IGF1R** (Insulin-like growth factor 1 receptor)
- **JAG1** (Jagged-1)
- **NTRK3** (Neurotrophic receptor tyrosine kinase 3)

## 2. Shared Properties
This cluster is characterized by **high PAE domain blockiness (average ~6.53)** and **moderate structural anisotropy (average ~1.92)**. The high blockiness indicates the presence of multiple, well-folded, rigid domains separated by flexible hinge regions, while the moderate anisotropy suggests these proteins form extended but somewhat compact shapes, rather than highly elongated rods.

## 3. Hypothesized Mechanical Role
Given the inclusion of DMD, DAG1, and MYLK, this cluster represents a key **trans-sarcolemmal tension-bearing scaffold**. The high blockiness enables a "shock absorber" or "spring-loaded" functionality. Rigid domains can firmly anchor to the extracellular matrix (via DAG1) and the intracellular actomyosin cytoskeleton (via DMD and MYLK), while the flexible inter-domain hinges buffer transient mechanical spikes (e.g., during rapid adolescent growth spurts and daily gravitational loading).

In the context of the Biological Counter-Curvature framework, these proteins act as local structural capacitors. If their hinge regions or binding domains are disrupted, the paraspinal musculature loses its ability to buffer asymmetric mechanical noise, transmitting unmitigated stress to the developing spine and triggering scoliotic buckling.

## 4. Concrete Test
**Phenotype / Tissue:** Paraspinal myotubes derived from patient biopsies.
**Test:** Cultivate patient-derived paraspinal myotubes containing *DMD* or *DAG1* structural variants (specifically in the flexible hinge regions between blocky domains) on flexible silicone substrates. Subject them to cyclical mechanical stretch mimicking adolescent growth loads. Measure focal adhesion turnover rates, sarcolemmal membrane integrity (e.g., via dye uptake assays), and intracellular calcium transients.
**Prediction:** Myotubes with variants in the "tension-buffer" domains will exhibit premature membrane micro-tears, accelerated focal adhesion failure, and dysregulated calcium signaling compared to controls under identical strain.
