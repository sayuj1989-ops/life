# Structure Hypothesis: Cluster 2

## Cluster Members
- MYLK
- PPARGC1A
- IGF1R
- DMD
- DAG1
- JAG1
- NTRK3

## Shared Properties
- Low/Moderate Anisotropy (Avg: 1.92)
- High PAE Blockiness (Avg: 6.53)

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
The structural metrics (high PAE blockiness with low/moderate anisotropy) indicate multi-domain proteins with highly structured, distinct folded regions that pack relatively compactly rather than adopting extended linear conformations. Given the presence of DMD (dystrophin), DAG1 (dystroglycan), and MYLK, this cluster is strongly associated with **ECM anchoring and tension transmission**. We hypothesize that the distinct rigid blocks (high PAE blockiness) act as force-transmitting nodules that mechanically couple the extracellular matrix to the intracellular actomyosin cytoskeleton. Under mechanical loading, these distinct blocks may shift relative to one another (domain-level conformational changes) to expose cryptic binding sites or activate downstream kinase activity, thereby adapting the spine's structural stiffness to gravitational loads.

## Concrete Test
Perform a live-cell FRET (Förster Resonance Energy Transfer) assay using tension-sensor modules inserted between the distinct folded domains of DMD or DAG1 in patient-derived AIS osteoblasts vs. healthy controls. Measure the FRET efficiency changes in response to cyclic uniaxial stretch to test if the domain-to-domain tension transmission is disrupted in scoliotic cells.
