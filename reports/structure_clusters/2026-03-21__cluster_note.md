# Cluster Note: Tension-Bearing Strain Anchors
**Date:** 2026-03-21

## Cluster Members
- PIEZO2
- LMNA
- PLOD1

## Shared Properties
- **Structural:** High Anisotropy (~4.20) and Low Intrinsic Disorder (~0.14).
- **Functional:** Enriched for mechanotransduction, nucleus, cytoskeleton, and ECM.

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
**Tension-Bearing Strain Anchors**
These highly anisotropic proteins act as rigid, tension-bearing structural links that transmit and resist mechanical strain from the extracellular matrix to the nucleus and within sensory structures. They form the physical hardware necessary to convert tissue-level forces (gravity) into cellular signals (mechanotransduction) without dissipating energy through disorder.

## Concrete Test
**The Anisotropic De-anchoring Test**
Apply cyclic uniaxial stretch (10%, 1Hz) to human paraspinal fibroblasts in a 3D collagen matrix. Use siRNA to individually knockdown PIEZO2, LMNA, or PLOD1. Measure the loss of cellular alignment and the reduction in tension-induced YAP nuclear translocation compared to control cells. If these proteins act as tension-bearing anchors, their loss will prevent the cell from sensing and orienting against the applied vector strain.
