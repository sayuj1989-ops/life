## Shared Properties
The proteins in this cluster are characterized by extremely high anisotropy (mean 4.20) indicating elongated structures, and very low disorder (mean 0.14) indicating high rigidity. These properties suggest they function as rigid structural struts rather than flexible tethers. They are located at critical mechanical boundaries: the extracellular matrix (PLOD1), the cell membrane (PIEZO2), and the nuclear envelope (LMNA).

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
**Rigid Mechanical Syncytium:** These proteins may form a continuous, rigid mechanical syncytium spanning from the extracellular matrix down to the nuclear chromatin. Their high anisotropy and low disorder allow them to act as physical struts that instantaneously transmit macroscopic tissue-level forces (like gravity or postural loading) directly to the nucleus, bypassing the slower biochemical signaling cascades to enable rapid mechanotransduction.

Apply a controlled mechanical point-load (using Atomic Force Microscopy) to the ECM and measure the propagation speed of the force to the nucleus, as well as the temporal delay in the activation of stretch-induced mechanosensitive genes.
