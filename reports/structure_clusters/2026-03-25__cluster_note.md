# Structure Cluster Note: 2026-03-25

## Cluster Identification
**Cluster ID:** 3
**Members:** LBX1, YAP1
**Top Pathways:** Somite, Muscle, Proprioception, Mechanotransduction, Hippo

## Shared Properties
Cluster 3 represents a distinct class of proteins characterized by:
- **Low Length:** Mean length of 392.50 residues.
- **Lower Confidence/High Disorder:** Mean pLDDT of 62.13 and a high disorder fraction proxy of 0.36.
- **Low Anisotropy:** Mean anisotropy index of 4.55.
- **Low Domain Complexity:** Mean predicted domain segments of 2.00.

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
Unlike high-anisotropy structural "antennas" (e.g., PIEZO1/2), the members of Cluster 3 (LBX1, YAP1) exhibit significant intrinsically disordered regions (IDRs). These proteins likely function as **dynamic mechanosensors or "mechano-buffers"**. Their high disorder allows them to undergo fluid conformational changes under mechanical load or tension during somite and muscle development. Rather than rigid force transmission, they may phase-separate or alter their binding kinetics under mechanical stress, dynamically gating Hippo signaling and proprioceptive development in response to the changing gravitational/loading environment of the developing spine.

## Concrete Test
To test this hypothesis, we can selectively delete the major intrinsically disordered regions (IDRs) in YAP1 and LBX1 while preserving their known folded binding domains. If these IDRs function as fluid mechanosensors, then *in vitro* cultures of somitic/muscle progenitor cells expressing the IDR-deleted variants will lose their ability to scale differentiation and alignment in response to varied uniaxial cyclic stretching, despite retaining baseline transcriptional or signaling capabilities in static conditions.
