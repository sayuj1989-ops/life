# Structure Hypothesis: Cilia Scaffold Mechanotransduction via IFT88

## Cluster Members
- IFT88

## Shared Properties
- Anisotropy Index: 7.86
- Length: 824.00
- Elongation Proxy: 2.61
- Domain Segments: 3
- Enriched Pathways: Cilia, Mechanotransduction

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
The high elongation proxy and distinct domain segmentation suggest IFT88 functions as a tension-bearing scaffold within the primary cilium. The cilium acts as an antenna for mechanotransduction in response to fluid flow or tissue strain. IFT88's structural anisotropy may allow it to deform under mechanical load, thereby modulating the transport rate of signaling complexes (like Hedgehog or Wnt components) along the axoneme. In the context of the spine, mechanical loading from gravity might be sensed through this cilia scaffold, leading to downstream mechanotransduction.

## Concrete Test
Perform a cell-stretching or fluid shear assay on osteochondroprogenitor cells or chondrocytes with and without IFT88 knockdown (or specific point mutations disrupting its highly anisotropic domains). Measure the resulting change in mechanosensitive downstream signaling (e.g., nuclear localization of YAP/TAZ or primary cilia deflection angle). If IFT88 acts as a mechanical scaffold, structural perturbation should blunt the pathway's responsiveness to applied strain.
