## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
Given the high elongation and presence in both cilia and mechanotransduction pathways, IFT88 is hypothesized to act as a **Cilia Scaffold**, playing a critical role in transferring mechanical load from the extracellular fluid flow into the intracellular cytoskeleton. Its extended multimodular structure provides the mechanical leverage necessary to amplify minute bending moments in primary cilia.

## Concrete Test
Isolate patient-derived osteoblasts or chondrocytes and introduce an IFT88 structural mutation (or partial knockdown). Expose the cells to cyclic fluid flow or mechanical strain in a bioreactor. Measure the downstream mechanotransduction markers (e.g., intracellular calcium influx, YAP/TAZ nuclear translocation) and assess overall cellular stiffness using atomic force microscopy (AFM). The prediction is that disrupting the IFT88 scaffold will uncouple fluid flow sensing from intracellular mechanical signaling.
