## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
The high disorder combined with moderate anisotropy suggests these proteins act as **Disordered Tension-Sensors**. Rather than rigid levers (like PIEZO1/2), the disordered regions serve as flexible, strain-transmitting tethers that can unfold or stretch under mechanical load, exposing cryptic binding sites or altering complex stability. This allows them to couple cytoskeletal tension directly to transcriptional programs (YAP1 in Hippo signaling) or somite/muscle developmental fate (LBX1).

## Concrete Test
Engineer YAP1 or LBX1 variants where the intrinsically disordered regions (IDRs) are replaced with rigid, length-matched alpha-helical linkers. Culture muscle precursor cells on substrates of varying stiffness and apply cyclic uniaxial stretch. Measure:
1. Nuclear localization efficiency of YAP1 under load.
2. Expression of downstream somite/muscle differentiation markers (e.g., *MyoD*, *Pax3*).
**Expected Refutation:** If the rigid linker variants exhibit normal load-induced signaling and differentiation, then the disordered tethers are not essential for their mechanosensory function.
