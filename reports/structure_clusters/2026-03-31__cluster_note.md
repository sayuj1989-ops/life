# Cluster-Derived Hypothesis: PIEZO2 and ACVR1 in Proprioceptive Tension-Sensing

**Date**: 2026-03-31

- **ACVR1**: Anisotropy=3.41, Disorder=0.12, Tags=Signaling,Mechanotransduction,Bone

## Shared Properties
- High structural anisotropy (elongation/asymmetry).
- Low disorder fraction, indicating rigid, ordered domains.
- Enriched for mechanotransduction and proprioceptive tags.

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
Given their highly anisotropic structures and structural rigidity, we hypothesize that the PIEZO2 and ACVR1 complex acts as a primary **tension-bearing mechanosensor** at the osteotendinous junctions. The high anisotropy suggests an elongated shape capable of spanning structural deformations, while the low disorder provides a rigid lever-arm to translate macroscopic tissue strain into molecular channel opening (PIEZO2) and downstream morphogen signaling (ACVR1) necessary for spinal alignment control.

## Concrete Test
Expose ex vivo spine segments to cyclic tensile loading and measure the co-localization and activation of PIEZO2 and ACVR1. Knock down ACVR1 in paraspinal muscle-tendon junctions of mice and evaluate if PIEZO2 strain sensitivity (measured via calcium imaging) and subsequent spinal alignment (Cobb angle) are compromised during rapid adolescent growth.
