## Shared Properties
Cluster 2 exhibits exceptionally high anisotropy (mean ~4.20) and low disorder (mean ~0.14), with strong enrichment for Mechanotransduction, Proprioception, Nucleus, Cytoskeleton, and ECM. These proteins share an elongated, semi-rigid "tension rod" architecture.

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
These high-anisotropy "tension rods" act as an interconnected mechanosensory scaffold, physically bridging the extracellular matrix (PLOD1), the plasma membrane (PIEZO2), and the nuclear envelope (LMNA). This structural continuum transmits macroscopic mechanical stress directly into nuclear deformation, serving as the primary strain sensor for gravitational loading.

## Concrete Test

Compare the FRET efficiency under static 1G loading versus simulated microgravity (clinorotation). The hypothesis predicts a simultaneous reduction in tension across both PIEZO2 and LMNA upon unloading, demonstrating their physical coupling.
