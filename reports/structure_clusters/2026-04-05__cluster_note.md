# 2026-04-05 Cluster Note: Mechanotransduction Chain

## Cluster Members
- **PIEZO2** (Anisotropy=4.44, Disorder=0.14)
- **LMNA** (Anisotropy=4.75, Disorder=0.26)
- **PLOD1** (Anisotropy=3.40, Disorder=0.03)

## Shared Properties
- **High Anisotropy (Mean = 4.20):** Suggests extended, rod-like, or spring-like conformations capable of transmitting force over distances.
- **Low Disorder (Mean = 0.14):** Indicates rigid, structured domains rather than flexible tethers.
- **Pathway Tags:** Mechanotransduction, Proprioception, Nucleus, Cytoskeleton, ECM.

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
These proteins are hypothesized to act as a **tension-bearing mechanotransduction chain**. Rather than acting independently, they may form a continuous mechanical axis linking the extracellular matrix (PLOD1, which modifies collagen crosslinking) through the plasma membrane mechanosensor (PIEZO2) and the cytoskeleton down to the nuclear envelope (LMNA). This extended structural network would allow immediate, physical transmission of macroscopic spinal strain to the nucleus to regulate gene expression for structural remodeling.

## Concrete Test (Cell/Tissue/Phenotype)
- **Experiment:** Isolate osteoblasts or bone marrow stromal cells (BMSCs) from wild-type and AIS patient models.
- **Intervention:** Apply controlled cyclic mechanical tension (using a flexible silicone substrate).
- **Measurement:** Measure immediate nuclear deformation (via live-cell imaging of LMNA), PIEZO2 activation (calcium flux), and subsequent downstream matrix production. Use siRNA to knock down PLOD1, PIEZO2, or LMNA individually to verify if breaking one link abolishes the nuclear strain response and ECM remodeling.
