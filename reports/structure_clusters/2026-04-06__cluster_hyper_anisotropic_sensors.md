# Cluster Report: Hyper-Anisotropic Tension Antennas
**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Source:** Cluster 2 (from outputs/afcc/ metrics)

## Cluster Members
*   **POC5** (Cilia, Centriole, Anisotropy: ~23)
*   **CDH23** (Gravity_Sensing, Mechanotransduction, Anisotropy: ~23)
*   **TPM2** (Muscle, Cytoskeleton, Anisotropy: ~23)

## Shared Properties
1.  **Extreme Anisotropy:** With a mean anisotropy index > 23, these proteins are remarkably extended, far exceeding typical fibrous or structural proteins (which usually range from 2 to 5).
2.  **Trans-cellular Linkages:** They reside in specialized structural contexts (tip links in stereocilia for CDH23, centriolar extensions for POC5, and actin stabilization for TPM2) where they bridge significant spatial gaps.
3.  **Directional Sensitivity:** Their functional pathways are exquisitely tuned to vectorial forces (gravity/acceleration for CDH23, flow/cilia bending for POC5, contraction vectors for TPM2).

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
**"Hyper-Anisotropic Tension Antennas act as Unidirectional Strain Relays"**

We hypothesize that due to their extreme aspect ratios, these proteins act as "Tension Antennas" that selectively transmit stress along a single defined axis while remaining compliant to orthogonal forces. Unlike bulk ECM or cross-linked cytoskeleton which distributes force in 3D, these proteins act like nanoscale "guy-wires" or "tip-links."

In the context of spinal development and gravity, fluid shear or tissue-level buckling (scoliosis) exerts mechanical forces. If these highly elongated proteins are aligned with the principal loading axis (gravity), they efficiently transmit tension to open channels (e.g., mechanotransduction via CDH23) or organize organelles (POC5). A loss of this directional pre-tension in microgravity, or an architectural mismatch during rapid growth, "slacks" these antennas, silencing the directional mechanosensory signal required to maintain straight spinal growth.

## Concrete Test
**In Vitro Microfluidic Vector Alignment Assay:**
1.  **System:** Cultured vestibular hair cells (for CDH23) or ciliated cells (for POC5) in a microfluidic chamber.
2.  **Perturbation:** Apply steady, pulsatile, and orthogonal fluid shear stress using the microfluidic system.
3.  **Readout:** Use live-cell super-resolution microscopy (STED or SIM) with fluorescent tagging to measure the 3D angular variance (alignment) of the protein's longitudinal axis relative to the flow vector.
4.  **Prediction:** Hyper-anisotropic proteins will sharply align parallel to the dominant vector of fluid shear. Introduction of a domain-truncation mutant (reducing anisotropy) will significantly broaden the angular variance and decrease downstream mechanosensitive calcium transients.
