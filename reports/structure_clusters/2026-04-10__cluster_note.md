# Cluster Note: Mechanotransduction & Proprioception Scaffolds
**Date:** 2026-04-10
**Cluster:** Rigid Integrators / Mechanotransduction (Cluster 2)
**Members:** PIEZO2, LMNA

## Shared Properties
- **Structural:** High Anisotropy (Mean: 4.60) indicating highly extended or non-globular architecture.
- **Functional:** Enriched for terms 'Mechanotransduction' (2) and 'Proprioception' (1). Both act to transmit or integrate mechanical forces.

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
**Trans-Cellular Tension Scaffold:**
Given their extreme anisotropy and functional roles, PIEZO2 (a membrane mechanosensor) and LMNA (nuclear lamina) may be physically or functionally coupled across the cell via the cytoskeleton. The high anisotropy suggests they form extended tension-bearing or tension-transmitting scaffolds that relay macroscopic spinal load/curvature directly to nuclear regulation, bypassing slow chemical diffusion.

## Concrete Test
**Test: The Tension-Transmission Integrity Test**
1. **System:** Paraspinal muscle fibroblasts or osteoblasts.
2. **Perturbation:** Disrupt the physical cytoskeletal link between the membrane (PIEZO2) and the nucleus (LMNA) using mild cytochalasin D (actin depolymerization) or specific LINC complex inhibitors.
3. **Readout:** Measure YAP/TAZ nuclear translocation and stretch-induced transcriptional activation under cyclic mechanical stretch.
4. **Prediction:** Decoupling the highly anisotropic components (PIEZO2 and LMNA) will disproportionately reduce the fast, early-phase mechanotransductive signaling gain compared to slower, biochemically mediated pathways.
