# Structure Cluster Hypothesis: Tension-Bearing Anchors
**Date:** 2026-04-11

## Cluster Members
- PIEZO2 (Piezo-type mechanosensitive ion channel component 2)
- ACVR1 (Activin receptor type-1)

## Shared Properties
- **High Anisotropy:** Mean Anisotropy Index = 3.93
- **Low Disorder:** Mean Disorder Fraction = 0.13
- **Enriched Pathways:** Mechanotransduction, Proprioception, Signaling, Bone

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
**Tension-bearing and proprioceptive anchoring.**
The rigid, elongated structural architecture (high anisotropy with low disorder) is hypothesized to act as a highly sensitive mechanical lever or "strain antenna." This rigid structure directly links extracellular matrix (ECM) or cytoskeletal tension to channel gating (PIEZO2) or receptor activation (ACVR1) without relying on large, disordered conformational changes. This structural geometry allows these proteins to transmit precise, high-fidelity mechanical signals necessary for maintaining spinal geometry and responding to gravitational loads.

## Concrete Test
**In vitro mechanical activation under membrane tension.**
Measure mechanically-evoked channel currents (for PIEZO2) and downstream signaling (e.g., SMAD phosphorylation for ACVR1) in human osteoblasts or chondrocytes. Apply precise degrees of localized membrane tension using high-speed atomic force microscopy (HS-AFM) or micropipette aspiration. Compare the mechanosensitivity and activation thresholds of wild-type proteins against truncated mutant variants lacking the rigid, highly anisotropic extramembrane domains.
