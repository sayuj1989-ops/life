# Cluster Note: Mechanotransduction & Proprioception (Cluster 0)

## Cluster Members
- PIEZO2
- ACVR1

## Shared Properties
- **Structural:** High Anisotropy (Mean 3.93) indicating an extended, non-globular conformation, combined with low intrinsic disorder (Mean 0.13), suggesting a rigid extended structure.
- **Functional Enriched Terms:** Mechanotransduction, Proprioception, Signaling, Bone.

## Hypothesized Mechanical Role
**Rigid Extended Levers for Mechanosensory Amplification**

The high anisotropy and low disorder of PIEZO2 and ACVR1 suggest they form rigid extended structural motifs. In the context of mechanotransduction and proprioception, this architecture may function as a lever or a rigid tension-transmitting element, efficiently propagating local mechanical perturbations (e.g., fluid flow, membrane stretch, or matrix deformation) to sensory or signaling domains. This extended rigidity could amplify small mechanical forces, enabling high-sensitivity mechanoperception crucial for bone adaptation and proprioceptive feedback.

## Concrete Test
**The Rigid Lever Test in Chondrocytes/Osteoblasts**
1. **System:** Primary osteoblasts or chondrocytes expressing endogenous PIEZO2 and ACVR1, cultured in 3D matrices.
2. **Perturbation:** Apply high-frequency, low-amplitude mechanical stimulation (vibration) or precise focal mechanical stretch using magnetic tweezers targeting their extracellular or tethered domains, with and without pre-treatment using mild structural destabilizers or specific hinge-introducing domain mutations.
3. **Readout:** Measure immediate calcium influx (for PIEZO2) and downstream Smad signaling (for ACVR1).
4. **Prediction:** Disruption of the extended rigid structure (lowering anisotropy or increasing disorder) will selectively attenuate the signaling response to low-amplitude, high-frequency mechanical perturbations, but not to bulk chemical activation, demonstrating the structural dependence of their mechanical gain.
