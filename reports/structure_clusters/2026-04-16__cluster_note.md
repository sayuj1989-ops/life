## Hypothesized Mechanical Role: Trans-Cellular Tension Cables
The structural convergence of PIEZO2 (membrane-bound mechanosensor) and LMNA (nuclear lamina structural protein) into a high-anisotropy cluster suggests they function as endpoints of a "Trans-Cellular Tension Cable" network. Their high aspect ratio (anisotropy) and relative structural rigidity (low disorder, high pLDDT) make them ideal for bearing and transmitting directional mechanical load from the extracellular environment, across the cell membrane, to the nucleus. Loss of gravitational loading would disproportionately affect these high-anisotropy tension-bearing structures compared to globular signaling proteins.
