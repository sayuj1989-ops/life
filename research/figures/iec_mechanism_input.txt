The Information-Elasticity Coupling (IEC) model posits that developmental information, encoded in HOX genes, acts as a source of effective curvature in biological structures growing against gravity. This process involves three stages: 1. Genetic Patterning: HOX gene expression establishes a spatial information field I(s) along the body axis. 2. Geometric Modification: This information field modifies the effective metric of the body, creating a 'biological countercurvature' that opposes gravitational loading. 3. Morphogenesis: The physical structure (e.g., the spine) grows and deforms to minimize energy in this effective metric, resulting in a stable, curved geometry (like the S-curve) that is mechanically robust.
