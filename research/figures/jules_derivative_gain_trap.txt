The Derivative Gain Trap mechanism visualizes how the adolescent growth spurt converts essential damping mechanisms into destabilizing factors, causing Metabolic Buckling. During childhood, proprioceptive feedback provides stable derivative gain, dampening mechanical perturbations. However, rapid spinal elongation ($L$) increases the absolute neural transmission delay ($\tau$). As $\tau$ crosses a critical threshold, the previously stable derivative gain undergoes a supercritical Hopf bifurcation. This delay-induced instability turns the corrective feedback forces into a destabilizing, scoliotic buckling mechanism, trapped by the high metabolic cost of shape maintenance against gravity.
