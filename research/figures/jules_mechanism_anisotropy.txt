The "Demand-Supply Anisotropy" feedback loop in mechanotransduction.
AlphaFold structural analysis of 23 mechanotransduction proteins reveals a 72% demand-supply anisotropy gap.
During adolescent growth (when length L > 0.35m), the physical distance and scale outpace the metabolic supply of mechanosensory proteins (like Piezo2).
This creates an "Energy Deficit Window" where the spine's active control cannot match the gravitational demand, leading to thermodynamic instability and buckling (scoliosis) as an energy-minimization strategy.
