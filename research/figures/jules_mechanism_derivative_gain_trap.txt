A flow diagram illustrating the "Derivative Gain Trap" hypothesis of Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS).

Components:
1. Pre-Adolescent State: Stable Control
   - Input: Proprioceptive feedback with small neural delay ($\tau$).
   - Process: High damping gains stabilize the spinal inverted pendulum.
   - Output: Straight, stable posture.

2. Growth Spurt (Adolescence): The Disturbance
   - Event: Rapid long bone growth elongates the body axis.
   - Consequence: Neural delay ($\tau$) increases significantly.

3. The Trap: Metabolic Buckling
   - Mechanism: Previously stable damping gains become destabilizing under increased delay (Derivative Gain Trap).
   - Bifurcation: The system crosses the critical threshold (Supercritical Hopf Bifurcation).
   - Outcome: Spinal buckling into a scoliotic curve to minimize thermodynamic free energy.
