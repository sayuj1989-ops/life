Title: Mechanism: The Derivative Gain Trap and Metabolic Buckling

Structure: A process flow diagram depicting the transition from stable childhood growth to adolescent scoliotic buckling, driven by delayed neural feedback.

Phase 1: Stable Equilibrium (Childhood)
- A straight, normal spine.
- Information Elasticity Coupling (IEC) is perfectly balanced.
- Small perturbations are corrected rapidly via high Proprioceptive Gain ($K$) with low Neural Delay ($\tau$).

Phase 2: The Onset of the "Derivative Gain Trap" (Adolescent Growth Spurt)
- Spine elongation ($\dot{L}$) accelerates rapidly.
- Neural Conduction Delay ($\tau$) increases due to the physical distance of nerve pathways lengthening.
- The high derivative gain ($K$), previously stabilizing, now acts on outdated positional information.
- A feedback loop visualizes the mismatch: "Gain ($K$) x Delay ($\tau$) > Critical Damping Threshold".

Phase 3: Energy Deficit & Metabolic Buckling
- The geometric cost of maintaining the spine scales as $L^4$, while metabolic supply (NAD+, ATP) scales only as $L^2$.
- This creates an "Energy Deficit Window" ($R_{deficit} > 1$).
- The spine, unable to maintain active damping against the derivative gain trap and lacking metabolic resources, undergoes a Hopf bifurcation.
- Visual: The straight spine buckles into a complex 3D S-curve (scoliosis), transitioning from a dynamic instability to a structurally locked-in deformity.
