Title: Mechanism: The Information-Elasticity Coupling (IEC) and Derivative Gain Trap

Concept: A detailed flow diagram demonstrating how the adolescent growth spurt creates a vulnerable state leading to metabolic buckling in the human spine.

Structure: A multi-scale causal flowchart mapping variables from molecular components to macroscopic clinical outcomes.

Nodes and Flow:
1. "Adolescent Growth Spurt" (Top, initiating event).
2. "Rapid Spine Elongation (L)" and "Nerve Conduction Lag" (branches off the growth spurt).
3. "Increased Neural Delay (\tau)" (results from nerve lag).
4. "The Derivative Gain Trap" (Central convergence node): Shows a non-monotonic curve where previously stable derivative gains (K_d) become destabilizing past a critical delay threshold (\tau*).
5. "Energy Deficit Window" (Side node interacting with the trap): Scaling cost L^4 exceeds supply L^2, depleting NAD+ and weakening the active muscular response.
6. "Metabolic Buckling (AIS Onset)" (Final outcome): Shows a micro-mechanical collapse of the active S-curve into a lateral C-curve due to the combined delay instability and energy deficit.
