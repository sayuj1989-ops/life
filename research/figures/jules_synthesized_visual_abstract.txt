Visual Abstract: Biological Countercurvature of Spacetime

The human spine maintains its unique S-shaped sagittal profile not as a passive mechanical structure, but as an active computational system defying gravity. This theory of "Biological Countercurvature" models the spine as an Information-Cosserat framework where biological shape emerges from active, metabolic shape-maintenance against gravitational load.

Three core pillars define this framework:
1. The Allometric Trap: The human spine operates near a critical Bio-Gravitational Number (Bg ~ 0.01), making shape stability intrinsically dependent on active metabolic control rather than passive stiffness.
2. The Energy Deficit Window: During the adolescent growth spurt, the geometric cost of shape maintenance scales to the fourth power (L^4), whereas metabolic supply scales to the second power (L^2). This creates a transient window of localized energy deficit and NAD+ insufficiency.
3. The Derivative Gain Trap: Neural feedback loops, essential for damping and stability in childhood, are destabilized during rapid growth due to increasing neural delay (\tau). This phase shift turns stabilizing derivative gains into scoliotic buckling forces—triggering Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) through a supercritical Hopf bifurcation.

Conclusion: AIS is not purely mechanical but a metabolic and temporal control failure, suggesting future prevention via targeted metabolic interventions.
