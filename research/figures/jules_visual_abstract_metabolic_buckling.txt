Biological Countercurvature: An Information-Cosserat Framework for Spinal Geometry

The "Biological Countercurvature" framework redefines Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) not as a pathological failure, but as a predictable thermodynamic phase transition—a survival mechanism against gravity.

Three interconnected theoretical pillars drive this mechanism:
1. The Allometric Trap: Humans uniquely occupy a vulnerability zone where the bio-gravitational number (Bg ~ 0.01) dictates an absolute dependence on active metabolic expenditure to maintain shape and prevent collapse.
2. The Energy Deficit Window: The active torque required to maintain vertebral alignment scales as the fourth power of height (L^4), whereas metabolic supply scales as the square of height (L^2). During the adolescent growth spurt, this mismatch opens an "Energy Deficit Window" where energy demand exceeds supply.
3. The Derivative Gain Trap: The rapid spinal elongation increases neural conduction delay (\tau). In control systems, increasing derivative gain with excessive delay flips stable damping into destabilizing buckling.

When the metabolic deficit becomes unsustainable, it triggers the "VIM Cascade," a sequential failure of metabolically expensive mechanosensors (Vimentin -> Lamin A/C -> Piezo2). Deprived of continuous ATP for structural maintenance and proprioceptive feedback, the spine buckles into a helical deformity.

"Metabolic Buckling" is the resulting scoliotic curve—a thermodynamic standing wave that sheds the energy cost the organism can no longer afford, trading geometric perfection for survival.
