Title: Visual Abstract: Biological Countercurvature of Spacetime

Concept: A striking, conceptual cover-art style illustration summarizing the theory of 'Biological Countercurvature'. It portrays the human spine not merely as a bone structure, but as an active, energy-consuming standing wave of information that locally counteracts the gravitational warping of spacetime.

Visual Elements:
- Background: A deep, dark cosmic void featuring a warped 3D spacetime grid (representing a gravitational well).
- The Spine: A glowing, ethereal human spine forming a perfect S-curve (lordosis and kyphosis). It should look cyber-biological, constructed from glowing golden threads of 'Information' or genetic code, rather than solid bone.
- Forces:
  - Downward Gravity: Heavy, dark purple or indigo arrows pressing down on the spine, illustrating the passive pull of gravity.
  - Upward Countercurvature: Radiant, golden light and upward-pointing vectors emanating from the spine, actively pushing back against the spacetime grid and the gravity arrows.

Mood and Style: High-end 3D scientific rendering, dramatic lighting, high contrast between the glowing golden information and the dark gravitational well. Suitable for a Nature journal cover or a keynote presentation.
