Title: The Spinal Jetlag Mechanism: Symmetry Breaking via Circadian Desynchronization

Concept: A schematic diagram illustrating how a phase shift ("jetlag") between the circadian clocks of the left and right vertebral growth plates converts planar sagittal buckling into 3D helical scoliosis.

Visual Elements:
1.  **Left Panel: Synchronized State (Healthy)**
    -   Two parallel vertical bars representing the left and right sides of the spine.
    -   Inside each bar, a sine wave represents the oscillating growth rate (clock gene expression).
    -   The sine waves are perfectly in phase ($\Delta \phi = 0$).
    -   Result: The spine grows straight upward.
    -   Label: "Synchronized Growth ($\Delta \phi = 0$)" and "Planar Symmetry".

2.  **Right Panel: Desynchronized State (Scoliotic)**
    -   The same two vertical bars.
    -   The sine waves are now shifted relative to each other ($\Delta \phi \approx \pi/2$ or 90 degrees).
    -   Visual effect: One side is "growing" (peak) while the other is "resting" (trough).
    -   Result: The spine twists into a helical spiral (corkscrew shape).
    -   Label: "Spinal Jetlag ($\Delta \phi \neq 0$)" and "Twist-Bend Coupling".

3.  **Mechanism Arrow:**
    -   An arrow connecting the phase shift to the twist, labeled "Twist-Bend Coupling Operator $\chi_{TB}$".

Style:
-   Clean, modern scientific diagram.
-   Colors: Use Blue for "Synchronized/Healthy" and Red/Orange for "Desynchronized/Scoliotic".
-   Background: White.
-   Annotations: Clear, sans-serif font.
