Biological Countercurvature is a theoretical framework that unifies developmental biology and general relativity. It proposes that living systems maintain their shape against gravity by actively modifying the geometry of the space they inhabit. This is analogous to how mass curves spacetime in general relativity. In this framework, genetic information acts as a 'source term' for curvature, effectively warping the biological spacetime metric. This allows organisms to grow straight or curved structures without constantly fighting gravity, but rather by following the 'geodesics' of their own information-generated geometry.
