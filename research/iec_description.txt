The Information-Elasticity Coupling (IEC) framework models biological development as a coupling between a developmental information field and the mechanical properties of a growing structure. 

Key components:
1.  **Information Field (I(s))**: A scalar or vector field representing morphogen gradients (e.g., HOX genes) along the body axis 's'.
2.  **Cosserat Rod**: The biological structure (e.g., spine) is modeled as a Cosserat rod with centerline position r(s) and orientation frame d_i(s).
3.  **Coupling Parameters (χ)**:
    -   **χ_κ (Chi-Kappa)**: Couples information to preferred curvature (growth direction).
    -   **χ_τ (Chi-Tau)**: Couples information to preferred torsion (twist).
    -   **χ_E (Chi-E)**: Modulates stiffness based on information (material properties).
4.  **Energy Minimization**: The final shape emerges from minimizing the total elastic energy, which includes bending, twisting, and stretching terms modified by the information field.

In the spinal context, this framework explains how subtle genetic information (I(s)) can drive the formation of complex 3D curves (e.g., the S-curve) against gravity by locally altering the "metric" or rest shape of the tissue. High gain on this coupling can lead to instabilities like Scoliosis.
