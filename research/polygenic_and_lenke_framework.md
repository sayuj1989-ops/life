# Polygenic Stacking and Lenke Curve Framework

## 1. The Allometric Trap & Polygenic Stacking (The 2-4% Prevalence)

The Biological Countercurvature framework proposes that the "Allometric Trap" is a universal constraint: every human adolescent enters a phase of energetic deficit (metabolic supply scaling at $L^2$ while mechanical demand scales at $L^4$) during peak height velocity. However, AIS prevalence is only 2-4%. Why?

The baseline stability margin for a generic adolescent is **+5.3 ms**, indicating they remain just above the critical Hopf bifurcation threshold. To cross the threshold into instability, the individual requires a combination of genetic or mechanical variants that exhaust this margin.

Our polygenic threshold model identifies how specific molecular variants stack to shift this margin:
- **Reduced damping $b$** (e.g., COL1A1/COL2A1 flexibility variants) causes up to a 29% reduction in structural energy dissipation.
- **Increased proprioceptive delay $\tau$** (e.g., PIEZO2/GPR126/MTNR1B variants) effectively increases baseline neural delay from 70 ms to $\sim 96.4$ ms.
- **Shifted structural demand $K_d$** (e.g., LBX1 variants) pushes the derivative gain to 12.96, close to the 12.6 critical limit.
- **Increased gravitational load $mgL$** (e.g., PAX1 variants) increases the effective gravitational parameter from 73.6 to 93.7.

Individually, these variants narrow the $+5.3$ ms margin. But when they co-occur, they flip the margin to negative (e.g., **-26.3 ms**), pushing the system into the unstable "Metabolic Buckling" regime. The 2-4% clinical prevalence represents the segment of the population whose individual-specific genetic combinations conspire to breach this threshold.

## 2. Lenke Curve Diversity (Multi-segment Cosserat Rod)

While the single-link inverted pendulum model captures the *onset* of global instability (when the Hopf bifurcation occurs), it does not predict *where* the spine buckles or the resulting shape. To explain the six distinct Lenke curve types, the framework is extended to a **multi-segment Cosserat rod** model.

This extension incorporates regional parameter variations:
- **Regional variation in stiffness $EI(s)$:** The rib cage buttresses the thoracic spine, increasing local stiffness, while lumbar lordosis has distinct flexural properties.
- **Segmental differences in proprioceptive delay $\tau(s)$:** Mechanoreceptor density varies between thoracic (T5-T12) and lumbar (L1-L5) segments.
- **Local damping $b(s)$ variations:** Disc composition, facet joint orientation, and paraspinal muscle bulk differ by vertebral level.
- **Asymmetric loading:** The aortic pulsation and cardiac offset create a small but systematic rightward bias in the thoracic spine.

When the global stability margin is breached, the specific Lenke curve type emerges as the dominant eigenmode of this regionalized system. The localized "Energy Deficit Window" determines which segments destabilize first:
- **Lenke Type 1 (Main Thoracic):** Peaks where the thoracic spine has minimal paraspinal muscle mass and maximal moment arm.
- **Lenke Type 2 (Double Thoracic):** Arises from two simultaneous collapse points at HOX transition zones.
- **Lenke Types 3 & 4 (Double/Triple Major):** Cascading bifurcations where compensatory curves cross $L_{crit}$.
- **Lenke Type 5 (Thoracolumbar/Lumbar):** Emerges later in the growth spurt in regions with greater torsional resistance.
- **Lenke Type 6 (TL/L dominant with minor thoracic):** Localized deficit at the thoracolumbar junction.
