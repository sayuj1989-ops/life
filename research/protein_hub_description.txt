Protein Energy Balance Network: Growth Demand vs. Structural Support in the Spine

This diagram categorizes key proteins involved in spinal stability into "Demand" (Energy Consumers/Growth Drivers) and "Supply/Support" (Energy Providers/Structure).

**Category 1: Growth Demand (The Accelerators)**
*   These proteins drive tissue expansion and increase the metabolic cost of maintaining geometry.
*   **GHR (Growth Hormone Receptor)**: Systemic growth regulator.
*   **IGF1R**: Local growth signaling.
*   **MTOR**: Metabolic growth master switch.
*   **MYC**: Proliferation driver.

**Category 2: Energy Supply (The Power Plant)**
*   These proteins provide the molecular fuel (ATP) and metabolic regulation to sustain the structure.
*   **PPARGC1A**: Mitochondrial biogenesis (ATP supply).
*   **ARNTL/CLOCK**: Circadian regulation of metabolism.
*   **GAPDH**: Glycolysis (energy in hypoxic disc).
*   **HIF1A**: Hypoxic response regulator.

**Category 3: Structural Support & Control (The Cost Centers)**
*   These proteins consume energy to maintain alignment and structural integrity.
*   **Mechanosensors**: **PIEZO1**, **LBX1** (Proprioception).
*   **Active Tension**: **MYH9** (Myosin), **ACTB** (Actin).
*   **ECM Scaffolding**: **FBN1** (Fibrillin), **COL2A1** (Collagen II), **ACAN** (Aggrecan).

**Relationships**:
*   **Growth Demand** scales up the **Structural Support** requirements (more tissue to hold up).
*   **Energy Supply** must meet the needs of **Structural Support**.
*   **Imbalance**: If **Growth Demand** > **Energy Supply**, the **Structural Support** fails (Scoliosis).

**Visual Style**:
*   Network graph or Balance Scale metaphor.
*   Group nodes by category with distinct colors (e.g., Red for Demand, Green for Supply, Blue for Structure).
*   Edges showing dependencies (e.g., Structure *depends on* Supply; Demand *strains* Supply).
