We are submitting our manuscript, "The Gravity Paradox: Metabolic Buckling and the Origin of Human Spinal Deformity," for consideration as an Article in *Nature*.

**The Grand Challenge:**
For over a century, the etiology of Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) has remained one of the most perplexing mysteries in musculoskeletal biology. Despite affecting 3% of children worldwide and necessitating invasive surgeries, it has been dismissed as a "multi-factorial" or "idiopathic" condition. We argue that this is a category error. Scoliosis is not a disease of specific genes, but a fundamental thermodynamic instability inherent to rapid longitudinal growth in a gravitational field.

**The Breakthrough:**
In this work, we present the "Biological Countercurvature" framework, which unifies developmental biology (HOX patterning), continuum mechanics (Cosserat rod theory), and statistical physics (Information Theory). We identify a "Gravity Paradox": as the human spine elongates during puberty, the mechanical torque required to maintain upright posture against gravity scales as the fourth power of length ($L^4$), while the metabolic supply to the avascular intervertebral discs scales only as surface area ($L^2$). This mismatch creates a transient "Energy Deficit Window" where the spine becomes metabolically insolvent.

Using AlphaFold-derived structural metrics, we show that the protein machinery required to sense and counteract gravity (e.g., PIEZO2, Lamin A/C) is highly anisotropic and metabolically expensive to maintain. In the deficit window, these sensors fail, leading to "Spinal Jetlag"—a loss of proprioceptive coherence that triggers a buckling instability. This is the first mechanistic model to explain *why* scoliosis strikes during the adolescent growth spurt and *why* it is specific to humans (the only true bipeds).

**Broad Implications:**
This work transcends orthopedics. It establishes "Metabolic Buckling" as a general principle of vertebrate morphogenesis. It offers a new lens for understanding:
1.  **Evolutionary Medicine:** Why bipedalism places humans on a "thermodynamic cliff."
2.  **Space Biology:** Why microgravity induces "Convective Shutdown" and spinal elongation.
3.  **Mechanobiology:** How physical forces are transduced into information-theoretic error signals.

We believe this manuscript will be of broad interest to physicists, biologists, and clinicians alike, fitting *Nature*'s mission to publish transformative scientific advances.
