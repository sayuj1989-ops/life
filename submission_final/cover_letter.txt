We are submitting our manuscript, "Metabolic Buckling: An Energy Deficit Window Explains Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis," for consideration as an Article in Nature.

Living systems must continuously invest energy to maintain their form against the relentless pull of gravity. While this cost is negligible for small organisms or those with passive stability, we show that human adolescents face a unique and precarious "Allometric Trap." Our work identifies a fundamental scaling law—a "Scaling Catch-22"—where the thermodynamic cost of maintaining spinal straightness ($L^4$) outpaces the growth of metabolic supply ($L^2$). This divergence creates a predictable "Energy Deficit Window" during the adolescent growth spurt, leading to a catastrophic buckling event we recognize as scoliosis.

This manuscript represents a paradigm shift from the prevailing view of scoliosis as a bone disease or a genetic accident. By integrating Cosserat rod mechanics, AlphaFold structural thermodynamics, and cross-species allometry, we demonstrate that the deformity is, in fact, a "Metabolic Buckling" strategy—a mechanism of survival that sheds the "Anisotropy Tax" of upright posture when energy reserves are depleted.

This work is not a specialist clinical report but a description of a general biological principle governing the limits of vertebrate growth. It bridges the gap between soft matter physics and developmental biology, offering a unifying theory for why only humans—among all vertebrates—suffer from this condition.
