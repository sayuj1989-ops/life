\noindent I am submitting for consideration the manuscript \textbf{``Metabolic Buckling of the Growing Spine''} for publication in \textit{Spine Deformity}.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What the manuscript addresses.} Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS) affects approximately 3\% of adolescents worldwide, yet after five decades of research no single unifying causal mechanism has been established. Current models explain correlates of AIS (genetics, growth velocity, proprioceptive deficits) but do not quantitatively predict \emph{why} deformity initiates in a narrow developmental window and why it preferentially affects the thoracic spine at T8--T10.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The central hypothesis.} This manuscript introduces a thermodynamic scaling framework connecting developmental information fields to spine biomechanics. We derive the \textbf{Bio-Gravitational Number} $\mathcal{B}_g = EI/(MgL^2)$, a dimensionless parameter governing anti-gravity shape maintenance. Cross-species analysis reveals humans occupy an ``Allometric Trap'' ($\mathcal{B}_g \approx 0.01$) where passive structural resistance fails and the normal spinal S-curve requires continuous metabolic maintenance. During adolescent growth, an \textbf{Energy Deficit Window} emerges: the cost of maintaining spinal geometry scales as $L^4$ while nutrient supply to avascular discs scales as $L^2$. When this deficit exceeds a critical threshold (spine length $L > 0.35$\,m, ages 11--15), the high-energy S-curve becomes thermodynamically unsustainable and collapses into lower-energy scoliotic modes. AlphaFold structural analysis identifies a 72\% mechanosensor--metabolic regulator anisotropy gap ($p = 0.011$) as the molecular substrate of this transition.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Key quantitative results directly relevant to the readership of \textit{Spine Deformity}.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The critical spinal length $L_{\mathrm{crit}} \approx 0.35$\,m, at which metabolic demand exceeds supply capacity, maps onto the 11--15 year age window of peak AIS incidence without parameter fitting to clinical data.
  \item Curvature localization emerges naturally at thoracic position $s/L \approx 0.55$--$0.65$ (T8--T10 levels) where the information-gradient is steepest, consistent with right thoracic AIS being the most common presentation.
  \item Cross-species Bio-Gravitational Number analysis (n=18 vertebrates) confirms humans uniquely occupy the ``Allometric Trap'' ($\mathcal{B}_g \approx 0.01$), explaining why AIS is predominantly a human condition.
  \item AlphaFold protein structural anisotropy analysis identifies mechanosensor proteins (PIEZO1/2, Vimentin) as metabolic intervention targets, with testable NAD$^+$ precursor supplementation predictions during Peak Height Velocity.
  \item The framework addresses three unexplained clinical patterns: (1) why AIS initiates at ages 11--15, (2) why thoracic spine is most vulnerable, (3) why 8:1 female predominance (females enter Allometric Trap earlier due to smaller vertebral body size).
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Clinical implications.} The framework generates specific, testable predictions including: (1) longitudinal proprioceptive latency measurements as a pre-deformity biomarker; (2) NAD$^+$ precursor supplementation (e.g., Nicotinamide Riboside) during Peak Height Velocity as a pharmacological curve-modification strategy; and (3) the identification of high-$K_D$ neuromotor phenotypes as a risk-stratification tool.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Originality and fit.} This is the first report to derive the AIS initiation window from first-principles stability analysis of a delayed control system, validated against independent clinical datasets (Cheng 2015, Sanders 2017, Weinstein 2008/2013, BrAIST). The manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere. All simulation code is openly available at \url{https://github.com/sayujks0071/life}.
